# Cover Brief: The Saeren Chronicles — Book One: Hazel Academy

For a cover designer / illustrator. This is art direction, not a plot summary. The single
job of this cover is to **promise the right book**: quiet, interior, literary-leaning,
grief-driven — not loud commercial spectacle. A cover that over-promises action will draw the
wrong reader and disappoint them on page one.

---

## Emotional concept

The feeling of holding something still while a great deal moves underneath — *the stillness
of deep water*. Composed surface, enormous undertow. Loneliness on the edge of being seen.
Not danger-as-thrill; danger-as-quiet-dread, with one small warm thing held against the cold.

---

## Color palette

- **Primary:** deep dark blue / near-black (the mother's focus-stone; deep water) — `#16233A`
- **Secondary:** cold stone grey, "a sky that has not decided whether to rain" — `#9AA3AB`
- **Accent 1 (warmth):** a single muted amber/lamplight glow — `#C8923D`
- **Accent 2 (signature):** one note of **rose** (Lightwell's rose-tinted lenses; restrained,
  not pink-cute) — `#B36A78`
- **Mood:** cool-dominant, with one deliberate warm/rose intrusion. The whole point is the
  *one wrong, chosen detail* in an otherwise composed field — mirror the book's signature
  ("blue shoes under all that careful autumn").

---

## Visual elements

**Must evoke (suggestion over depiction):**
- A single small figure made small *on purpose* by the frame — back turned, or partial, never
  posing or weapon-forward. Slight, silver-blonde if shown at all.
- One held object glowing faintly: a dark-blue carved stone at the throat. This is the book's
  truest image.
- Texture of grey institutional stone, tall cold windows, iron gate — implied, atmospheric.
- A faint suggestion of two magics — light and dark — not at war on the cover, but *braided* or
  meeting along a seam. The book's whole idea is wholeness, not battle.

**Must avoid:**
- A girl in a dramatic stance holding a weapon / fireball / glowing hands toward camera.
- A dragon rendered as spectacle / the dragon dominating the cover. (There is a dragon; it is
  not what the book is about. A small, distant, or implied creature is fine; a roaring
  cover-dragon is a false promise.)
- Romantasy signaling (two figures, embrace, swords-and-roses maximalism, gold-foil overload).
- Generic chosen-one glow-up imagery (girl crowned in light, "the special one" radiance).

---

## Typography

- **Title treatment:** elegant serif, restrained, slightly literary; high contrast, not
  fantasy-blackletter, not bold-commercial slab. Think upmarket trade hardcover, not mass-market.
- **Author name:** subtle for a debut; prominent only if the author has platform.
- **Genre signal:** the typography should read "upmarket YA / crossover literary fantasy" — the
  shelf where Allison Saft and Margaret Rogerson live — not epic-fantasy gilt or romantasy foil.

---

## Three concept directions

**A — The Stone (recommended, truest to the book).**
Near-black field. A single dark-blue carved stone, faintly lit from within (amber + a thread of
rose), hung on a fine chain that disappears off-frame as if at a throat we cannot quite see.
Cold and intimate. Title in restrained serif. Minimal, literary, gift-able.

**B — The Small Figure at the Gate.**
A slight figure seen from behind, made small against the grey stone bulk of Hazel and its iron
gate, one hand at her throat. Overcast palette, one window lit warm amber high in the building.
Atmosphere of arrival and dread. Signals "school story with an undertow."

**C — The Braided Seam.**
Abstract/symbolic: a vertical seam down the cover where light and dark meet — not clashing, but
woven — with the focus-stone or a single small figure at the meeting point. The most "concept"
direction; best if you want to foreground the severed-magic theme over character.

---

## Reference *feel* (not content)

- The upmarket-literary restraint and single-symbol focus of recent Allison Saft / Margaret
  Rogerson trade editions.
- Frances Hardinge UK hardcover design language: atmospheric, illustrative-but-restrained,
  unmistakably *literary* YA.
- Take from these: **mood, restraint, single strong symbol, serif elegance.** Do not copy
  layout or imagery.

---

## Avoidances (summary for quick reference)

- No weapon-forward heroine pose.
- No spectacle-dragon.
- No romantasy two-figure/foil maximalism.
- No chosen-one radiance.
- No mass-market or blackletter fantasy type.
- Keep it cool, quiet, and let the one warm/rose accent do all the talking.
