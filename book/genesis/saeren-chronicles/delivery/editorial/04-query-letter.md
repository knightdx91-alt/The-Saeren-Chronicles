The last ordinary morning of Viridia Saeren's life smelled like scorched bread and her
mother's tea. Three days later she walks through the gates of Hazel — the most prestigious
school for light mages in the country — orphaned, silent, and certain of one thing: no one
is ever going to see her grieve.

Viridia is good at not being seen. She has always been good at it, and she is about to learn
why it has kept her alive. In a world that long ago tore its magic cleanly in two — light
from dark, permitted from forbidden — Viridia was born whole. Both halves. The one thing
Hazel was secretly built to make sure cannot exist. To be known is to be killed by decree,
so she makes herself small: she lets a sharp-tongued older girl sponsor her, she learns in
secret what she truly is, and she befriends Alice Pembrook, the only person at Hazel who
looks past the careful quiet and sees the drowning underneath. But a school that suppresses
what it fears has enemies who would rather see it burn — and on one terrible night, a girl
who can survive anything by disappearing must choose between the many she can save and the
one she cannot bear to lose.

THE SAEREN CHRONICLES — BOOK ONE: THE HAZEL YEARS is a YA fantasy complete at 94,000 words.
It is interior, slow-burn, and grief-driven — a school story on the surface and, underneath,
a story about identity and what it costs to refuse to be made small. It is the first book of
a planned trilogy but resolves a complete emotional arc and can stand on its own. It will
appeal to readers of Allison Saft's *A Far Wilder Magic* and Margaret Rogerson's *Sorcery of
Thorns* — that upmarket, character-first end of YA fantasy where the prose is lyrical, the
magic is intimate, and the heart of the book is a girl learning who she is allowed to be.

## Notes for the author (delete before sending)

- **Keep it one page.** As written (minus these notes) it is one page. If your bio runs long,
  trim the hook paragraph, not the comps.
- **The comp line is doing positioning work.** It signals "upmarket / character-first," not
  "next big commercial blockbuster." That is deliberate — see `05-comp-titles.md`. Swap in a
  different comp only if you can defend it as recent, same-lane, and not an aspirational megahit.
- **Lead with the agent's wishlist if you can.** A specific MSWL match in the first line is the
  single highest-yield personalization for this book, because its appeal is a *taste*, not a hook.
- **Be ready to speak to the deferred payoffs** (Alice's fate, the family-tree name, the war) as
  intentional Book-Two structure if asked. They are not gaps; they are the shape of a trilogy opener.
