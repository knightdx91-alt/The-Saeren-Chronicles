# Short Synopsis (jacket copy / blurb)

*The Saeren Chronicles — Book One: The Hazel Years*
~250 words · spoiler-light · for query bodies, catalog copy, and the back cover

---

The last ordinary morning of Viridia Saeren's life smelled like scorched bread and
her mother's tea. Three days later she walks through the gates of Hazel — the most
prestigious school for light mages in the country — orphaned, silent, and carrying
a grief she has decided no one will ever see her hold.

She is good at not being seen. She has always been good at it. What she does not yet
understand is *why* it has kept her alive. In a world that long ago tore its magic
cleanly in two — light from dark, safe from forbidden — Viridia was born whole. Both
halves. The one thing Hazel exists to make sure cannot happen.

So she makes herself small. She lets a sharp-tongued older girl sponsor her. She
befriends Alice, the only person at Hazel who looks past the careful quiet and sees
the drowning underneath. She learns, in secret, what she is — from a three-hundred-
year-old tiger, from an ancient language no one is supposed to be able to speak,
from a headmistress in rose-tinted spectacles who watches Viridia far too closely.

But a school that suppresses what it fears has enemies who would rather see it burn,
and a girl who can survive anything by disappearing will, one terrible night, have
to choose between the many she can save and the one she cannot bear to lose.

For readers who love grief held with control, found family that costs something, and
a heroine whose quiet is her armor — until she decides it isn't.
