# Cover Brief — The Saeren Chronicles, Book One: The Hazel Years

> For the cover designer. Visual language, not narrative. Do not generate images from this; it is direction.

## Emotional Concept

The single feeling the cover must evoke: **a held breath in a beautiful, dangerous place.** Quiet on the surface, enormous underneath — exactly like the protagonist. Grief and wonder in the same frame. The viewer should sense containment under pressure: something whole and luminous being kept very, very still inside a world built to suppress it. Not a battle cover. A *secret* cover.

## Color Palette

- **Primary — Dark slate / storm grey-blue** (#2B3440 to #3C4A5A). The grey stone of Hazel, the storm in Lor-ar's eyes, the cold morning light. The dominant field.
- **Secondary — Deep midnight + clean white, paired** (#0B0E14 / #F4F1EA). The two halves of the gift: light and dark held together, never one swallowing the other. This pairing is the book's whole thesis and should appear somewhere on the cover, ideally in the central magical element.
- **Accent — Dark-blue stone glow** (#1E4FA3 with a faint inner luminance) and a **single thread of prismatic shimmer** (subtle red/gold/green/blue iridescence — Raizen) used sparingly.
- **Warmth accent — rose** (#C46A78), used only as a small, deliberate note (see "rose-tinted" motif), against all the cool. The one warm thing in a cold frame.
- **Mood:** cool overall, with two or three intentional warm/luminous breaks. High contrast between the cold field and the glowing focal point.

## Visual Elements

**Must include (in priority order — the designer should choose a strong focal hierarchy, not cram all of these):**
1. **The dark-blue stone** — a carved, faintly glowing pendant with old script along its sides. The strongest single emblem of the book; an excellent central object for a symbol-forward cover.
2. **Light-and-dark held together** — a small orb, flame, or core showing white and midnight black braided in one space, neither overtaking the other. The thematic heart.
3. **Hazel's grey stone facade and iron gate** — tall windows that give nothing back, hedges trimmed unnaturally flat, an iron gate. Ideal as a cold architectural backdrop or a silhouette through fog.
4. **Lor-ar, the sylvan tiger** — horned (bone-white horns with fine blue veining), fur in shifting sky-blue and forest-green, storm-lit eyes. Can anchor a creature-forward cover or appear in silhouette/at a distance.
5. **Raizen, the prismatic dragon** — small, newborn-to-young, scales throwing back red/gold/green/blue at once. Best used as the iridescent accent rather than a roaring centerpiece (this is not an action cover).
6. **Rose-tinted motif** — a whisper of rose light/tint at one edge or across the lenses-of-the-world; ties to Lady Lightwell's rose-tinted spectacles and the warmth-in-coldness theme. Subtle.

**Must avoid:**
- A girl in a ballgown / romance-novel posing. Viridia is slight, watchful, composed; if a figure appears, she is still and contained, often seen from behind or in partial silhouette — never glamorized.
- A roaring-dragon, swords-crossed, high-action montage. This book's power is restraint; the cover must promise interiority and stakes, not spectacle.
- Generic "chosen one stands on a cliff" silhouette clichés.
- Neon/saturated "gamer fantasy" palettes. Keep it literary-cool and grounded; the world has trains and motorcars (early-20th-c. texture), not high-medieval.
- Bright cheerful school imagery. Hazel is beautiful and ominous, never cozy.

## Typography

- **Title treatment:** elegant serif with a cold edge — high contrast strokes, slightly condensed; refined but with weight. THE HAZEL YEARS large; THE SAEREN CHRONICLES set smaller as the series line above or below. Consider a faint dark-to-light gradient on the title lettering to echo the two-halves motif.
- **Author name:** moderate prominence — debut-appropriate, clean, secondary to the title.
- **Genre signals:** serif + cool jewel-toned palette + a single luminous magical object signals literary/upmarket YA fantasy (Bardugo/Nix shelf), distinct from breezy contemporary-YA sans-serif treatments.
- Series-positioning line: small caps, "BOOK ONE OF THE SAEREN CHRONICLES."

## Visual References (for FEEL, not content)

- **Leigh Bardugo, *Six of Crows* / Grisha hardcovers** — symbol-forward, cold jewel palette, restraint, a single iconic emblem doing the work.
- **Garth Nix, *Sabriel* / Old Kingdom reissues** — atmospheric, dark, dangerous-beautiful; magic treated as solemn and consequential.
- **Naomi Novik, *Uprooted* / *Spinning Silver*** — that wintry, literary-fantasy stillness; a world that is gorgeous and quietly threatening.
- Take from these the **iconography-over-illustration** approach and the **cold, contained mood** — not their specific objects.

## Avoidances (summary)

- No spoilers in imagery (no burning school, no battle, no Alice — the loss is the gut-punch of the read, not the sell).
- No misleading romance framing.
- No clutter: one luminous focal idea (the stone, or the two-halves core) beats five competing elements.
- Keep the warmth deliberate and rare; the whole cover should feel like the held breath before the world moves.
