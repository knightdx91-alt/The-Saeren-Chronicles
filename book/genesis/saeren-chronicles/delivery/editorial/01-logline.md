# Logline & Elevator Pitch

*The Saeren Chronicles — Book One: Hazel Academy*
YA fantasy · ~94,000 words · Book One of a planned trilogy (standalone-with-series-potential)

---

## Logline (one sentence)

When a newly orphaned girl arrives at an elite school built to suppress the very
magic she was secretly born whole with, she must stay small enough to survive a
world that kills what it cannot categorize — until the day staying hidden costs
the one friend who ever saw her.

---

## Elevator pitch (2–3 sentences)

Fourteen-year-old Viridia Saeren is the only person alive born with both halves of
a magic her world deliberately tore in two, and she has just learned that the
prestigious school meant to nurture her gift was built to bury it. *The Hazel
Years* is a quiet, interior, grief-driven fantasy about a girl who only wants to
disappear — and the slow, costly discovery that being seen by the right people is
worth more than being safe from everyone. It is a school story on the surface and,
underneath, a story about grief, identity, and what it takes to refuse to be made
small.

---

## Why the pitch leads with the quiet

This book's strength is not spectacle; it is a controlled, observant voice and an
embodied grief that most fantasy at this age range does not attempt. The pitch is
built to attract readers and gatekeepers who *want* that — and to honestly warn off
those who want a loud, hook-first commercial blockbuster, who would be the wrong
champions for it.
