# Full Synopsis (complete — contains the ending)

*The Saeren Chronicles — Book One: Hazel Academy*
~1.5–2 pages · full spoilers, including the Chapter 16 climax and the Book-Two hook
For agents and editors who request a full synopsis. Do not use as marketing copy.

---

**VIRIDIA SAEREN**, fourteen, arrives at **Hazel Preparatory School** three days after
her parents die suddenly — a loss she refuses to grieve where anyone can witness it.
Slight, silver-blonde, watchful, she has spent her whole life being good at one thing:
not being seen. Her dying mother's last ordinary advice — *be careful who you let see
you do it* — has hardened, in grief, into a fortress. At Hazel, the country's elite
school for *light* mages, she means to disappear into the rankings and survive.

She cannot. From the first day, things see her. Her mother's blue focus-stone warms
and flares when the headmistress, **Lady Lightwell** (rose-tinted spectacles, a
general's stillness), sweeps her gaze across the new arrivals. A fourth-year named
**Amber Summers** — chipped black nails, armored wit — clocks the stone at Viridia's
throat and sponsors her, which is not the kindness it looks like. And **Alice
Pembrook**, a warm, first-generation mage who does not belong and knows it, sits down
beside the drowning stranger and simply refuses to leave. Alice becomes the first
person to see Viridia clearly and stay.

**Inciting truth:** Viridia is not a light mage. She is the only person alive born
with *both* halves of a magic her world deliberately severed generations ago — light
and dark, whole. Hazel was built, beneath its prestige, to detect and suppress
exactly this. To be known is to be killed by decree.

Across the school year, in secret and at growing cost, Viridia learns what she is.
A three-hundred-year-old shapeshifting tiger, **Lor-ar**, attaches himself to her and
teaches her the forbidden half — rune-craft, the old discipline that *the object
remembers*. She bonds, against a sponsor's explicit warning and at near-fatal cost, a
prismatic dragon, **Raizen**, summoning him by over-pouring a pact-call she was told
to keep small — and in doing so announces herself, once, to a hungry thing in the
dark. She is taken in by the **Dangris**, warrior-teachers who name her and arm her.
She learns the ancient **Moravian** language no light school admits is still alive.
Each gift is also a tether, and each pulls her further from the safety of being no
one. The grief never lifts; it only learns to live alongside a self she is becoming.
Lady Lightwell watches all of it, withholding Viridia's name from every list she
keeps — protecting a secret she will not explain.

**Climax (Chapter 16):** The capital's army comes for Lightwell — who is herself a
hidden dark mage and the quiet heart of a resistance — and burns Hazel to clear the
"infestation." The night before, Viridia reads the signs (a soldier in a laborer's
coat, short kitchens, a wrong wagon) and *acts*: she warns Lightwell, offers to pull
Alice from the medical ward. Lightwell overrules her — *moving people is how people
are found* — and orders her to stay hidden. Viridia obeys. It is a choice she will
never stop owning. When the attack comes, she fights her way out: kills a captain by
inscribing an explosion rune onto his own protective shield (a botched stroke splits
her own forearm — she will carry the scar, as Lor-ar carries his); and in the burning
main hall, where Lightwell duels the **High Chancellor** to a standstill she is slowly
losing, Viridia makes her one decisive, hidden intervention — a rune under the
Chancellor's heel that buys the seconds a rescue needs. She does not save Lightwell
(she cannot; the Chancellor came for her and never even sees Viridia). She does not go
back through three burning corridors for Alice. She spends her single decisive act on
the **eight** children she *can* save, portals them out, and turns away from the one
she loves most. Lightwell dies; her last words name Viridia chosen and send her to
find **Jazen**, Lightwell's son.

**Resolution (Chapters 17–18):** Viridia leads the survivors to a hidden dark-mage
camp where children are taught the old language over breakfast — a whole world the
capital spent six centuries pretending it had destroyed. Jazen, grieving his mother,
is done hiding; war is a week away. Viridia, who arrived wanting only to disappear,
understands that the small girl she was burned up in the corridors of Hazel — and that
she does not mourn her, because the small girl could not have carried any of this. She
asks Jazen for one thing: word of Alice, alive or dead. The not-knowing is the wound
the book ends on. Her mother's line turns over in her at last — not only *hide*, but
*you decide who sees*. She faces forward.

**Book-Two hook:** Alice's fate is deliberately unresolved — and is now partly
Viridia's own choice, which makes it an open wound rather than a loose thread. A frayed
thread in the world's oldest magic carries her mother's name and may, someday, mend the
severing itself. The hungry thing in the dark heard her, once, and does not forget.

**This is, honestly, a Book One: a complete emotional arc (grief to purpose) whose
larger mysteries are paid forward by design.** That is its risk and its shape.
