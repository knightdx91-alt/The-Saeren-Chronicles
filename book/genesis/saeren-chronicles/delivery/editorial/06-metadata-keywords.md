## Amazon description (conversion version)

> **The last ordinary morning of Viridia's life smelled like scorched bread and her mother's tea.**
>
> Three days later, she walks through the gates of the country's most elite school for mages —
> orphaned, silent, and certain of one thing: no one is ever going to see her grieve.
>
> Viridia is good at not being seen. She's about to learn why it's kept her alive.
>
> In a world that tore its magic cleanly in two, Viridia was born whole — both halves — the
> one thing this school was secretly built to make sure could never exist. To be known is to be
> killed. So she makes herself small. She learns, in secret, what she truly is. And she lets one
> person in: Alice, the only girl at Hazel who sees the drowning underneath the quiet.
>
> Then the people who fear what this school hides decide to burn it down — and a girl who
> survives by disappearing must choose between the many she can save and the one she can't bear
> to lose.
>
> A quiet, lyrical, slow-burn fantasy about grief, found family, and a girl who refuses to be
> made small. For readers of Allison Saft and Margaret Rogerson.
>
> *If you've ever held a grief no one else could see, this is a book about the people worth
> letting in — and what it costs to choose them.*

*(First ~150 characters land the hook before "Read more": the scorched-bread line plus the
orphaned/silent/no-one-sees-her-grieve promise.)*
