# Back-Cover Copy — The Saeren Chronicles, Book One: The Hazel Years

> No spoilers. Hooks and entices; does not resolve.

## Primary Back-Cover Copy

*She has learned to grieve where no one can see. She has not learned that the safest place in the world can be the one most likely to kill her.*

Three days after she loses everything, Viridia Saeren is sent to Hazel — the country's finest school for light mages, all iron gates and grey stone and quiet, careful rank. She means only to disappear into it: to be small, to be unseen, to carry her grief folded down where no one will find it.

But Viridia is carrying something else, too. An ancient focus that has been thirsty for a thousand years. A heritage written into a family tree she was never meant to read. And a secret that the whole world kills for — because Viridia was born with both halves of a magic that was severed in two, on purpose, by people who feared what it could be.

At Hazel she finds a tiger out of legend, a dragon bound to her for life, a dead language that bends magic to a single true word, a friend who sees straight through her, and a headmistress whose kindness is a kind of warning. She finds, against everything, a reason to stay.

And then the most powerful man in the country comes to the school wearing glasses made for seeing exactly what she has hidden — and what he decides will bring a long, quiet war straight through the front door.

*The world told her she was half a thing in a world of half-things. The world was wrong about her. It is about to find out how wrong.*

**For readers of Leigh Bardugo, Tamora Pierce, and Garth Nix — the first book of THE SAEREN CHRONICLES.**

## Amazon Description (conversion version)

**She arrived to disappear. She is about to become the one thing her world cannot allow to exist.**

Three days after both her parents are killed, fourteen-year-old Viridia Saeren is delivered to Hazel — the most prestigious school for light mages in the country. She wants only to be small, quiet, and unseen.

But Viridia is hiding more than her grief. She was born whole, with *both* halves of a magic the world severed in two, long ago, out of fear — and the school built to nurture talent is the very place built to erase what she is.

Inside Hazel's grey walls she finds an extinct tiger who speaks the truth no one else will, a dragon bound to her for life, a dead language that turns magic into a single true word, and a friend who loves her without wanting anything back. She finds, against all odds, a reason to stop hiding.

Then the most powerful man in the country arrives — wearing glasses made to see exactly what she is. And what he decides will bring a long, buried war crashing through the door.

*A YA epic fantasy of grief and belonging, bonded creatures and ancient magic, and a girl who was told she was broken in a world that broke itself.*

**If you've ever felt like the wrong shape for the room you were handed, THE HAZEL YEARS will feel like coming home — and then like fighting for it.** Perfect for fans of the Grisha trilogy, Tortall, and the Old Kingdom. Book One of THE SAEREN CHRONICLES.
