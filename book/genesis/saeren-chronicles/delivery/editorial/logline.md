# Logline — The Saeren Chronicles, Book One: The Hazel Years

## Primary

When a newly orphaned girl is sent to a prestigious school for light mages, she must hide the one thing that would get her killed — that she alone was born whole, with both halves of a deliberately severed magic — while grief, a buried heritage, and a coming war close in on the only place she has left to belong.

## Alternates

**A (stakes-forward).**
A grieving fourteen-year-old arrives at an elite magic school carrying a secret that the whole world kills for: she is the only person alive born with both halves of a magic that was severed in two on purpose — and the school that promises to nurture her is the institution built to erase what she is.

**B (irony engine, punchy).**
The safest place anyone could have handed Viridia Saeren is the most dangerous place she could be: a school for light mages, run on the quiet erasure of the dark — and she is the only person in a thousand years born with both.

**C (character + theme).**
Told all her life that she is half a thing in a world of half-things, an orphaned girl discovers she may be the only whole person she has ever met — and that being whole, in a world that severed itself out of fear, is not a gift but a death sentence she has one school year to learn to survive.
