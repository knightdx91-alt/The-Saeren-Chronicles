# Positioning & Honesty Note

*The Saeren Chronicles — Book One: Hazel Academy*
Read this before you query anyone. Its purpose is to make sure you spend your queries on the
*right* people — so the book's genuine strengths land instead of being judged against
expectations it never set out to meet.

---

## What this book IS

- A **quiet, interior, character-first** YA fantasy with a controlled, observant, slightly
  literary voice.
- A genuinely **grief-driven** story: a just-orphaned girl who has decided no one will see her
  grieve, and the slow, costly discovery that being seen by the right people is worth the risk.
- A **found-family and friendship** book at its heart (no romance, no spice).
- A book with a real **moral/political spine**: a world that severed its own magic out of fear,
  and a protagonist born whole into it — the dual light/dark gift carries the theme, not just
  the plot.
- A **complete emotional arc** (grief → purpose) that stands on its own while opening a trilogy.
- Craft-ready: clean prose, strong emotional anchors, a protagonist whose composure is the
  whole point, validated as publishable via boutique/literary or self-pub by an honest internal
  read (and worth confirming with real external readers).

## What this book is NOT

- **Not a loud, hook-first commercial blockbuster.** Its pitch is a premise and a voice, not a
  one-line scream. It will lose in a frame built for the next megahit.
- **Not romantasy**, not spice, not enemies-to-lovers. Pitching it that way guarantees the wrong
  reader.
- **Not a spectacle-dragon / weapon-forward adventure.** There is a dragon and there is a battle,
  but the engine is interiority and grief.
- **Not a standalone with all its payoffs delivered.** By design, the marquee mysteries (Alice's
  fate, the family-tree name, the war, the severing) are paid forward to Book Two. The antagonist
  gets one strong scene and exits. This is a deliberate Book-One shape — and it is the single
  thing that makes it a harder *first* sale to a buyer who wants everything resolved in one volume.

---

## What this means for who you query

| Path | Honest verdict | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Big-5 / top commercial agency | Hardest fit as-is — wrong shape for that buyer, not a quality issue | Optional; only if you also do the floor-raising polishes (see genesis-floor report §"8.0→8.5") |
| Boutique / literary-leaning agent | **Closest to a clean yes** | Primary target — lead here |
| Small / independent press | **Strong fit** | Primary target — many take un-agented |
| Self-publish | **Ready now**, with the right cover/blurb and (ideally) Book Two coming | Fully viable parallel path |

---

## The two truths to hold together

1. **Lead with the strengths.** The quiet, the embodied grief, the dual-gift moral complexity,
   and the found family are not apologies — they are the *features*. Frame them confidently.
2. **Be honest about the shape.** Tell agents/editors it's an interior, slow-burn trilogy opener
   that resolves a feeling and defers its mysteries. The right gatekeeper hears that as a
   deliberate, confident book. The wrong one hears a mismatch — and you *want* them to pass early
   rather than champion it into the wrong frame.

Query the people who want exactly this book. There are fewer of them than there are agents in the
loud lane — but for them, this is the book they've been asking for.
