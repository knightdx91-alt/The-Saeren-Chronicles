## Question-by-question findings

### (a) Information-flow violations from the new visibility — NONE (verified clean)

The worry was that Viridia's new Ch.3/Ch.4 visibility creates either (i) a forward-reference where someone knows something they shouldn't, or (ii) a contradiction with later beats that depend on her being unnoticed.

- **No forward-reference created.** The people who now *notice* Viridia in Ch.3–4 are exactly the people who were already inside her secret by other means: Mrs. Zoran (who already reads the focus and its "made for someone with both" nature on-page, Ch.3 ¶187–197), Lady Lightwell (who already clocked the stone at orientation, Ch.1 ¶156, and again at the desk, Ch.4 ¶95), and Amber (who clocked the matching necklace "at the door the first morning," Ch.5 ¶16). The conversions make Viridia *choose* to be seen by allies who were already onto her. No new character learns anything ahead of its on-page reveal.
- **The "unnoticed" payoff is explicitly reconciled, not contradicted.** Ch.9 ¶61 has Lightwell directly reference the Ch.4 essay: *"It is the kind of thing that gets a girl noticed, Miss Saeren. Not always by people you would want to be noticed by."* This converts the new visibility into a tracked, intentional plot beat rather than a loose end. Ch.16 ¶81 then closes the loop cleanly: the Chancellor *"had come for Lightwell. He had never seen Viridia at all. She had hidden exactly right... and everyone she could not hide was dying tonight because the woman at the center of it had finally run out of ways to hide herself."* The book consistently distinguishes **noticed-within-the-resistance-circle** (Lightwell/Zoran/Amber, all allies) from **unseen-by-the-capital/Chancellor** (the survival axis). Those are two different audiences and the manuscript never conflates them.

**Verdict:** Consistent. The visibility conversions are absorbed by existing machinery.

### (b) Ch.3 "lose anonymity / be seen choosing" vs the canon that she survives by being HIDDEN — RECONCILED (no contradiction)

- Ch.3 ¶95 is careful and self-aware about exactly this: she trades *"the small safety of going unnoticed for the larger fact of being the one who decided,"* and does it "in a warm crowded room full of growing things" — i.e., in front of classmates and a sympathetic teacher, not in front of the capital. Ch.4 ¶171 reprises the same framing ("the anonymity she had walked in craving... Lady Lightwell remembered her now").
- The survival mechanism in Ch.16 is **invisibility to the capital/Chancellor**, not invisibility to her own school. Those are not in tension. A girl can be the talk of Mrs. Zoran's classroom and Lightwell's office and still be a person the Chancellor "never saw," because the Chancellor's instrument (the glasses of true seeing) was trained on Lightwell, and Viridia spends Ch.11–15 deliberately banking her core to a flicker by daylight and lying to the trap-question (ENTITY_STATE; Ch.14). The "hidden" that keeps her alive is core-concealment from the capital, which the conversions never breach.
- **Canon check:** CLAUDE.md guardrails ("Viridia: grief held inward — NO ONE sees her cry") are not touched by the visibility conversions; see Minor-2 for the only adjacent flag.

**Verdict:** Reconciled on-page. No fix required.

### (d) Ch.16 escape-deciding rune-craft vs fixed causality and Ch.10 seeding — CONSISTENT (verified clean)

- **Causality intact.** The fixed joint — *Chancellor came for Lightwell; the glasses read Lightwell; Viridia unseen* — is stated outright in Ch.16 ¶81 and is not disturbed by the new rune-craft beat. Viridia's decisive act in Ch.16 ¶137–143 is explicitly **from cover** and **small/hidden** ("the small hidden thing instead of the large seen one"; "a single rune... not a weapon, only a hitch"). It buys the eight children's seconds without revealing her: ¶141 dramatizes the near-miss where the Chancellor "almost" feels her reaching and she lets go — and crucially she is *not* seen (*"She had not been seen. She had been a hair from it."*). This *strengthens* rather than violates the unseen-survival logic.
- **Rune-on-object craft is properly seeded.** Ch.10 ¶77–79 establishes the entire mechanic the Ch.16 beats depend on: laying a shape "onto a thing" so it "waits / keeps"; the mantra *"The object remembers"*; practice on a river-stone (warming-shape); and the specific failure mode — an **inverted stroke** on the iron bench-arm producing a "deep, drinking cold" that numbed two fingers. She had laid a hundred of them onto river-stones on quiet nights."). This is reverse-Chekhov-clean: every Ch.16 payoff has a Ch.10 setup.
- **No contradiction with the death-symbol layer.** Ch.16 ¶91 correctly distinguishes the captain-kill ("a simpler, colder thing... not the death-magic Drake had only begun to teach her"), consistent with ENTITY_STATE (Drake "teaches... DEATH SYMBOL (later)") — i.e., the book is careful NOT to claim she used training she hasn't had.

**Verdict:** Consistent and well-seeded. No fix required.

## WARNINGS

### [WARN-1] Ch.5 "hungry thing in the dark" is an orphaned thread in Book One
- **Audit:** 5 (Plot Thread Tracking) / 3 (Information Flow context).
- **Chapter:** 5.
- **Evidence:** Ch.5 ¶109 (the over-pour call "rolling out into parts of the wood that had no names... something large heard it and did not come") and Ch.5 ¶171 (Lor-ar: *"out there something heard it and did not come — which is worse than if it had. You have announced yourself, the once, to a hungry thing in the dark, and a thing like that does not forget the taste of a door thrown open. That is the tail your defiance has on it. You will carry it whether you meant to or not."*).
- **Description:** This is a deliberately emphatic setup (a named consequence — "the tail your defiance has on it") delivered by the most authoritative voice in the book. A targeted search across Ch.6–18 finds **no payoff and no further reference**. The phrases "hungry thing," "announced yourself," "did not forget the taste," and "a thing in the dark that heard her" do not recur.
- **Important nuance / why this is WARNING not CRITICAL:** The "hungry thing" is NOT the same entity as the Ch.10/14 **Hollowing / void-absence** seed. The Hollowing is described as an *emptiness with a moving edge* born of war-drained land (Ch.10 ¶75), advancing "ridge by ridge," and the source "flinches" from a related *absence* at the rim of the lattice (Ch.14). The Ch.5 thing, by contrast, is a discrete *listener* that "heard a door thrown open" and "does not forget the taste." The manuscript never links them. So this reads as a genuine new dangling thread the agency revision introduced, not a covert payoff.
- **Suggested fix (author/editor choice; do not auto-resolve):** Either (i) **plant one downstream callback** — e.g., a single beat in Ch.10–15 where Lor-ar, scenting the dark, references "the thing that heard you that first day" as distinct from the Hollowing, explicitly deferring it to Book Two; or (ii) **fold it into the Hollowing/void seed** by adding one clause that ties the Ch.5 listener to the rim-absence (making it an early, personal brush with the Book-Two void thread); or (iii) **accept it as an intentional Book-Two hook** and log it in ENTITY_STATE `open_mysteries`. As written it is unlogged in either STATE.yaml or ENTITY_STATE.yaml.
- **Cascade risk:** Low. Adding a single callback line in Ch.10–15 must respect the style gate (simile/em-dash ceilings) and must not conflate it with the Hollowing if option (i) is chosen.
