# Grammar / Line-Edit Pass — The Hazel Years (Ch. 1–18)

Copy-editor pass for line-level correctness and prose hygiene. No files were
edited. Story/continuity were out of scope.

## Headline findings

The manuscript is **mechanically very clean**. The automated sweeps found:

- **0** misspellings of canon names (Viridia, Alice Pembrook, Jazen, Lor-ar,
  Raizen, Lady Lightwell, Mrs. Zoran, Amber, Meros — all consistent).
- **0** typo doubled words (the only `had had` hits are correct past-perfect).
- **0** `a/an` errors, **0** "could of/would of," **0** obvious its/it's slips.
- **0** double spaces, **0** spaces before punctuation.
- All chapters have **balanced** quotation marks (even straight-quote counts).
- **0** "and and / the the" run-together typos.

So there is essentially nothing "embarrassing" at the typo level. The real
line-edit issue is a **single recurring stylistic habit**: cumulative
conjunction stacking — sentences built on `X, and Y, and Z (, and W…)`.

This device is part of the **locked Chapter 1 voice** (the "drowning,
and had been for three days, and would be tomorrow" cadence), so it is partly
intentional signature rhythm and should NOT be eradicated wholesale. The note
below is about the **overuse** in later chapters where it has drifted from
deliberate crescendo into default sentence construction.

**Total double-"and" sentences: 358** (349 excluding the locked Chapter 1).
Of those, **103** are triple-or-more "and" within one sentence.

Density rises sharply after Ch. 5 and peaks in **Ch. 14 (35)**, **Ch. 10 (31)**,
**Ch. 17 (27)**, **Ch. 13 (26)** — these are the chapters to thin first.

**Recommendation:** keep the device for genuine emotional crescendos (the
ending of Ch. 6, Ch. 17, Ch. 18 — where the pile-up *is* the point). Convert
the routine ones to a period, a semicolon, or a subordinate clause. Target:
no more than ~1–2 deliberate cumulative sentences per chapter.

### TOP FIXES — worst / most fixable stacks

1. **Ch. 5** — "Amber checked the corridor before she closed the door, **which
   Viridia noticed,** and pulled two chairs down off a table, **which Viridia
   also noticed,** and sat in one and gestured at the other, and for a moment
   said nothing at all, **which Viridia noticed most of everything.**"
   *Problem:* triple "and" + triple "which Viridia noticed" — comic by accident,
   and the final clause ("noticed most of everything") is ungrammatical.
   *Fix:* "Amber checked the corridor before she closed the door. Viridia
   noticed. She pulled two chairs down off a table — Viridia noticed that too —
   sat in one, gestured at the other, and for a moment said nothing at all,
   which Viridia noticed most of all."

2. **Ch. 6** — "She slept the deep blank sleep of the truly spent, **and** the
   school went on without her, **and** the light left the window, **and**
   somewhere a tray of food was brought up and set on the nightstand and went
   cold, **and** through all of it the small horned tiger lay against the
   wall…"
   *Problem:* five "and"s; reads as a list rather than the lyrical beat it
   wants to be. *Fix:* break after "without her." and after "window." —
   "She slept the deep blank sleep of the truly spent. The school went on
   without her; the light left the window. Somewhere a tray of food was brought
   up, set on the nightstand, and went cold. Through all of it the small horned
   tiger lay against the wall…"

3. **Ch. 8** — "The shell was thicker than she expected, **and** tougher,
   **and** it fought her, **and** she set her teeth and pulled harder, **and** a
   long curved piece came free…"
   *Problem:* four "and"s in an action beat where short sentences would raise
   tension. *Fix:* "The shell was thicker than she expected, and tougher. It
   fought her. She set her teeth, pulled harder, and a long curved piece came
   free…"

4. **Ch. 9** — "And the High Chancellor will not meet him, **and** will not stop
   the killing, **and** one day soon the asking is going to run out, **and** then
   there will be war, **and** it will be a bad one, because…"
   *Problem:* five "and"s in dialogue. Allowable as building dread, but trim to
   three for punch: "…will not meet him, will not stop the killing. One day
   soon the asking runs out, and then there will be war — and it will be a bad
   one, because…"

5. **Ch. 10** — "She thought her mother would have liked the tiger, **and** the
   dragon, **and** the language, **and** Mrs. [Zoran]…"
   *Problem:* simple list reading as stacked clauses; use serial commas.
   *Fix:* "She thought her mother would have liked the tiger, the dragon, the
   language, and Mrs. Zoran…"

6. **Ch. 14** — "…**and** harder to govern, **and** impossible to count and rank
   and file into the neat shapes a frightened council liked, **and** so the
   council had not fought them in the open…"
   *Problem:* long sentence with four "and"s plus an embedded triple
   ("count and rank and file"). *Fix:* split after "frightened council liked."
   and start "So the council had not fought them in the open…"

7. **Ch. 13** — "She was sitting at a polished marble table, **and** nine others
   sat around it, **and** the light through the high windows was the light of a
   century she had only read about, **and** the faces around the table were grave
   and tired and powerful, **and** she understood…"
   *Problem:* five "and"s setting a scene. *Fix:* period after "around it." and
   after "read about." to let each image land.

8. **Ch. 16** — "Lamps had been knocked from the walls **and** flame was climbing
   the old dry panelling, **and** the smoke lay along the ceiling and reached
   down, **and** there were bodies, **and** Viridia made herself not look at the
   bodies…"
   *Problem:* in a high-tension action beat the chain dilutes urgency.
   *Fix:* break into staccato: "Lamps had been knocked from the walls. Flame
   climbed the old dry panelling. Smoke lay along the ceiling and reached down.
   There were bodies. Viridia made herself not look at them…"

*(Full machine-generated lists of every double- and triple-"and" sentence are
reproducible via `tools/style_check.py`-style sweeps; the eight above are the
representative worst cases per the brief's "TOP FIXES" request.)*

## ISSUE TYPE 2 — Filter verbs (she felt / saw / knew / watched / noticed)

Counts of `[character] + felt/saw/noticed/watched/realized/knew/heard/sensed`:

Highest: **Ch. 11 (22)**, **Ch. 14 (22)**, **Ch. 12 (15)**, **Ch. 13 (15)**.
Lowest and cleanest: Ch. 7, 8, 9, 18 (≤5).

**Worst cluster — Ch. 14 vision sequence**, seven near-consecutive openers:
> "She felt the size of the world… **She saw** the pact. **She saw**, after, the
> severing. **She saw** the council… and **she felt** them reach into the source
> itself… **She saw** more than the fact of it. **She felt** the fear that had
> driven the cut… **She felt** the satisfaction of the mages…"

*Problem:* repeated `She saw/She felt` sentence-openers dull what should be the
book's most overwhelming sensory passage, and the filter distances the reader
from the vision. *Fix:* drop the filter on at least half — let the images land
directly: "The pact. Made in the [setting]…" / "The severing came after." /
"The council of frightened mages at the marble table…" Reserve "she felt" for
the one or two beats where her *reaction* (not the sight) is the point.

**Other examples to recast (Ch. 11):**
- "she **realized** with mild surprise, happy" → "and she was — mild surprise —
  happy."
- "She **heard** them before she **saw** them" (acceptable; the double here is a
  fixed idiom and reads fine — leave).

*Recommendation:* a light pass converting roughly half the filter verbs in
Ch. 11–14 to direct rendering. Not a per-instance error list; treat as a
density target.

Tight third-person on Viridia produces frequent consecutive `She …` openers in
every chapter (sampled heavily in Ch. 1, which is locked). This is largely a
voice trait, not an error, and the brief asks only for "close proximity"
flags. Watch for runs of 3+ identical openers when revising the Type-2
filter-verb clusters above (fixing those will also break the `She saw/She felt`
opener runs). No standalone fixes itemized — handled by the Type-2 pass.
