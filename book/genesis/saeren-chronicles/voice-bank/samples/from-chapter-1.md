# Voice Bank — Samples drawn from the LOCKED Chapter 1 (the benchmark)

These are the canonical voice exemplars. Match this register, restraint, and detail-logic in all chapters.

### 1. Understatement / the planted line (grief carried by silence)
> "Just be careful who you let see you do it." / Viridia had thought it was an ordinary thing to say. / She thought about it for the rest of her life.

### 2. Domestic sensory texture (warmth before loss)
> He set the plate down in front of Viridia with a flourish, dark crusts and all, and kissed the top of her head on the way past, smelling of soap and machine oil and the cold morning he'd already half walked through to fetch the milk.

### 3. The deep-water stillness (a person read through stillness)
> There was a way her mother held stillness — not the stillness of someone resting, but the stillness of deep water, of something with a great deal moving underneath a calm surface.

### 4. The one wrong detail (the signature perceptual move)
> Her shoes were blue. Bright, plain, slightly scuffed blue, the blue of a thing chosen because it was liked rather than because it matched. Under all that careful autumn, the blue shoes were practically shouting.

### 5. Grief as ambient image (never named "grief")
> Her fingers closed around the dark-blue weight of it at her throat, the carved edges biting gently into her palm, and she held on the way you hold the side of a pool when the water is deeper than you expected.

### 6. Voice under pressure (the inner mantra; control held, not lost)
> The pressure behind her eyes built and built. She breathed around it. She did not let it past. No one sees you do it, she told herself, in something close to her mother's voice. You decide who sees.

### 7. Viridia's dialogue (spare; the true thing as a small gift)
> Viridia did not know what to say to that, so she said the first true thing that came to her. "I like your uniform."

### 8. Alice's voice (warm, fast, self-deprecating, no edges)
> "That's a good name. Mine's just Alice. There were three Alices in my year at home." She made a small face about it, comic and unselfconscious. "I always had to be Alice P."

### 9. Double-edged authority (Lady Lightwell's second meaning)
> You were chosen. Said one way it was reassurance. Said another way it meant you were selected, and assessed, and we know exactly why you are here.

### 10. The ugly/plain sentence in a quiet moment (rhythm grounding)
> There was nothing for it. She went.
