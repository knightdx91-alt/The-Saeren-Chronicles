## 1. PREMISE (structural)

A fourteen-year-old girl, newly and silently orphaned, arrives at a prestigious school for light mages carrying the one thing that would get her killed if anyone knew: both halves of a deliberately-severed magic, merged into a single whole core. Over one school year she must survive an institution built to categorize, rank, and quietly suppress what it cannot contain — while grief, a hidden heritage, and a coming war close in around her.

**Irony engine:** The school exists to nurture magical talent; it is in fact built to suppress the only complete magic left in the world. The thing that makes Viridia extraordinary is the thing she must hide to live. The safest place she has been handed is the most dangerous place she could be.

**Escalation ladder (maps to turning points):**
- ~25% (Ch.5–6): Viridia summons Lor-ar with both halves of the gift in front of a witness — she can no longer pretend, even to herself, that she is ordinary.
- ~50% (Ch.8–9): The bond with Raizen and the truth about the school's hidden war — the danger is no longer abstract; it is inside the walls.
- ~75% (Ch.13–14): Top marks bring the High Chancellor's attention; the well of magic shows her what the world lost. The hunters now know where to look.
- Climax (Ch.16–18): The attack, the escape, the open wound of not knowing if Alice lives.

**The question (book spine):** *Can Viridia survive in a world that wants her dead — and at what cost to the girl she was?*

**Concept Pitch Test:** "The only person born whole in a world that severed its own magic must hide it inside the school built to destroy what it can't categorize." PASSES — shareable.

## 2. THEME AS QUESTION

**Series theme:** Division is artificial and harmful — the severing of light and dark was an act of fear, and the same impulse that broke the magic breaks people.

**Book-One question (never answered on the page):** *What is the difference between being whole and being broken — and who gets to decide?* Viridia is told all her life the world is one way; she will discover she may be the only whole person she has met, in a world that would call her the broken one.

Theme presence target: ~75–85% of chapters. Allow it to recede in Ch.11 (the Dangris — action/competence) and partly Ch.3.

## 3. CHARACTERS (chaos profiles)

### Viridia Saeren — protagonist (POV)
- **Surface:** quiet, watchful, slight silver-blonde girl; reads as shy.
- **Wound:** both parents killed three days before the book opens; she does not know the full truth.
- **Lie:** "If no one sees me grieve, the grief cannot take me — and if no one sees what I am, I am safe."
- **Want:** to survive, to understand what she is, to not be seen.
- **Need:** to let herself be seen, and to let herself grieve.
- **Arc:** grief-numbed arrival → purposeful survivor who has let exactly two or three people in.
- **AUTHENTIC COMPOSURE (documented):** her restraint is deliberate and load-bearing — NO ONE sees her cry. Chaos is therefore SUBTLE and filtered: intrusive thoughts she dismisses fast, a memory that ambushes her, a control that nearly (not quite) fails. The near-failures matter precisely because she wins them. The eventual real breakdown (later book) is being banked.
- **Irrelevant obsession:** naming things. She files away names of paintings, plants, people — labelling the world so it cannot come apart.
- **Cognitive distortion:** assumes grief makes her a burden; reads others as wanting reassurance and provides it at cost to herself.
- **Voice markers:** dry, rare humor (a gift when it lands); short declaratives under pressure; notices the one detail that doesn't fit (blue shoes, chipped nails); reaches conclusions and keeps them.

### Alice Pembrook — supporting protagonist
- **Wound:** first-generation mage; quiet certainty she doesn't belong.
- **Lie:** "I borrowed this life; someone will come tell me there's been a mistake."
- **Want:** to belong; to be taken seriously; to help people.
- **Arc:** excited newcomer → genuine friend with her own competence (medical wing, the Moravian language).
- **Chaos:** experiences joy without embarrassment; homesickness leaks out sideways; practical where Viridia is philosophical.
- **Voice markers:** warm, fast, self-deprecating, no edges; over-shares then waves it off; asks the kind question and does not push.

### Amber Summers — sponsor → peer
- **Wound:** at eight, found both parents murdered; shattered her own light core trying to bring them back, succeeding only long enough to say goodbye.
- **Lie:** "If I give someone else the chance I was given, the cost will mean something."
- **Want:** to give others her chance. **Need:** to stop defining herself by the loss.
- **Chaos:** easy-going manner over real damage; dark humor; impulsive (sponsored a three-day first-year); deflects sincerity with jokes.
- **Voice markers:** lounging cadence, dry, ticks points off on her fingers, "I've always been a bit impulsive."

### Lor-ar — sylvan tiger, spirit walker
- Three centuries of patience; finds humans fascinating and exhausting; affection through honesty, not warmth; dry wit. "little one" — RATION it; save for moments that matter.

### Lady Isolde Lightwell — headmistress, complicated ally
- Dark core hidden under a light-core necklace (SHOWN via mana sight, never stated). Runs a resistance through the school. Jazen's mother (withheld). Composure that is itself a warning. Blue shoes = the one human contradiction.

### High Chancellor Erik Meros — antagonist
- False grandfatherly warmth; clinical assessor; true believer in light supremacy; silver-framed glasses of true seeing (plant EARLY); underestimates Viridia.
