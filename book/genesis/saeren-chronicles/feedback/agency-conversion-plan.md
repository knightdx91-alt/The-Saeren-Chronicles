# Agency Conversion Plan — Book One: The Hazel Years

**Purpose:** Fix the central structural flaw flagged by both external reviews — Viridia is too **passive**. Events happen *to* her, are *given to* her, or are *done on her behalf* far more often than she *causes* them (Analytical Review E1; Beta Panel consensus #2). This document is a **design plan only**. No manuscript prose is edited here.

**Method:** Reuse beats that already exist. Do **not** invent new subplots or new plot events. Change the *grammar of causation* so Viridia drives beats she currently receives — and attach a real **cost** to each new decision, because agency without cost is not agency.

**Hard constraints respected throughout:** Pembrook; JAZEN (not Jasen); Lady Lightwell's ROSE-tinted spectacles; Viridia silver-blonde/green-eyed/5'5"/slight, grief held inward, NO ONE sees her cry; trains+motorcars; no Hazel houses; no Johnathan Masters / void woman; planted refrains kept EXACT ("be careful who you let see you do it / you decide who sees"; "no one sees you do it"). The **fixed climax causality is untouchable**: the Chancellor came for *Lightwell*; the silver lenses read *Lightwell's* dark core; the decree followed; Viridia survived by hiding perfectly + the Ch.10-seeded rune-craft + Lor-ar's leap. Conversions must not contradict that chain. Word floor ≥ 85,000 and the style gate (simile ≤4/1k, em-dash ceilings, no new shared 4–6-word phrase) must remain satisfiable — length impact is noted per conversion.

## PART A — THE DECISION MAP

Tag legend: **[ACTS]** = a choice by Viridia that *causes or bends* the plot (with its cost noted). **[PASSIVE]** = something happens *to* her / is given to her / done on her behalf / she observes or reacts. "[ACTS-soft]" = a *moral/intellectual* stance she chooses that has no plot consequence and little personal cost (real but low-wattage agency).

### ACT ONE (Ch. 1–6)

**Ch.1 — The Path to Hazel**
- Parents already 3 days dead; she is *delivered* by Aleric. **[PASSIVE]** (inciting event is pre-page-one).
- Tastes cold metal at the gate / stone warms — she notices, tells no one. **[PASSIVE]** (observation).
- Misreads the copper-haired girl as "cruel." **[PASSIVE]** (a *wrong* read — characterization, not plot).
- Lets the silence cost her the smile with Alice; **chooses** not to smooth it over. **[ACTS-soft]** (a small relational choice, no plot weight).
- Stands when Alice says "Walk with me?" — accepts. **[PASSIVE]** (responds to an offer).

**Ch.2 — The Emergency Meeting**
- Amber declares herself sponsor "as of an hour ago." Given *to* her. **[PASSIVE]**.
- The focus + the unsigned note ("*Tell her no. Keep this with you always.*") are *placed in her luggage*. **[PASSIVE]** (the book's most-noted "arranged, not chosen" beat).
- Reads the note as *surveillance* rather than gift. **[ACTS-soft]** (interpretive stance; no action taken).
- **Chooses** to open the forbidden spellbook at night → finds the family tree, the dateless name, Bella's letter. **[ACTS]** (a real if small act of will; cost = re-opened grief, a secret taken on). *This is one of her few genuine self-initiated actions in Act One.*
- The maimed fourth-year / dragon lesson is *witnessed*. **[PASSIVE]**.

**Ch.3 — Introduction to Magic**
- Volunteers "Energy" / declines a focus from the box. **[ACTS-soft]** (chooses to answer truthfully despite the note's "don't draw attention").
- Mrs. Zoran *wakes* the focus, *reveals* "made for someone with both," *gives* the warning about her brother. All delivered **to** her. **[PASSIVE]**.

**Ch.4 — History and Lunch**
- Writes "**No**" on the History essay, builds the argument, turns it in despite the social risk. **[ACTS-soft → ACTS]**. *The single fully-active, self-initiated, costed choice in the early book.* The Analytical Review names this (with the Ch.16 rune-kill) as one of her only two real agency beats. Cost = exposure of her conscience; it later draws Lightwell's attention.
- **Amber spends her one irreplaceable favor** to get Viridia into Warfare + private summoning. Viridia stands "quiet behind her as instructed." **[PASSIVE]** (the agency is *Amber's*; the protagonist literally watches her own plot turn happen).

**Ch.5 — Amber's Story**
- Receives Amber's backstory. **[PASSIVE]**.
- The summoning: she **chooses** to reach "with all of it," to answer her real need ("not to be alone"), kneels before Lor-ar, says "Yes." **[ACTS]** — *but* heavily framed as instinct/being-answered ("the pact heard a need that large and answered it"), and the cost is unintended (near-drain, nosebleed), not chosen. Net: a willed reach, then a thing that *comes to her*. **[ACTS/PASSIVE blend]**.

**Ch.6 — Lady Lightwell's Concern**
- Summoned to account; Lightwell decides her fate ("excused tomorrow," "see me in the morning"). **[PASSIVE]**.
- Lor-ar's lore delivered. **[PASSIVE]**.

**ACT ONE TALLY:** Real plot-bending **[ACTS]**: ~2 (spellbook open, essay) plus the summoning blend. Everything load-bearing (sponsorship, focus/note, Warfare placement, the bond *answering*) is **[PASSIVE]**. **Ratio ≈ 2 / 12 ≈ 17% active.** This is the slow-opening + passivity complaint, and they are the same problem.

### ACT TWO (Ch. 7–12)

**Ch.7 — What Lor-ar Knows**
- The mana-sight, the "you are whole / the world is broken" thesis, the size of her core — all *shown/told to* her. **[PASSIVE]** (the Analytical Review's "preached, not enacted" core).
- **Chooses NOT to tell Alice** what she sees (the "lonely kindness"). **[ACTS-soft]** (a costed restraint — real, but it *prevents* action rather than causing plot).

**Ch.8 — The Forest at Night**
- Lor-ar takes her to the dragons. **[PASSIVE]**.
- "The pact had chosen for her." Raizen is *handed* to her by the bond-moment. She helps him from the shell and names him. **[PASSIVE]** (helping the hatch is reactive; the bond is imposed by the pact). Carries Varissa's death-threat as instructed. **[PASSIVE]**.

**Ch.9 — Lady Lightwell's Office**
- Delivers the message (as instructed). **[PASSIVE]**.
- States her *want* ("protect myself / help people"), states her belief about dark mages. **[ACTS-soft]** (declarations; the midpoint finally names a goal, but reactively).
- The resistance, Jazen, the war-clock — all *revealed to* her. **[PASSIVE]**.

**Ch.10 — The Language of Magic**
- Taught the language by Lor-ar. **[PASSIVE]** (learning).
- **Chooses to teach Alice to see / teach her the language.** **[ACTS]** (self-initiated; low stakes, but a real causal act — it changes Alice and later Mrs. Zoran).
- **Teaches Mrs. Zoran the language**, who then resolves to teach the whole school. **[ACTS]** (her teaching *causes* a change in the institution — one of her few outward-rippling actions). Cost = exposure risk.
- Amber reports the near-killing / war-clock. **[PASSIVE]**.

**Ch.11 — The Dangris**
- Dangris *arrive and offer* to train her; *give* her four Damascus spears. **[PASSIVE]** (gifts; the "made for her / waited for her" pattern the Hostile reader flags).
- Within the sparring she **reads Venquar's tell and lands a hit**. **[ACTS-soft]** (active skill, contained inside a lesson; no plot consequence).
- Lor-ar feeds her the life-fire/open-core lesson (the Ch.16 Chekhov gun). **[PASSIVE]** (received).

**Ch.12 — The Ranking Test Is Announced**
- Moved out of Zoran's class (decided *for* her). Announcement *delivered*. **[PASSIVE]**.
- **Deduces the Chancellor will bring a "tool" to *look*, and resolves to give it nothing.** **[ACTS-soft → ACTS]**. *This is strong: her deduction + the decision to bank her core is the plan that later saves her life.* Cost = three days of grueling self-suppression; the strain of "being less than she is." It is preparation, not yet plot-bending, but it is her own strategy.

**ACT TWO TALLY:** Genuine outward **[ACTS]**: teaching Alice, teaching Zoran (institutional ripple), and the bank-the-core *strategy*. The spine of the act (dragons, Dangris, lore, war-clock) is received. **Ratio ≈ 3 / 13 ≈ 23% active** — better than Act One, driven by the teaching beats, but the *plot* still arrives at her.

### ACT THREE (Ch. 13–18)

**Ch.13 — The Day of the Test**
- Banks her core under the Chancellor's gaze (executes the Ch.12 plan). **[ACTS]** (sustained, high-stakes self-suppression — active concealment that works).
- The scenario-disc: on the 5th scenario she **picks the rod for "No"** — refuses to condemn the children, knowing it risks exposure. **[ACTS]** (a costed moral choice; the disc goes gold). *This is the History-quiz stance escalated — the report says "build more like this.")*
- Combat test: reads Tobias, can't beat him, concedes. **[ACTS-soft]**.
- "Follow me back to the arena" — *summoned* by the Chancellor. **[PASSIVE]**.

**Ch.14 — The Portal**
- The well/source visit is *administered* to her by the Chancellor as a "reward." **[PASSIVE]** (she's sent; the larger core is given).
- **Chooses to touch the pool with bare magic** → receives the severing-truth, finds her mother's thread, senses the absence. **[ACTS/PASSIVE blend]** (the touch is willed; the flood of knowing is poured *into* her).
- **Lies to the Chancellor's careful last question** with a worried face. **[ACTS]** (active deception; high stakes; cost = sustained dread, knowing he half-suspects).

**Ch.15 — Amber Returns**
- The war-clock status ("another one named… on the desk") *reported to* her by Amber. **[PASSIVE]**.
- **Chooses, again, NOT to tell Alice/Amber about the well** (the want crests and breaks). **[ACTS-soft]** (costed restraint; prevents rather than causes).
- Names the war as "when" at Raizen's prompting; promises Alice the weekly just-us night. **[ACTS-soft]** (relational/internal).

**Ch.16 — The Attack** (CLIMAX)
- The attack happens *to* the school while she sleeps; she is woken. **[PASSIVE]** (E3: the war-clock expires off-page; the Hostile reader's "knew-all-day, did nothing" seam).
- Lor-ar kills the first soldier; she reacts. **[PASSIVE]**.
- **Kills two soldiers with the spear.** **[ACTS]** (reactive combat, but her own competence).
- **Inscribes the explosion-rune onto the captain's staff and unmakes him.** **[ACTS]** — *her single most causally decisive beat in the book* (the report and Devourer both flag the captain as the only on-page antagonist she personally defeats). Cost = the botched stroke feeds on her arm; permanent scar.
- **The alcove choice: save Alice (the one) vs. save the eight.** **[ACTS]** — *the thematic and structural high point;* the one irreversible, costed moral choice the whole book lifts toward. Cost = Alice, and lifelong guilt.
- Casts the portal, gets the eight out. **[ACTS]** (her competence saves them).
- The central duel (Lightwell vs. Chancellor) she is *ordered to walk away from* and watches from the gallery. **[PASSIVE]** — *she spectates the duel that decides everything* (the report's core climax finding). Survival = hiding + luck.

**Ch.17 — The Forest and the Camp**
- Leads the children out; asks each if they saw the ward; **[ACTS]** (leadership; the asking is self-initiated and costed).
- Shadow-bound and *assessed* by Jazen; *taken* to the camp. **[PASSIVE]**.
- Reaches for the Alice-bond that isn't there. **[PASSIVE]** (the book's best beat — but it is grief *received*).

**Ch.18 — What Comes Next**
- Observes the council argument; reads the players. **[PASSIVE]** (watches).
- Offers Jazen the "everyone's right" insight. **[ACTS-soft]** (counsel).
- **Asks Jazen to put out word on Alice.** **[ACTS]** (self-initiated; the one thing she reaches out and takes). Faces forward; reinterprets the refrain as *choose*. **[ACTS-soft]** (internal).

**ACT THREE TALLY:** This is where she finally acts — bank-and-lie (Ch.13–14), the rune-kill + alcove + portal (Ch.16), leading out + asking for Alice (Ch.17–18). **Ratio ≈ 8 / 16 ≈ 50% active.** But the *decisive* one (the climactic duel) is spectated, and the attack itself overtakes her asleep.

### OVERALL TALLY & WORST STRETCHES

| Act | Genuine plot-bending [ACTS] | Total significant beats | Approx. active ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ch.1–6 | ~2 (+1 blend) | 12 | **~17%** |
| Ch.7–12 | ~3 | 13 | **~23%** |
| Ch.13–18 | ~8 | 16 | **~50%** |
| **Whole book** | **~13–14** | **~41** | **≈ 33% active** |

Roughly **one beat in three** is Viridia *causing* the story; two in three are done *to* or *for* her. The active beats also cluster heavily in the back third, leaving the opening (the part an acquiring editor reads first) at its weakest.

**Longest stretches with NO plot-bending [ACTS] beat:**
1. **Ch.5 (after the summoning reach) → Ch.9** — the entire dragon-bond / midpoint-revelation run. Raizen is *given*, the lore is *told*, the resistance is *revealed*; her only acts are receiving and being assessed. **This is the worst passive stretch in the book** — ~4 chapters where the protagonist is carried.
2. **Ch.7 essentially whole** — the thesis is preached to a passive listener (the report's "asserted, not achieved").
3. **Ch.16's climactic duel** — at the most important moment she is ordered out and watches.

This is the evidence base. The fix: convert existing passive beats into costed choices, weighted toward Ch.1–9 (to repair both passivity and the slow open at once) and the Ch.16 duel (to make her causally decisive at the climax, not merely protected).

## PART B — THE CONVERSION PLAN

Five surgical conversions. Each reuses an existing beat and only changes *who decides* and *what it costs*. Priority order reflects the brief: ≥2 conversions in Ch.1–9; the climax made causally decisive; built on her proven-active beats (the essay stance, the rune-kill).

---

### CONVERSION 1 — The focus-note: "Tell her no" becomes a *defied-or-obeyed* choice with consequences
**Priority: HIGHEST (Ch.1–9 fix #1; fixes slow open + the #1 "arranged, not chosen" complaint)**

- **Chapter & current beat:** Ch.2, the unsigned note in the third suitcase — "*During your first magic class you will be asked whether you need one. Tell her no. Keep this with you always.*" Then Ch.3, Viridia simply complies: she declines the box and shows Mrs. Zoran the focus.
- **What it is now (why passive):** The most-flagged "destiny-by-arrangement" beat (Hostile #3; Analytical E1). A stranger pre-decides her words; she obeys. She is *managed*, exactly as the prose itself admits.
- **Proposed change — the DECISION:** Keep the note (and its surveillance reframe — that stays). But make Ch.3 a real fork she *chooses*, against the note. Have Mrs. Zoran ask the class question; Viridia, having read the note as a stranger's hand "arranging" her, **deliberately refuses to be arranged** — she almost takes a focus from the box to prove to herself that she is not following an unseen handler's script... and then *chooses* her mother's focus anyway, but **for her own stated reason** (it's her mother's; it's the last warm thing she owns), explicitly overriding the note's instruction in her own head. The plot outcome (she keeps the heirloom focus) is unchanged; the *grammar* flips from "obeyed an instruction" to "made the choice the instruction tried to make for her, on her own terms."
- **What it costs her:** A real, visible price — by refusing to simply obey, she does the one thing the note warned against: she lets Mrs. Zoran *wake* and examine the focus openly (rather than quietly pocketing it as the handler intended), which is precisely what flags her to a teacher and seeds Lightwell's attention. She trades safety-by-obedience for self-determination, and it costs her the anonymity she came to Hazel craving. She knows it as she does it.
- **Ripple check:** Mrs. Zoran's "it was practically begging" reveal (Ch.3) and Lightwell's watchfulness (Ch.4/6) already follow from the focus being examined — this conversion *motivates* that examination as Viridia's own doing rather than passive compliance, strengthening downstream causation. No canon risk: focus stays the heirloom, Moravian inscription intact, "Tell her no" line kept verbatim as the thing she chooses to override. Light touch needed in Ch.4/6 to let one line acknowledge that her *choice* drew the eye.
- **Effort / payoff:** **Small reframe** (≈150–250 words reworked in Ch.3, one acknowledging line in Ch.4 or 6). **High payoff:** converts the single most-cited passivity beat, gives the opening an early *active* protagonist, and turns "arranged" into "chose despite being arranged" — which also answers the chosen-one-fatigue critique.

### CONVERSION 2 — The sponsorship/Warfare placement is *earned/provoked* by Viridia's own act
**Priority: HIGH (Ch.1–9 fix #2; she currently "stands quiet behind Amber as instructed" while her plot turns)**

- **Chapter & current beat:** Ch.4, the Lightwell office scene. Amber spends her one favor; Viridia "stood quiet behind her as instructed" and watches the most consequential schedule-change of her year happen *for* her.
- **What it is now (why passive):** The agency is entirely Amber's. The protagonist literally observes someone else bend her plot — the cleanest illustration of E1.
- **Proposed change — the DECISION:** Keep Amber spending the favor (that's Amber's arc and her cost — untouched). But give Viridia **one decisive intervention inside the scene** that *tips* it. When Lightwell is refusing the Warfare placement and on the verge of sending them away, Viridia — breaking her "quiet behind her" instruction — **speaks for herself**, and what she says is the thing that turns Lightwell: she names, plainly and at her own risk, *why* she needs it (tied to the essay she just wrote, or to the truth that she has "no one" and will not be "pleasant and helpless"). It is Viridia's words, not Amber's favor alone, that make Lightwell relent — Lightwell grants it because the *girl* showed her something, not only because the sponsor paid.
- **What it costs her:** She breaks cover. The whole scene she was supposed to be invisible; by speaking she shows Lightwell exactly the quality ("you notice things… the most dangerous thing about you") that marks her for the rest of the book. She also *shares* the cost of the favor: Amber spent the currency, but Viridia spent her anonymity and put herself on Lightwell's radar on purpose. Two people now pay for this door.
- **Ripple check:** Strengthens Ch.6 and Ch.9, where Lightwell's pointed interest in Viridia must feel motivated — now it's because Viridia *spoke up*, not just because a sponsor vouched. Amber's "I spent it on you" and the "one favor, no more" rule are preserved exactly. Lightwell's "you argue like someone's taught you to" can transfer or extend to Viridia. No canon risk. Keep Lightwell's rose lenses, the white/onyx crystals, the warding.
- **Effort / payoff:** **Moderate scene-rework** (≈250–400 words restructured in Ch.4's office scene). **High payoff:** removes the starkest "she watches her own plot" beat; gives her a second early *active* moment; deepens both her and Lightwell. Note: roughly net-neutral on length (reallocates dialogue), so no word-floor or style-gate pressure.

### CONVERSION 3 — The bond-call (Lor-ar) becomes a *chosen rule-break* that costs her
**Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH (repairs the worst passive stretch, Ch.5; reduces "the forest just answered" + "destiny-by-prop")**

- **Chapter & current beat:** Ch.5, the summoning. Framed so the *forest/pact* answers a need ("the pact heard a need that large and answered it whether we willed it or no"); Lor-ar arrives, offers the bond, and the prose leans on Lor-ar "chose to grant you a companion."
- **What it is now (why passive):** Her one Act-One "act" is heavily diluted by being *answered* and *granted* — the Hostile reader's "destiny-by-prop." She reaches, but the world supplies.
- **Proposed change — the DECISION:** Keep everything that's fixed (Lor-ar exists, the extinct sylvan tiger, the bond). Reframe the *call* as a deliberate, knowing **rule-break**: Amber has explicitly warned her (Ch.5 already has the "call something too big and it can kill you" lesson) to start small — a mana toad — and Viridia, fully understanding the risk, **chooses to pour everything out anyway**, overriding Amber's instruction, because she decides she would rather risk death than settle for a small bond that might not stay. The arrival of Lor-ar then reads as the *consequence of a choice she made against advice*, not as the pact graciously answering. Lor-ar can even name it: she did not summon him so much as *refuse the safe call* and throw the door open wide enough that something large heard.
- **What it costs her:** Real and immediate — the near-fatal drain (already in the text: nosebleed, collapse) is now the *price of her defiance*, not an accident. Add the relational cost: she disobeyed Amber, who is shaken by it (Amber's "you poured yourself out like a jug with no bottom" already exists — let Amber be *angry/frightened* that Viridia did it on purpose). And the lasting cost: Lor-ar's warning that she has announced herself to "some hungry thing" out past the clearing — a defiance with a tail on it.
- **Ripple check:** Preserves the pact mechanics needed for Ch.8 (the dragon bond still flows from "a need that large"). Tighten one or two lines so the pact "answering" reads as *her* having forced the question, not the universe gifting. Keeps Lor-ar's traits, size-shifting, "little one." Minor touch in Ch.8 so the dragon-bond echoes the same "she reaches past the safe limit" pattern (which also helps Conversion-adjacent the "two artifacts made for her" complaint by making *her overreach*, not destiny, the engine).
- **Effort / payoff:** **Small-to-moderate reframe** (≈200–300 words across Ch.5, a few lines in Ch.8). **Good payoff:** converts the diluted central Act-One act into a clear costed choice and directly addresses the destiny-by-prop critique that caps Originality.

### CONVERSION 4 — The pre-attack warning is *read and acted on* (wrongly, tragically) instead of slept through
**Priority: HIGH (fixes E3 + the Hostile reader's #1 credibility seam; makes Act Three her drama, not the night's)**

- **Chapter & current beat:** Ch.16, the long "she knew all day and overruled it" passage, ending in "She should have trusted it," followed by the attack arriving while she sleeps ("The screams came in the deep middle of the night").
- **What it is now (why passive):** The hyper-observer is made implausibly inert for exactly one day so the attack can both surprise and feel seeded; the war-clock expires *off-page* while she's asleep. Both reviews flag this as the book's worst credibility gap and the clearest "overtaken, not acting" beat.
- **Proposed change — the DECISION:** Keep the fixed climax causality fully intact — the Chancellor still came for Lightwell; the lenses still read Lightwell; the decree is still signed; the school still falls; Viridia still survives by hiding + rune-craft. Change only this: instead of talking herself out of the warning (Ch.16 currently has her *almost* warn Lightwell and stop), have her **act on the wrongness — and act wrongly.** She reads the signs (the soldier in the laborer's coat, the wrong wagon, the short-staffed kitchen) and **makes a decision**: she goes to *warn Lightwell* (or to get Alice out of the ward early). Her intervention is real but *misfires* in a way that does not change the macro-outcome but *is* her choice: e.g., Lightwell, already knowing the blow is coming and having chosen to stand, tells her it is handled and to hold position / keep hidden — so Viridia *obeys an order to do nothing*, which is a chosen, costed restraint rather than a plot-convenient nap. **Or** (stronger): she acts to move Alice and is *turned back* / *runs out of time* by her own caution, so the loss of Alice is partly the consequence of a decision she made, giving her guilt real counterfactual weight (exactly the report's Top-Fix #1: "make Alice's loss something her choice causes, not just something the night does to her").
- **What it costs her:** Everything that matters. If she warns Lightwell and is told to hold, she carries having *obeyed* into the catastrophe (a chosen wall against her own instinct). If she tries to reach Alice and is turned back by time/wards/her own banking discipline, then the alcove choice in the same chapter becomes the *second* time that night she chooses the many over the one — and Alice's loss is causally tied to a decision, not to sleeping. Either way she is *awake and deciding* when the war comes through the door.
- **Ripple check:** This is the most delicate conversion — it must **not** let her warning change who survives at the macro level (canon: Lightwell falls, school burns, Alice's fate stays open). Safest framing: Lightwell has *already* decided to make her stand (she sent the dark-mage-evacuation plan into being chapters ago); Viridia's warning only confirms what Lightwell knows, so the outcome holds but Viridia's agency and guilt are both real. The "knew all day" interiority (Ch.16) gets *converted* into action rather than deleted, so it stops reading as a foreshadowing cheat. Touch needed: Ch.15's war-clock (the decree "on the desk") can be nudged so the *expiry* is something she半-anticipates, removing the "expires off-page while she sleeps" charge. Keep "No one sees you do it" mid-battle line; keep the rune-kill and alcove exactly.
- **Effort / payoff:** **Moderate scene-rework** (≈300–500 words reworked at Ch.16's opening; one threading line in Ch.15). **Very high payoff:** repairs the single worst credibility seam, converts the war-clock from off-page expiry into the protagonist's own race-and-fail, and — crucially — makes Alice's loss partly *caused* by Viridia, which the Analytical Review names as the highest-impact fix in the book. Mild length addition (acceptable; helps word floor).

### CONVERSION 5 — The climactic duel: give Viridia ONE causal lever on its outcome (so she is decisive, not protected)
**Priority: HIGHEST (the brief's explicit requirement: make Ch.16 a climax where she is causally decisive, not protected)**

- **Chapter & current beat:** Ch.16, the gallery. Viridia watches Lightwell vs. the Chancellor; Lightwell sends the mental order "*You cannot help me here. Do not try. Save as many as you can… Go.*"; Viridia obeys and leaves. The duel that decides everything happens without her.
- **What it is now (why passive):** "The heroine spectates her own climax" (Analytical Review, the central climax finding). Her survival is by *not being seen*; the decisive event is fought by someone else and she is *ordered away* from it.
- **Proposed change — the DECISION:** Do **not** insert her into the duel as a combatant (that would break the fixed causality and the "she survived by hiding" truth, and would re-trigger the MC-shield critique). Instead, give her **one decisive, deniable lever** that *changes an outcome* without changing who wins the duel — using a power the book has already armed:
  - **Option A (preferred — reuses the Ch.11 "open core / read the thread" gun + the Ch.10 "object remembers" rune-craft):** From the gallery, *unseen*, Viridia **chooses** to use the dark-side thread-reading Lor-ar taught her — not to fight the Chancellor head-on (she'd lose / be seen) but to do one precise, hidden thing that *buys Lightwell the seconds she needs to hold the line long enough for the eight to be gathered and the portal cast*. E.g., she inscribes/feeds a single rune that disrupts the Chancellor's footing or his life-fire thread for one beat — enough that Lightwell is not overwhelmed *before* Viridia gets the children out. The Chancellor still ultimately prevails over Lightwell (canon holds); but **whether the eight escape at all now hinges on Viridia's hidden act**, not just on Amber's shield. She *causes* the rescue's possibility, decisively, from cover — agency and concealment both preserved.
  - **Cost built in:** doing it nearly exposes her (the Chancellor *almost* feels the interference / the lenses sweep the gallery), and it deepens her guilt — because the same power that buys the eight their seconds is power she chose *not* to spend trying to reach Alice. She made the furnace-core serve the many; she will wonder forever whether she could have spent it on the one.
- **What it costs her:** Near-exposure (she risks the very thing she spent three days learning to prevent); and the moral weight that she *had* a decisive power in that room and chose to aim it at the eight, not at Alice or at saving Lightwell — turning the alcove choice from a single fork into the spine of the whole climax.
- **Ripple check:** **Must preserve:** Chancellor came for Lightwell; lenses read Lightwell's dark core; decree signed; Lightwell *falls* (Jazen's Ch.17 grief depends on it); Viridia survives by hiding + quick rune-craft + Lor-ar. The lever changes only the *escape's* contingency, never the duel's winner. Keep Lightwell's "you were chosen… Go" order — but now Viridia *partly disobeys the "do not try" in a hidden way she can live with*, which also pays off the Ch.18 reinterpretation of the refrain ("not only hide — choose"). Watch the MC-shield line: keep the act *costly, deniable, and small* so it doesn't read as the fourteen-year-old out-magicking the duel. Coordinate with Conversion 4 so her Ch.16 choices form one chain: *act on the warning (and fail to save the one) → in the gallery, spend her hidden power on the eight → the alcove → the portal.*
