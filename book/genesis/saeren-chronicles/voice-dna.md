# Voice DNA — Hazel Academy

> Derived from the LOCKED chapter-1.md (the benchmark) and the character bible. The narration is close third on Viridia, past tense.

## GLOBAL NARRATIVE VOICE
- Close third limited on Viridia. Past tense. British-inflected, slightly formal but never cold.
- **The signature move:** Viridia reads a person or a room through the ONE detail that doesn't fit (blue shoes under autumn colors; chipped black nails; a tooth turned out of line). The narration finds that detail and lets it stand for the whole.
- Grief is ambient — it colors perception without being named. Doors that won't open again. Holding the side of a pool when the water is deeper than expected.
- **Restraint is the engine.** The most devastating beats are understated. The narration tells LESS than the moment demands. "She thought about it for the rest of her life."
- Sensory texture is concrete and domestic (scorched bread, soap and machine oil, cold tea). 30–40% of detail is pure texture.
- Rhythm varies: long deep-water sentences for grief and reflection; short flat declaratives at the moment of greatest pressure.

## VOICE UNDER PRESSURE (Viridia)
At peace: long, observant, layered sentences. Under pressure: sentences shorten and flatten; she retreats into cataloguing and naming; the inner mantra surfaces ("No one sees you do it. You decide who sees."). The body presses toward release and she presses back — eyes burning, breath managed, the stone gripped — and she WINS, every time, in Book One. The near-loss is the drama.

## PER-CHARACTER VOICE CARDS (cover-the-name)
- **Viridia (dialogue):** spare. Often a single word ("Yes."). When she speaks more, it's a precise true thing offered as a small gift ("I like your uniform."). Dry humor, rare. Never explains herself.
- **Alice:** warm, fast, runs on; self-deprecating; over-shares then waves it off ("There were three Alices in my year at home. I always had to be Alice P."); asks the kind question and does not push.
- **Amber:** lounging, dry, ticks points on her fingers; deflects sincerity with a joke; "I've always been a bit impulsive"; under it, real damage.
- **Lor-ar:** formal, ancient, amused; affection through honesty; "little one" rationed; finds humans fascinating and exhausting.
- **Lady Lightwell:** composed, weightless precision, double-edged kindness ("You were chosen") with a second meaning under the warm surface.
- **Mrs. Zoran:** enthusiastic, kindly, drops her guard into real risk ("Knowledge is power").
- **Drake:** gruff, clipped, grief disguised as bluntness ("Doesn't look like much"). **Varissa:** warmer, pragmatic, fierce about Raizen.
- **High Chancellor Meros:** false grandfatherly warmth over clinical assessment.

## ANTI-PATTERN BUDGET (commercial fiction clean band 0–8/20; aim ≤5)
Watch especially: #11 explanatory-extension similes (cut the unpacking, trust the reader), #14 emotional-control demonstration (Viridia's control must sometimes nearly fail, never narrated analytically), #19 graduated reveal (vary structure per outline), #4 em dashes (Ch.1 leans on them — ration in later chapters), #10 described emotions (carry emotion in objects/actions). Keep ONE ugly sentence per chapter, in a quiet moment.

## DIALOGUE MECHANICS
Comma inside quotes before tags. "said"/"asked" invisible; avoid creative tags. Characters interrupt, trail off, answer the wrong thing. Silence counts.
