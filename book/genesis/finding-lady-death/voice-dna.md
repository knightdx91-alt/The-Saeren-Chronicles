# Voice DNA — Finding Lady Death (*Death and Remembrance*)

> Executable, enforceable voice spec. Derived from the author's opening scene
> (`research/original-draft.txt`) = the binding benchmark. The Writer writes FROM this;
> dialogue-polish and hook-craft check AGAINST it; the editor/evaluator and the style gate
> hold the line. **[PROPOSAL]** items await author confirmation.

---

## 1. GLOBAL NARRATIVE VOICE

- **POV / tense:** Close third on **Alice**, **past tense**, throughout — including
  dialogue tags ("Alice asked," "he said"). **[PROPOSAL — RECOMMENDED]:** the opening mixes
  past narration with present-tense tags; standardize to PAST. This preserves the opening's
  *narrative* spine and is the romance default. Ch.1 polish silently fixes the tags.
- **Vocabulary level:** ~4/10. Plain, warm, unpretentious, contemporary. Never ornate.
- **Sentence rhythm:** Mixed, leaning short-to-medium; conversational cadence; the
  uncanny stated plainly (a god on a hospital floor described like a colleague).
- **Formality:** Conversational/casual. Narration sits close to Alice's plainspoken,
  practical mind.
- **Humor:** Dry, frequent-but-light; mutual deflection between the leads is the engine of
  their chemistry. Humor is the salt, not the dish.
- **Emotional register:** Open but never melodramatic. Big feeling is *earned by
  understatement* — circle the emotion, don't report it. Tenderness undercut by a joke,
  then the joke dropped at the exact right moment.
- **Distinctive features:** bedside intimacy; the uncanny made ordinary; endearments doing
  heavy lifting ("little one"); practical questions as emotional tells ("What happens
  now?").
- **Dialogue share:** target **35-45%** per chapter (romance-appropriate; the opening runs
  ~60%, which is fine for a reveal chapter but should settle lower in narrative chapters).

### Anti-patterns (the voice NEVER does)
- Purple/ornate metaphor; "the universe held its breath" cosmic grandiosity.
- Emotional-temperature reports ("a wave of grief washed over her," "fear coursed through
  him").
- Analytical/explanatory similes ("it was as if… , the way a X does Y") — the #1 AI tell.
- Metacognitive narration ("she realized she was realizing").
- Head-hopping (single POV — never leave Alice's perspective).
- Em-dash overuse (style gate: **≤4 per chapter, absolute**). Note: the opening leans on
  dashes; convert most to periods/commas in polish.
- Adverb-loaded dialogue tags ("she said worriedly").

### VOICE UNDER PRESSURE (how the narration breaks under Alice's overload)
- Sentences **fragment**; clauses drop.
- Alice **retreats into clinical precision** — vitals, counts, names, procedure — as a
  shield against feeling (the prose goes *cooler* as the stakes go hotter).
- Humor goes **brittle**, then **vanishes** entirely.
- At maximum overload the prose goes **flat / numb** — deliberately under-described (see
  Ch.15). Numbness, not purple, is how this book signals devastation.
- Her stress-tell surfaces: a clipped **"What happens now?"** / "Okay. Okay."

## 2. PER-CHARACTER VOICE CARDS

### Alice Haywood
- **Vocabulary band:** Educated-but-plain; medical terms used precisely and then
  translated; no showing off. Midwestern-neutral.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Short-to-medium; rapid practical questions when overwhelmed;
  lists/counts when she's coping. Hedges with "I think" / "I just."
- **Rhythm:** Statements that resolve into a question. Deflecting one-liners.
- **Verbal tics:** "What happens now?" (signature, from the opening's last beat);
  "Okay" used to self-steady; "I just—"; names body parts/pulses out of habit.
- **Never says:** cosmic/grandiose register; she will not call anything "destiny" or
  "fate" sincerely without flinching from it; no flowery declarations of love (she shows it).
- **Sample line:** *"He's got maybe two hours, his sats are tanking, and you're telling me
  the god of death is busy. Okay. Okay — what happens now?"*

### Acheron (Death)
- **Vocabulary band:** Old diction sanded down by modern slang — "ripping off a bandaid,"
  "botched," "I just wanted to make sure you were okay." Says **"Gods"** (plural), never
  "God." Understates the apocalyptic.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Gentle, slightly formal baseline; trails off when it hurts;
  placating constructions ("Now hold on, let me finish").
- **Rhythm:** Soft declaratives; boyish self-deprecation; long patience that cracks into
  speed when the clock is mentioned.
- **Verbal tics:** **"little one"**; **"You always were a sharp one"**; "Gods, I'm not
  doing this right"; nervous grins narrated around his speech.
- **Never says:** clinical/medical jargon (Alice's domain); cruelty; he will not *pressure*
  her ("it's up to you… how you want to live your life").
- **Sample line:** *"I had just gotten married when the choice came. Gods. I'm botching
  this again — okay. Little one, the short version is: I've been looking for you for a very
  long time, and I'd really rather not lose you to the explanation."*

### The First Death (antagonist)  [PROPOSAL — name/gender open]
- **Vocabulary band:** Courtly, archaic-formal, *unbroken* by slang (the opposite of
  Acheron — that contrast is the cover-the-name solution). Reasoned, never raised.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Balanced, periodic sentences; rhetorical questions; the calm of
  someone who has already won the argument in his head.
- **Verbal tics:** addresses Alice as **"child"** or "Doctor" (formal, never "little one" —
  reserved to Acheron); "Surely you see"; "It is only right."
- **Never says:** slang; apology; raised voice; he never threatens — he *offers.*
- **Sample line:** *"He kept you from death and calls it love. I would only ask you to do
  your work as you swore to — to do no harm. Surely you see the difference, child."*

### Dr. Marisol Reyes (mentor)
- **Voice marker:** Clipped imperatives, gallows humor. "Chart it or it didn't happen."
  Never finishes a soft sentence.

### Jonah Okafor (friend)
- **Voice marker:** Over-talks, fills silence, comfort-quotes statistics; slips into third
  person when stressed.

### Amy Garber (the dying patient)
- **Voice marker:** Tart, unsentimental; calls Alice "Doctor" with affectionate irony.

## 3. VOICE DIFFERENTIATION MATRIX (cover-the-name, pre-solved)
| Character | Sharpest distinguisher | Contrast vs nearest |
|---|---|---|
| Alice | rapid practical/clinical questions; "What happens now?" | vs Acheron: she's clinical & questioning, he's gentle & declarative |
| Acheron | "little one" + modern-slang-softened old diction; says "Gods" | vs First Death: same archaic root, but Acheron breaks formal with slang & self-deprecation |
| First Death | unbroken courtly formality; "child," "Surely you see"; never apologizes | vs Acheron: Acheron always cracks formal; First Death never does |
| Reyes | clipped imperatives, gallows one-liners; unfinished sentences | vs Alice: Alice questions, Reyes commands |
| Jonah | over-talks, stats-as-comfort, third-person-under-stress | vs everyone: he fills silence; the leads use silence |
| Amy | tart, dry, "Doctor" with irony | vs Reyes: Amy is patient-side & wry, Reyes is staff-side & terse |

> No two characters share a sharpest marker. Acheron vs First Death (the riskiest pair —
> both archaic) are split by the **slang-break test**: Acheron always softens formal
> diction with modern slang and self-deprecation; the First Death never does, and never
> says "little one."

## 4. ANTI-PATTERN BUDGET (genre: paranormal romance; density per 1,000 words)
> Enforced by the style gate (`tools/style_check.py`) + disruptor. The Writer aims UNDER;
> the disruptor cuts down to these ceilings.
- **Similes/metaphor markers:** **≤4.0 / 1,000w** (style-gate hard ceiling; romance band).
- **Em-dashes:** **≤4 per chapter, ABSOLUTE** (style-gate hard rule — note the opening
  over-uses dashes; convert most in polish).
- **Explanatory/analytical similes ("as if / the way a X…"):** ≤2 / 1,000w.
- **Adverbs in dialogue tags:** near-zero (≤1 per chapter).
- **"It was as if / almost as though":** ≤1 per chapter.
- **Metacognitive narration ("realized she was realizing"):** ≤1 per chapter.
- **Emotional-temperature reports ("a wave of X washed over her"):** ≤1 per chapter — and
  in pressure scenes, ZERO (use fragmentation/numbness instead).
- **No NEW repeated 4-6 word phrase across chapters** (deliberate motifs go in the gate's
  ALLOWLIST: "little one," "you always were a sharp one," "what happens now," "the grace
  period," "do no harm," boatman/lantern phrasing).

## 5. OPEN-DOOR / MODERATE INTIMACY — how it reads
- **Heat = OPEN-DOOR, MODERATE:** sensual on-page, **not graphic**; not erotica. Two
  earned scenes (Ch.8 first, Ch.13 known-love), each freighted with the impossibility and
  the clock — intimacy carries PLOT/emotion, never gratuitous.
- **Register in intimate scenes:** stays in the global voice — plain, warm, emotion-forward;
  focus on **sensation, tenderness, and what it MEANS** (he can't be seen; she can; what
  are they) over anatomical detail. Suggestive, not explicit. No purple; the em-dash and
  simile budgets still apply.
- **Voice-under-pressure applies here too:** at the peak, Alice's prose can fragment into
  short, present sensory beats; Acheron's nervousness (the eternal being undone) is the
  emotional hook, not performance.
- **Climax/transformation (Ch.18-19):** NO heat — switch to a **sacred/quiet register**;
  the taking of the mantle is reverent, not sensual.

## 6. BENCHMARK SAMPLES (canonical targets — from the opening scene)

benchmark. Three target modes every downstream agent measures against:
1. **CONTROLLED** — the Brookwood reunion (opening, paras at Amy's bedside): warm, plain,
   dialogue-forward, uncanny-made-ordinary. *This is the default sound.*

   under maximum grief (clinical-shield + fragmentation). Sample label:
   `sample-02-breaking.md`.
3. **IRRELEVANT-THOUGHT** — to be written: Alice reheating coffee / cataloguing bad ward art
   mid-cosmic-conversation (human noise). Sample label: `sample-03-irrelevant-thought.md`.
> Action for the chapter loop: extract 10-15 short passages into `voice-bank/` once Ch.1 is
> finalized; ≥3 must show the voice BREAKING, ≥2 must include irrelevant thought.
