> Opens IMMEDIATELY after Book One Ch.18 (`research/book1-final-chapter-18.md`) — same
> morning / next days in Jazen's hidden resistance camp. Voice = Book One (close third on
> Viridia, past tense). All binding Book-Two beats from the Series Roadmap are mapped below
> and tagged **[BINDING]**. Structural approach varies; no two consecutive chapters repeat.

## ACT ONE — THE CAMP (Ch.1–6) · arrival, Alice resolved, the option named

### Ch.1 — The Box of Invisible Air  · *chronological* · ~4,800
Days after the finale. Viridia learns the camp from the inside: routines, the wards, the
refugee Hazel girls folding in. Establishes the war-clock (Jazen marches "in a week") and
the camp's fragility now that Lightwell's warnings are gone. Introduce named villagers as
living people: **Brutus** (rebuilding a wall), **Mirelle** (teaching children Moravian at the
long table), **Hiram** (a ward-mage, tired). Viridia is useful, watchful, banking her furnace
to a flicker by daylight. Raizen still away across unmeasurable miles — the bond a warm ache.
END HOOK: the first supply line returns with survivors and rumor from Hazel's roads.

### Ch.2 — The Thread Pulled Taut  · *parallel* (Raizen's return / the rumor) · ~4,900
**Raizen returns** — across the bond first, then in the flesh with Drake, who has carried news.
The reunion: wordless contentment, then his first real sentences (he speaks now). Joy, briefly.
Cut against: a returning scout's rumor — soldiers pulled survivors *alive* from Hazel and took
them toward the capital; among them "a heavyset first-gen girl, a healer's hands." Viridia does
not let her face move. Hope and dread in the same breath. **[BINDING: Alice's fate — planted.]**
END HOOK: Viridia asks Jazen to confirm it.

### Ch.3 — Alive Is Not the Same as Safe  · *chronological* · ~4,800
**[BINDING: Alice's fate RESOLVED in first three chapters.]** Jazen's lines confirm it: Alice
Pembrook is alive, held in the capital — first-generation healers are being collected, not
killed (the capital "uses what it can use"). Relief that is its own kind of agony: now there is
a person to save inside the place that wants Viridia dead. The march is no longer abstract. First
real Jazen scene that develops HIM (not just the plot): his Jan-grief, his fear of his own power.
Seed the moral engine privately: Viridia, lying awake, first lets herself think the forbidden
thought — *I could end all of this. I know how.* She puts it away. **[BINDING: Jazen development begins.]**

### Ch.4 — His Mother's Papers  · *spiral* (returns through Lightwell's cache) · ~4,800
Camp community deepens (Mirelle, Brutus, the children; Amber climbing back). Jazen shares what
his mother left — a compartmented cache of papers, names, routes. Among them, in Lightwell's
hand, a copy of an **unsigned note** matching the one packed with Viridia's focus. **[BINDING-adjacent:
the unsigned-note thread surfaces — Lightwell sent it.]** And a fragment of a **family line** with one
name that "doesn't sit right" — **re-seeds the family-tree mystery** (its Bk1 vehicle burned). Viridia
keeps her face still; files it. Jazen begins, gently, to guess what she is. END HOOK: she realizes the
papers also hold a way to reach moderate light mages — a thread she might pull instead of a sword.

### Ch.5 — The Furnace and the Match  · *essayistic→scene* · ~4,700
Viridia tests her power away from camp (Lor-ar present, dry counsel). She feels the world's lattice
of broken cores through her mana sight and the source-connection, and understands fully, for the
first time, that she *can* mend the severing — not for one person, for everyone, at once. **The
rebirth becomes a real option.** Lor-ar names the cost and the question without answering it. The
moral problem is now hers to hold. END HOOK: she decides she will try the *peaceful* roads first —
she will not reach for the unforgivable thing while any other door is open.

### Ch.6 — The Window Narrows  · *chronological* · ~4,900
War pressure made concrete: a patrol comes close; a skirmish at the wards; **Hiram is wounded**
holding the screen (foreshadows the camp's exposure). Viridia uses her spears and restraint, not
her full core — keeping hidden costs lives, and she feels it. Jazen and the council strain. END OF
ACT ONE: every day the window for a bloodless answer shrinks; Viridia commits to spending it. END
HOOK: she asks Jazen to let her try to reach the capital's moderates before the march.

## ACT TWO — THE QUESTION (Ch.7–13) · the attempts, the challenge, the inevitability

### Ch.7 — What She Tries First  · *parallel* (the embassy / the dragons) · ~4,900
**[BINDING: Viridia's real attempts at a peaceful solution — what she tries.]** Attempt #1: using
Lightwell's contacts, she drafts and sends a true message to a faction of light mages who might
listen — proof the severing was deliberate, an offer of the whole gift freely given, not imposed.
In parallel, she and Jazen portal to **Drake and Varissa** to consult. **[BINDING: Drake & Varissa
consulted/involved.]** Varissa's fierceness, Drake's grief. END HOOK: Drake agrees to teach her
the one thing she's avoided.

### Ch.8 — Dust to Dust  · *chronological* · ~4,800
**[BINDING-adjacent: the death symbol taught & destined for meaningful use.]** Drake teaches the
**Death symbol** — what ends any magic, closes any portal. He teaches it as grief, not as a weapon
(he lost a child to the last war). Viridia learns it and is frightened by how clean it is. Attempt
#2: the moderates' reply arrives — interest, then silence, then a forgery/betrayal: the capital
intercepts and twists the overture into "dark mages plot to corrupt your children." A road closes.
END HOOK: Amber, who carried the message line, confronts Viridia about what she's really planning.

### Ch.9 — The Right  · *parallel* (two arguments, one room) · ~5,000 · DIALOGUE-HEAVY
**[BINDING: the explicit moral challenge — Amber asks whether Viridia has the right to remake
everyone's magic without consent.]** The book's spine, made flesh. Amber: *you fought a government
that controlled people through their magic — how are you different if you change every core alive
without asking?* Viridia has no answer that survives being said aloud. Jazen's mirror: he too has
power he's terrified to use. No resolution — the question is left standing, heavier than before.
END HOOK: a third peace road opens — a moderate councilor will meet, in secret.

### Ch.10 — The Almost  · *chronological* · ~4,900
**[BINDING: what almost works.]** Attempt #3: the secret meeting — it *nearly* works; a moderate is
genuinely moved. Interleave: Viridia finally reads Lightwell's papers in full — the unsigned note
confirmed as Lightwell's hand, and the off-pattern family name examined (Book-Three seed kept live).
Then the almost collapses: the Chancellor, through the glasses-of-true-seeing network, exposes the
meeting; the moderate recants or is killed; the capital declares total war and parades **Alice and the
other captives** as proof of the "dark corruption." Every peaceful road is now closed — by the enemy,
not by Viridia's choice. END HOOK: the camp's location may be compromised.

### Ch.11 — The Cost of Hiding  · *chronological* · ~4,900 · partly action
The camp is found. The capital's force strikes the hidden village (they lost their warning when they
lost Lightwell — Bk1's finale paying off). **[BINDING: ≥3 named villagers whose deaths matter.]**
**Mirelle dies** shielding the children at her long table; **Hiram dies** holding the ward so others can
flee; **Brutus is mortally hurt** defending what he built. **[BINDING: the village destroyed — begins.]**
Viridia stops hiding her power to save who she can — and it is not enough. END HOOK: in the ash, Jazen
says the thing the council feared: there is nothing left to protect by waiting. They march now.

### Ch.12 — Goodbyes  · *mosaic* (partings across the camp) · ~4,800
The army assembles from the survivors. **[BINDING: Drake & Varissa say goodbye to Raizen.]** They
sense the shape of what's coming (a True-Bond reaching toward the source) and say goodbye to their son
as if it were forever — grief in their two registers. Viridia sits with the decision she's circling:
if the war cannot be won without it, she will mend the severing — and she tells no one but Lor-ar, who
does not absolve her. Quiet character beats: Amber, the rescued girls, the dead remembered. END HOOK:
they take the road to the capital.

### Ch.13 — The Road and the Walls  · *chronological* · ~4,900
The march; the approach; the capital's walls. Tactics, dread, the small human business of an army of
the grieving. Jazen at his fullest here — **[BINDING: Jazen developed across ≥4 chapters before death]**
(this is his fourth-plus). His private fear that he'll lose control again; Viridia's promise to be the
hand on his arm. END OF ACT TWO: the two armies face each other before the capital. The Chancellor, in
his glasses, rides out to parley — with Alice and the captives in chains. END HOOK: he offers terms no
one can accept.

## ACT THREE — THE WEIGHT (Ch.14–20) · the battle, the death, the rebirth, the changed world

### Ch.14 — The First Hour  · *accelerated chronological* · ~4,900 · battle
The battle joins. Viridia's spears, her restraint finally spent in the open, the death symbol held in
reserve. The named dead of Ch.11 are avenged and more fall. Brutus's last stand pays off his Bk1 fear
(he dies on the field, not hidden). Chaos, cost, the captives' position on the wall. END HOOK: Viridia
sees a path to Alice through the shadows — and a worse thing: Jazen breaking toward the Chancellor.

### Ch.15 — Through the Shadow  · *parallel* (Viridia's rescue / Jazen's charge) · ~4,900
**[Alice payoff begins.]** Viridia shadow-walks to reach the captives; the reunion attempt — Alice
alive, changed, calm hands and all. Cut against Jazen, grief-blind, cutting toward the Chancellor
against the plan. Viridia must choose: finish the rescue or stop Jazen. The choice costs (echo of the
Bk1 corridor — she cannot save everyone at once). END HOOK: she reaches one of them too late.

### Ch.16 — Before Both Armies  · *chronological* · ~4,900 · THE HINGE
**[BINDING: Jazen's death given full weight — beheaded by the High Chancellor before both armies.]**
Not a sentence — the whole chapter bends around it. Jazen reaches the Chancellor; is taken; and is
executed in the open, deliberately, as a message, the glasses watching. The armies see. Viridia sees.
**The breakdown Book One banked finally breaks** — restraint fails completely, on the page. END HOOK:
in the silence after, she stops being careful about who sees what she is.

### Ch.17 — The Decision  · *chronological / interior* · ~4,800
The resistance is breaking; the Chancellor triumphant. Viridia, past restraint, makes the choice she
swore she'd avoid: she will mend the severing — for everyone, without asking, because every other door
is shut and the bodies are real. **[Drake's intervention:]** the Chancellor moves to end her; **Drake
kills the High Chancellor with his death-flames.** **[BINDING: High Chancellor dead.]** With the
immediate threat gone, Viridia turns toward the source. Amber's question rides with her — unanswered,
chosen anyway. END HOOK: she reaches for the well of all magic.

### Ch.18 — The Rebirth  · *fragmented→whole* (overwhelm) · ~5,000
**[BINDING: the magical rebirth scene expanded to ≥3 full pages.]** **[BINDING LOCATION (author, 2026-06-23):
she PORTALS to the physical Book One cavern — the low rough stone chamber, the pedestal, the pool that is the
source itself (Bk1 Ch.14) — and performs the rebirth THERE, at the actual well, with her hand in the pool, ALONE
with the source (not from the battlefield rise; the Ch.17 "reached for the well" hook = her opening the seam back
to the cavern).]** Viridia at the source, mending the six-hundred-year severing for every living core at once —
written as overwhelm resolving into a single terrible clarity. She touches **the thread with Bella's name** again
at the same pedestal, and again cannot follow it (Book-Three thread held). She feels the world change. **[BINDING:
everyone's magic made whole, and none of them know why.]** The cost to her is named, not narrated flat. END HOOK:
she returns through the portal to a battlefield that has simply… stopped — every mage on both sides suddenly
holding the whole of themselves and not understanding (the stopping happened while she was away at the well; she
steps back onto the rise into the aftermath of it).

### Ch.19 — What Remains  · *chronological, quieter* · ~4,900 · wonder + grief
The war is over (no one can fight what no one understands; the Chancellor dead, his cause hollow).
**[BINDING: Raizen's transformation into human form — written with wonder and a touch of humor.]** He
steps into a human body for the first time and is delighted, appalled, and funny about feet, cold, and
buttons. **[BINDING: Drake and Varissa say goodbye to Raizen — completed.]** **[BINDING: Lor-ar released
from his bond, returns to the forest.]** — a quiet, costly goodbye; "little one," last and meant.
**[Alice resolved:]** freed, reunited — the friendship under the new weight. END HOOK: the camp is gone;
where do the living go now.

### Ch.20 — None of Them Know Why  · *essayistic→forward* · ~4,800 · FINALE
**[BINDING: ending — war over; Chancellor dead; Jazen gone; village destroyed; everyone whole and none
know why.]** Viridia counts the cost: Jazen, Mirelle, Hiram, Brutus, the camp, the girl she was. What
she gained: Alice back, Raizen at her side in a new shape, a world made whole by a choice she still
cannot justify and would make again. She faces forward — not safety, movement; not resolution, residue.
FINAL BEAT **[BINDING: seed Marick/the lab]** — ONE cold paragraph, far away: in a laboratory a man named
**Marick**, holding the whole of a magic he never earned and does not understand, is about to make a very
bad mistake. Do not explain it. Let it sit. (And, abstract, the void-rim differential a shade wider —
no entity, no void woman — Book-Three thread.)
