## 1. PREMISE (structural)

A fifteen-year-old refugee carrying world-altering knowledge — that she can restore
the whole of everyone's severed magic — must decide whether she has the right to
remake every living person without their consent, while a war she cannot stop closes
in and the resistance leader whose grief mirrors her own marches toward his death.

**Irony engine:** Viridia fought a government that controlled people through magic.
The one thing that could end the killing is an act of the same kind — changing every
core in the world without asking. To save people from control she would have to
control them once, totally, in the dark. *The cure is the disease, performed by the
right hands.* The book never lets her off that hook.

**Escalation ladder (maps to turning points):**
- ~15% (Ch.2–3): Raizen returns; Alice is confirmed alive and captured. Viridia now
  has a person to save and a reason to want the war to end faster than it can.
- ~30% (Ch.5–6): Viridia realizes she *can* mend the severing for the whole world —
  the rebirth becomes a real option, not a dream. The moral problem becomes hers to hold.
- ~50% (Ch.9): Amber asks the question the whole book turns on — *do you have the
  right?* — and Viridia has no answer that survives saying it aloud.
- ~75% (Ch.13–16): The camp is found and burned; the march; Jazen's death before
  both armies. Every road but the rebirth closes.
- Climax (Ch.17–20): The decision, the rebirth, the cost, the changed and uncomprehending world.

**The question (book spine):** *When you alone can end the suffering, does the power
to do it give you the right — and what does using it cost the one who does?*

**Concept Pitch Test:** "A fifteen-year-old who can heal the whole world's magic must
decide whether saving everyone gives her the right to change them all without asking."
PASSES — shareable, and it names the moral engine, not just the war.

## 2. THEME AS QUESTION

**Series theme:** Division is artificial and harmful — the severing of light and dark
was an act of fear, and the same impulse that broke the magic breaks people.

**Book-Two question (never cleanly answered on the page):** *Is there a difference
between control that cages and control that frees — and who is allowed to decide for
everyone?* Viridia will do the thing she cannot justify, because the alternative is
worse, and the book refuses to tell her she was right. The residue is the point.

Theme presence target: ~80–90% of chapters (this book IS its theme). Allow it to
recede for breath in the camp-community chapters (Ch.4) and Raizen's return (Ch.2)
and his transformation (Ch.19, wonder/humor).

## 3. CHARACTERS (Book-Two state; full canon in character-bible.md + book1-entity-state.yaml)

### Viridia Saeren — protagonist (POV)
- **Now:** 14 turning 15; whole core grown to a "furnace"; portal-capable; always-on
  mana sight; True-Bonded to Raizen; Moravian fluent; can mend the severing.
- **Wound (Book Two):** she left Alice and does not know if she lives; she has begun
  to be SEEN (Book One's arc) and now those she lets in keep being taken or killed.
- **Lie (evolved):** "If I am strong enough and careful enough, I can save them all
  without it costing me the right to call myself different from the Chancellor."
- **Want:** to find Alice; to end the war without becoming a tyrant.
- **Need:** to accept that some acts cannot be made clean — that she will have to
  choose, and carry the unjustifiable, and remain herself anyway.
- **Arc:** the girl who chose who could see her → the person who decides FOR everyone,
  once, and must live with having done it. **The breakdown Book One banked is spent
  here** (Jazen's death / the rebirth's cost) — restraint finally, fully fails, on the page.
- **Voice markers:** unchanged — reads the one detail that doesn't fit; short flat
  declaratives under pressure; the mother's line as compass ("be careful who you let
  see you do it" → now also "you decide who sees"); dry rare humor as a gift.

### Jazen — resistance leader (DEVELOP fully across ≥4 chapters, THEN kill with full weight)
- **Wound:** sister Jan burned; once killed a town's worth of soldiers in grief and
  has been terrified of his own power ever since; now his mother dead too.
- **Lie:** "If I strike hard enough and fast enough, no one else I love gets taken."
- **Want:** to make the killing stop; to not lose control again.
- **Need:** to let someone else carry part of it (he reaches this with Viridia).
- **Mirror:** he is Viridia twenty years on — grief-powered, restraint-bound, terrified
  of what he can do. His arc is the warning and the permission she both needs.
- **Death:** beheaded by the High Chancellor before both armies (full weight — the
  hinge of Act Three). His grief shown openly throughout (he cries; she does not).

### Amber Summers — peer / the moral conscience
