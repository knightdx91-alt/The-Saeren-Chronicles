## PART A — Act One front-pressure

**Problem:** Ch.1–5 run on interior positioning (the "closing window," the banked moral thought,
the careful face). The first external pressure that forces the plot is the **Ch.6 patrol** — ~25%
in. The "window is narrowing" clock is *named* repeatedly (Ch.3, 5) before it is *dramatized* (Ch.6).

**Goal:** make the closing window an *event* once, earlier, so dread becomes jeopardy before Ch.6 —
without cutting the interiority (relocate it onto the new beat) and without disturbing the
well-built Alice-confirmation (Ch.2–3) and Lightwell's-papers (Ch.4) reveals.

**Candidate locations (pick ONE; author/writer chooses the specific event):**
- **Ch.3 is the softest chapter** — it is almost pure interior (the three-day wait for Alice's
  answer). It is the natural host for an early external probe: a concrete sign the capital is
  *moving* (a ward-mage feeling the screen tested; a scout who doesn't come back; a too-near patrol
  that doesn't breach but proves the window is closing on a clock the enemy controls, not just one
  Viridia narrates). This converts the named "window" into a felt one and gives Ch.3 an external
  spine it currently lacks.
- **Alternative:** seed the Hiram-failing-ward jeopardy earlier (it currently lands in Ch.6). A
  visible early sign that the screen is thinning makes the "two pillars and a child" arithmetic a
  rising pressure across Act One instead of a Ch.6 reveal.

**Constraint:** do not pre-empt the Ch.6 patrol's function (first true breach + the fight-small
discipline + Hiram taking the blade). The earlier beat is a *probe / near-miss / omen*, smaller
than Ch.6, escalating toward it — not a duplicate battle.

**Scope:** one inserted/expanded scene (~600–1,000 w) in Ch.3, plus trimming one or two of the
"window is narrowing" *narrated* restatements in Ch.3/5 now that it's dramatized. Net roughly
word-neutral.

## PART B — Develop-not-restate the moral question (NARROWED — read this note first)

**Re-read correction (2026-06-29):** the diagnosis letter listed Ch.3/5/7/9 as restatements. A full
read of **Ch.9** shows it is *genuine development*, not restatement, and is the **strongest beat in
the book**: Amber escalates the question from the abstract ("how are you different from the council")
to the personal and unanswerable ("**I'm on the sheet — I shattered my own core on purpose; you'd
heal my chosen wound against my will; you can't leave me off the way you left off the dragon**"),
and Jazen supplies the *mirror* (the opposite sin — not-acting out of fear of what you are). **Ch.9
is the payoff. PROTECT IT — do not touch it.**

So the real restatement problem is narrower:

**Problem:** the **private "I could end this / and then she banked it" meditation** appears in
near-identical form **twice** — Ch.3 (lines ~408–420: she takes the world-mending thought out in
the dark, looks at its size, "shut it back down") and **Ch.5** (the same move, re-stated). The
reader is shown the same self-revelation of her own capability, in the same gesture, twice, before
Amber externalizes it in Ch.9.

**Goal:** make the two private appearances *develop* rather than echo, so the ladder reads
Ch.3 → Ch.5 → Ch.9 as **escalation**, not repetition.

**Change ledger:**

| Beat | Now | Disposition |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Ch.3 private "I could end this / banked it" (first articulation, in the dark after Alice confirmed) | Ch.3 | **KEEP as the first, clean statement** — this is where the thought is born. Leave intact. |
| Ch.5 private re-statement of the same "I could / I banked it" | Ch.5 | **CHANGE: make it advance, not echo.** Two options (author's pick): (a) **add a new constraint or fact** she learns about the doing (e.g. she proves at the rowan how *trivially easy* it is — Ch.5 already does this; lean the meditation into *that escalation* — "the ease is the danger," not "I could and I won't" again); or (b) **compress** the restatement to a few lines that explicitly build on Ch.3 ("the thought she'd banked three nights ago had not stayed banked; it had grown a new edge") rather than re-opening the box from scratch. |
| Ch.3 Amber's "the dark always votes yes" night-fire challenge | Ch.3 | **KEEP** — distinct function (Amber as conscience, first contact). |
| Ch.7 Drake's "you stand where the severing-hands stood" | Ch.7 | **KEEP** — develops it onto the cosmic/death-symbol axis; not a duplicate. |
| **Ch.9 the full moral challenge (Amber "I'm on the sheet" + Jazen's mirror)** | Ch.9 | **DO NOT TOUCH** — the developed payoff. |

**Scope:** a single targeted revision to the Ch.5 private meditation (~one passage), so it reads as
the *second rung* of a ladder rather than a second photograph of the first rung. No new plot. This
is the cheapest change that addresses the deepest note and it *strengthens* the Ch.9 payoff by
making the climb to it monotonic.
