# Climax Agency Fix — "Witnessed Cost" (2026-06-24)

Addresses the #1 cross-pass review finding (`review-synthesis-2026-06-23.md`):
protagonist passivity / agency theft at the climax — Drake kills Meros *for* her,
and the world-mend happens alone in a sealed cave, off-stage, **unwitnessed**.

## Approach chosen (author, 2026-06-24): WITNESSED COST
Reject the two canon-breaking levers (Viridia's hand ending Meros violates the
BINDING "Drake kills Meros with death-flames" beat *and* the "she is not a killer /
she mends, not destroys" theme; moving the rebirth onto the battlefield violates the
settled "portal to the physical Book-One cavern" decision). Instead, locate her
agency in **the mend being witnessed as cost** rather than in the kill.

## Changes (prose only; canon, plot, and both binding beats untouched)

- **Ch.17** — reframed the moment the rifle levels. Before: she *gathered herself to
  reach* and *knew she would not get there before the shot* (a helpless kneeler awaiting
  rescue). Now: she **does not wait** — she sets herself against the seam and **begins to
  open the way to the source** as the barrel comes up, choosing to die with her hand on
  the last door rather than empty-handed. Drake's death-fire therefore **clears the last
  obstacle to an act already underway**, not a rescue of a passive girl. One downstream
  line adjusted for consistency ("bought her the *finishing* of the thing she had already
  begun").

- **Ch.19 (new opening beat, ~330w)** — **Amber** is on the rise when Viridia steps back
  through, and **sees the cost land**: a girl come back blind, swaying, wrung to the wick,
  reading the world by the front of her eyes. Amber gets a shoulder under her and walks her
  off the rise. The sacrifice is no longer entirely off-stage: it is **witnessed by the one
  person who named the cost before she paid it** (Amber, who asked "do you have the right").
  Amber explicitly *can't tell what she did* — only what it took — so Ch.18's binding
  capstone ("none of them know why") is preserved. Also closes review finding #5 (Amber
  sidelined through Ch.14–18; relief banked too late) by re-centering her at the hinge.
