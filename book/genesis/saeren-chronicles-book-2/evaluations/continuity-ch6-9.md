not prose): record BOTH in ENTITY_STATE — the desk-man (thread closed) and the councilor
(the Ch.10 meeting) — as separate entries.

**WARNING-2 (resolve, light): "blinded seer" wording vs. what actually happened.**

crushes only the ONE starred lens and "left him the rest" — i.e., she destroyed his
TRUE-SEEING (the glasses), not his eyes; he walks home sighted. The chapter's own narration
twice leans on "blinded" ("the seer blinded," "a blinded seer who ran home"), which reads
as literal eye-blinding and lightly contradicts "left him the rest." FIX (optional micro,
or just note for the record): treat "blinded" as figurative-for-his-true-sight in
ENTITY_STATE so a later chapter doesn't render him eyeless. No prose change required if the
metaphor is accepted, but it is the one spot a careless future chapter could trip on.
