## Chapter 10 — "The Almost" — Floor 8.7

The "what almost works" beat lands with real engineering: the costed coat detail, the old councilor turning to the *girl* first, the lens on the already-checked roof, and the reveal that the parade-block was pre-cut (the door was never real). This is plot-as-tragedy done cleanly, and it converts Viridia's agency into cost (her one banked-furnace slip gets a man killed) per the round-6 doctrine. The Lightwell-papers interleave resolves the unsigned-note thread (Lightwell wrote it, unsigned on purpose) and re-homes the off-pattern family-tree name as a live Book-Three seed.

- **Pacing 8.7** — the chapter's structural weakness. The Lightwell-papers section (para 43–47) is a long static interior block dropped in the middle of a tense negotiation; it is gorgeous but it stops the clock hard, and the "she filed it" / "the dead kept their secrets" register repeats inside it. **Fix:** trim the family-tree paragraph (47) by ~25% — it circles the same not-knowing three times; one pass would hit harder.
- **Originality 9.0 / World 9.0 / Char 9.0** — strong; the true-glass "backtrack a lit mage to her root" mechanic is a clean, frightening escalation that also drives the next chapter. Slight deduction: the glass-network exposition (para 65) leans expository.
- No dimension under 8.5; clears the gate.

## Chapter 11 — "The Cost of Hiding" — Floor 8.7

The camp's destruction. Hiram setting the ward down slow rather than letting it fall; Mirelle's back as the table; Brutus and the beam (deliberately left mid-dying for a Ch.14 payoff). Emotion (9.7) is the batch high — the "four strides too far" arithmetic and the counting-children motif are devastating without ever naming the feeling, exactly the Book-One restraint engine.

- **World 8.7 / Originality 8.7** — the fire-mage raid is competently rendered but is the most genre-familiar material in the batch (a raining-fire assault on a refugee camp); it earns its place emotionally rather than inventively.
- **Pacing 9.0** — best-paced of the four; the 40-minute hold / sky-changes structure gives the action a clock and a turn.
- One craft note (not gate-affecting): para 79's "would not finish for three more chapters" and para 89's "three more chapters of him holding on to the beam" are **overt narratorial flash-forwards that break the close-third frame** — the narrator stepping outside Viridia to promise the reader future chapters. Book One's voice foreshadows through Viridia's perception ("she thought about it for the rest of her life"), not through chapter-counting. **Fix:** recast both as Viridia's interior premonition ("his dying had a longer outline than this") and drop the literal "three chapters." This is the single clearest voice-blemish in the batch.

## Chapter 12 — "Goodbyes" — Floor 8.5 (batch floor)

Mosaic of partings: Drake (loud, from the sky) and Varissa (silent, close to the ground) saying goodbye to Raizen; Lor-ar refusing to absolve; the birthday beat (Viridia turns 15 on-page, unmarked, per the standing decision); Brutus and the beam. Theme (9.5) and Character (9.5) are superb — the two dragon-grief registers are a genuine character invention, and Varissa's "I grieve close to the ground… bring him back, whatever shape" is the strongest single speech in the batch.

- **Pacing 8.5** — the reason this is the batch floor. As a mosaic of five goodbyes (dragons → Amber/girls → Brutus → Lor-ar → Jazen), it is structurally a sequence of static two-handers with low forward momentum; the army is *assembling* rather than *moving* for most of it, and three of the five beats end on the same note (a weight "filed whole" / handed across / carried). The Lor-ar scene (para 95–113), while thematically essential, restates Ch.9's no-absolution conclusion at length. **Fix:** the chapter can lose ~150–200 words from the Lor-ar exchange (the "everyone mistakes my silence for a blessing" point is made twice) and gain a beat of physical forward motion mid-chapter so the partings happen *against* the march, not in a held pause. This would lift Pacing to ~8.8 and the Floor with it.
- **Originality 8.7 / World 8.8** — the dragon-goodbye material is fresh; the deduction is for the birthday beat, which is handled with lovely restraint but is a quiet, low-stakes interior that slightly competes with the larger grief for the chapter's middle.
- Clears the gate at exactly 8.5. It is the one chapter I would flag for an *optional* light pacing pass — not required, but it sits on the line.

## Three weakest dimensions across the batch (with fixes)

1. **Pacing (range 8.5–9.0, batch's lowest dimension overall).** The Act-Two middle (Ch.12 + Ch.13) runs two consecutive low-momentum, elegiac chapters of partings and march-texture, and Ch.10 stalls in a long interior papers-block. *Fix:* (a) interleave the Ch.12 goodbyes with physical forward motion so they happen against the march; (b) trim the Ch.13 army-texture open ~20% to bring the wall on sooner; (c) trim Ch.10's family-tree paragraph. None are gate failures — these are tightening passes to keep the documented series-long pacing concern in check.
2. **Originality (8.7 across Ch.11–13).** The connective tissue — fire-raid on a camp, march to a walled capital, parley with chained captives — is the most genre-familiar material in the book. It works because the *emotional* and *thematic* execution is fresh, but the staging is recognizable. *Fix:* lean harder on the one-detail-out-of-place signature in the set-pieces (the gold top course, the boots, the beam) — these are where originality lives; give each big beat one such detail and resist the familiar wide-shot.
3. **World-building (8.7–8.8).** A few mechanics arrive as on-the-spot exposition rather than felt detail: the glass-backtrack network (Ch.10 para 65, and the scout's relay para 105), the "a ward this size wants three" rule (Ch.11 para 21). *Fix:* these are fine but slightly tell-y; where possible, let Viridia's mana-sight *show* the rule (she already "sees the ward thinning from underneath") rather than have a character state the arithmetic.

## Verdict — PACING

**Acceptable, on-watch.** No chapter falls below the 8.5 gate on pacing, but pacing is the lowest-scoring dimension across the entire batch and Ch.12 sits exactly on the floor because of it. The specific risk the author flags series-wide — "too quiet / too slow" — is concentrated in the **Ch.12→Ch.13 pair**, which runs two consecutive reflective chapters and lightly violates the outline's "no two consecutive chapters repeat" structural rule. The action chapter (Ch.11) and the tragic-collapse chapter (Ch.10) are well-paced. Recommendation: an *optional* tightening pass on Ch.12's Lor-ar scene and Ch.13's army-texture open (≈300–400 words total) would resolve the only real pacing soft spot without touching plot. Not required to pass; advisable given the standing concern.

## Verdict — VOICE-MATCH

**Strong match to the established Ch.1–9 / Book-One voice.** All four chapters reproduce the engine: close third on Viridia, the one-detail-out-of-place reading (the off-cut coat, the tooth turned out of line, the gold top course, the boots round the neck), grief carried in objects and arithmetic rather than named, the "filed it whole" cataloguing under pressure, the careful-face mantra, and the short flat declaratives at peak pressure ("We march now." / "Yes."). Per-character voices hold: Drake gruff-from-a-height, Varissa close-to-the-ground, Lor-ar's rationed "little one" and ancient honesty, Amber's deflection — all consistent with the voice cards. Simile density is low (0.7–1.1/1k), em-dashes well under ceiling.

**One concrete voice blemish to fix (Ch.11):** the two literal chapter-count flash-forwards — "would not finish for three more chapters" (para 79) and "three more chapters of him holding on to the beam" (para 89) — break the close-third frame by having the narrator address the reader's future reading rather than Viridia's perception. Recast as interior premonition and drop the chapter-counting; this is the one place the prose steps outside the benchmark voice. (A milder version of the same omniscient slip appears in Ch.13's "the way the truest things in this book got said" / "this war" — the narrator naming "this book"/"this war" as authorial categories; consider softening, lower priority.)
