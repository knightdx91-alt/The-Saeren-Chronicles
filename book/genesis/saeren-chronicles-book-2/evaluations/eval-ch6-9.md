## Per-chapter notes

### Ch.6 — The Window Narrows (Floor 8.7, Pacing)
The graft chapter, and it lands. The skirmish is concrete and the moral pressure is
specific: she KILLS to keep the secret (4 down, 2 by her hand), and the cost is paid in
"a coin she could feel" against the cup of furnace she can't feel — a clean inversion of
the Ch.5 rim-cost lesson. Hiram taking the blade rather than dropping the ward is genuinely
moving and load-bearing for the act. Floor is Pacing only because the council debrief +
Jazen walk-out stack two reflective stretches back to back after the fight; both earn their
place but the chapter's last third decelerates. Voice is a dead-on match to Ch.1–5 and to
Book One (close third, the "cold working part," the careful face, banked-furnace imagery).

### Ch.7 — What She Tries First (Floor 8.6, Pacing)
Excellent character chapter; the Drake/Varissa scene is the strongest new material in the
batch. Varissa's "you left him off the ledger" lands a real, unanticipated moral hit
(Viridia never counted Raizen's True-Bond into the rebirth cost) and Viridia's flat,
unflinching admission is exactly her voice. Drake's grief-as-weather, the death-symbol
offer seeded here. Floor is Pacing: the interleaved "man in the grey office" POV intrusions
(twice) are elegant but slow the dragon scene's momentum, and the chapter is two long
set-pieces (letter, mountain) joined rather than escalating. Still well clear of 8.5.

### Ch.8 — Dust to Dust (Floor 8.7, Pacing)
The death-symbol lesson is taught as grief, not weaponry — Drake's daughter, the
unkillable door, "you will hate how easy it is" — and the symbol's design (the unjoined
ring + inward line, deliberately echoing Lightwell's peace-mark) is a strong, resonant
invention. The road-betrayal turn (her true overture printed as the broadsheet "A WARNING
TO EVERY MOTHER") is a gut-punch and thematically perfect: the truth makes the better
poster. Floor is Pacing because the chapter carries two full beats (the lesson, then the
betrayal + Amber's confrontation) and the seam between them is a hard tonal pivot rather
than a build.

### Ch.9 — The Right (Floor 8.6, World-building)
The spine of the book, and the best chapter in the batch. Amber's moral challenge is
delivered in full and Viridia LOSES it on the page — her three rehearsed answers each
collapse into the voice of the council she hates ("making them whole" = "making them
safe"). Jazen's mirror (the opposite sin: not-acting out of fear of what you are; the
eighty dead, Jan in the cart) is devastating and gives the question a second axis. Amber
putting herself "on the sheet" — a core whose chosen wound would be healed against her
will — is the emotional peak of all four chapters. Floor is World-building only by
relativity: it is a near-bottle dialogue chapter, so the world recedes (appropriately);
nothing is wrong, the dimension is simply less exercised. Theme 9.6 / Emotion 9.5 are the
highest marks in the batch and deserved.

## Voice-match verdict
Strong and consistent. Ch.6–9 are indistinguishable in register from Ch.1–5 and faithful
to Book One: close third on Viridia, the "cold working part" doing arithmetic while the
rest of her feels, the banked-furnace/coal imagery, sentences that name the thing exactly
and refuse to make it larger or smaller. The adopted (epic-branch) chapters do not read as
a seam in voice — the graft is invisible at the prose level.

## Recommendation
PASS all four. Optional (non-blocking) polish: trim one of the two Ch.7 "grey office"
interludes and tighten the Ch.6 council coda to lift Pacing off the floor. Not required.
