## What the review asked for, and what we found
The review's #1 note ("too much thinking on walls/gardens, repetitive moral arithmetic, conflicts
resolve via introspection") matches our own developmental diagnosis. On a full pass to act on it,
**most of the recurrence turned out to be load-bearing, not redundant:**
- The **temptation ladder** (Vale Ch.3 → un-mend Ch.4/11 → Lest Ch.12 → city-men Ch.20) *escalates*;
  each instance is a new scene doing new work. Cutting rungs would break the escalation.
- Ch.7 (source-read, the accelerating-rate reveal) and Ch.13 (the **synthesis** that resolves the
  refusals into the positive "balance/valve" answer — and even lampshades the repetition: Amber,
  "I've heard the two things you won't do for a year; I want to hear one you *will*") are the spine.
- The genuine triple-restatement was the **denouement**, already fixed in r2 (Ch.20/21/22 → one).

So the literary-preserving cut surface is **smaller than the review's broad brush implies**, and
deep cuts into the ladder/synthesis were **declined on purpose** to protect the voice and the
escalation (the reason the author chose this option over the commercial revision).

## Edits made (genuine redundancy + two clean fixes; no canon moved)
1. **Ch.5 — consolidated the post-reveal restatement pile.** The binding pull-reveal ("I made this;
   the pull is the rebirth; the Horrors come toward me") is kept intact; the **three** subsequent
   re-statements of "she is the source" (the "larger than the no / larger than Amber's question"
   pile + the "stone/beans" callback + a third re-run) were consolidated to one, preserving the
   reveal's weight and the forward turn (go to the source; the mark can't close a pressure).
   ~110 words removed.
2. **Ch.15 — fixed an accidental meta-slip:** "she had spent **fourteen chapters** of her life
   walking the other way from it" (the number coincided with the chapter count) → "the better part
   of a year." Reads as authorial intrusion; now clean.
3. **Ch.1 — de-duplicated the "small true things" echo** (the review flagged it as a Books 1–2
   carryover used twice in the opener): the second instance reworded ("the way the truest things
   always reached her first") so the motif lands once in Ch.1.
4. Reverted a phrasing my Ch.5 edit introduced ("made of herself" → "made herself") to clear a
   cross-chapter repeat-gate collision.
