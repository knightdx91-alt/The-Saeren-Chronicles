## ACT ONE — THE UNSETTLED WORLD (Ch.1–7) · the remade world; sight returning; the first Horror; the rim is wrong

### Ch.1 — What the World Became · *chronological* · ~4,800
~A year after the rebirth. Re-establish Viridia (16) and the found-family where they settled after the
east road: **Raizen** learning to be human (wonder + the load-bearing relief beat), **Alice** slowly
recovering her warmth (healer again, hands no longer only "calm"), **Amber** the conscience. The remade
world from the inside: everyone now carries both halves of the gift and almost no one is trained for the
dark half — wonder and small disasters both. **NEW INSTRUMENT:** her mana-sight has been returning across
the year (present, not yet whole), and with it the source-sense — births and deaths register quietly in
her, always. Establish the weight of that. END HOOK: a death registers WRONG — drained, not natural —
somewhere far off; her returning sight catches a tear in the weave it could not have read a year ago.

### Ch.2 — The First Door That Should Not Be · *chronological / partly action* · ~4,900
They reach the wrongness: an unstable **portal** torn into the world, and the **first Horror** through it —
void-stuff that drains the life from what it touches (YA: consequence, cut the camera on the worst).
They barely stop it; her returning sight reads the portal as a *tear*, not a made door. **[BINDING seed:
the Horrors / unstable portals are loose.]** END HOOK: it is not the only tear — she can feel others, faint,
opening.

### Ch.3 — A City With No Head · *parallel* (the settlement / news of the capital) · ~4,800
The DEEPER conflict: the leaderless capital and the political chaos of sudden equality — old hierarchies
collapsed, opportunists and frightened factions, people with power they can't govern. Establish that the
human chaos may be the worse danger. A faction wants Viridia — the girl who remade the world — to lead, or
to *fix* it with her power. **The temptation is named** (the thing she fought: control through magic). END
HOOK: they ask her to use what she is; she feels how easy it would be.

### Ch.4 — The Easy Mark · *chronological* · ~4,800
Relationship deepening (Raizen's human arc; Alice; Amber's deflecting honesty) against escalation: a second
Horror incident. **First use of the Death symbol to CLOSE a portal** — and its eerie easiness flagged exactly
as Drake warned (it asks nothing; that is its danger). It works — but closing one tear does not stop more
opening. END HOOK: the tears are spreading faster than she can close them.

### Ch.5 — What She Will Not Do · *parallel* (the pull to rule / the trail to the source) · ~5,000 · dialogue-heavy
The temptation peaks early: the practical case for Viridia imposing order with her source-power is strong,
and **Amber's evolved question** lands — *now that you can feel every core, what stops you from running them
all?* She refuses, and wrestles with the refusal being a luxury people are dying for. Meanwhile the
investigation: the tears aren't random — there's a *pattern* (a trail toward the western lab region, AND a
direction in the pull itself). END HOOK: the pull points somewhere — toward the source. Toward her.

### Ch.6 — The Cost of Half-Sight · *chronological / action* · ~4,800
Escalation + real loss (YA-consequence): a Horror reaches a settlement / someone they care for, and her
not-yet-whole sight means she reads it a beat too late. Grief held inward. The world is fraying. END HOOK:
she resolves to go to the source itself and read the rim properly — to find where the tearing comes from.

### Ch.7 — The Rim, Read Whole · *essayistic→scene* · ~4,800 · END OF ACT ONE
Her sight returns enough — at the source (portal to the physical **Book One cavern**: pedestal, pool — match
the prose) — to read the rim of everything the way she used to. And the **void/source pressure differential
is WIDE** now, vastly wider than the "shade" she half-saw at the rebirth (Bk2 Ch.20). The dread crystallizes:
the world is being *pulled at*, and the pull runs toward the source she became. END HOOK: the first cold
inkling that she is the axis of it — held, not yet confirmed.

## ACT TWO — THE PULL (Ch.8–15) · the lab; the gut-punch; the political crisis; the temptation to control

### Ch.8 — The Man Who Picked Up the Chalk · *chronological* · ~4,900
The Marick thread surfaces: the western lab and the working that tore the first great veil. **Marick** — the
forty-year candle handed the whole dark half in the rebirth, who ignored the worn warning and *opened* a
severed-half working he could not govern — is the proximate spark. Introduce **Fen**, his young assistant
(the one who will run). But Marick's portal is a *symptom*: closing it (Death symbol) does not slow the pull.
END HOOK: the proximate cause is found and it changes nothing — the tearing continues.

### Ch.9 — Drake's Daughter, Again · *chronological / interior* · ~4,800
Consult the ones who know: **Drake & Varissa** (the mountain). Drake's Bk2 backstory pays off — his daughter
died to a **standing void-door** that could not be pushed shut, and he closed it with the **Death symbol**.
He names the void/source **balance**: the void is not evil, it is the counterpart; the symbol closes a *door*
but cannot fix a *pressure*. END HOOK: Drake tells her the truth she's been circling — something far larger
than Marick is wrong, and it is the shape of what *she* did.

### Ch.10 — Absence Drawn to Presence · *fragmented→clear* · ~5,000 · THE GUT-PUNCH (~50%)
With her sight whole at the source, she works it out and the source confirms it: **the Horrors are not
attacking — they are pulled toward the source the way absence is drawn to presence. The rebirth ran the weave
full and widened the differential. She made this.** [BINDING: the key revelation.] Render as overwhelm
resolving into one terrible clarity (the Bk2 Ch.18 counterpart). Every life the Horrors have taken traces
back to her cure. END HOOK: the cure had a cost no one could see, and it is here, and it has her name on it.

### Ch.11 — The Ledger Reopens · *chronological, quieter* · ~4,800
The aftermath of knowing — grief inward (no weeping). **Amber's question at its hardest:** you couldn't ask
them before you remade them; can you fix this without deciding for them again? The temptation to *un-mend* (give
the severing back to stop the pull) — monstrous, and she won't unmake the world's wholeness. The political
world worsens around the dread. END HOOK: a human crisis forces her hand before she has an answer.

### Ch.12 — The Thing She Fought · *parallel* (the human war / her restraint) · ~4,900 · the temptation peaks
The equalized-magic chaos erupts into a human conflict more dangerous than the Horrors. Viridia is pushed —
by need, by a faction, by the bodies — to **seize control "for everyone's good,"** the exact crime of the
council that cut the world. She comes to the edge of it. **The leash** (Amber / Alice / Raizen — echo of the
Bk2 hand-on-arm) holds. END HOOK: she chooses NOT to rule — and is left with the harder problem: how do you
fix a pressure without controlling anyone?

### Ch.13 — Balance, Not Victory · *essayistic→scene* · ~4,800
The real answer forms: not un-mending, not control, not defeating the Horrors — **BALANCE.** Acknowledge the
void as the necessary counterpart, *contain* the source's overflow, make the world sustainable. Research /
Moravian / the cavern. **Bella's-name thread** tempts (follow it down into the source for the answer — at what
cost?) [author's call: attempt + pay, or hold]. END HOOK: the cost of balance becomes clear, and it is terrible
and personal.

### Ch.14 — The Brink · *accelerated chronological* · ~4,900 · action
The largest Horror incursion yet, converging with the human conflict; major near-loss; the world at the edge.
The wholesale-Death-symbol temptation (seal every portal / close the source) returns — but that edges toward
*killing magic again*, the council's crime in a new coat. She refuses the easy monstrous fix. END HOOK: she
commits to balance despite what it will take from her.

### Ch.15 — What It Will Take · *mosaic* (gathering) · ~4,800 · END OF ACT TWO
Gather what balance needs: Drake + the Death symbol's true use (closing not a door but a *pressure*), the
cavern, **Raizen at the far end of the True-Bond** (braced, as in Bk2 Ch.18), maybe the **pact / the Dangris**
(resolve the open question — the ancient human–beast pact answers the call again), Alice/Amber. The personal
cost named on-page. END HOOK: they go to do it — witnessed this time, not alone in the dark (lesson from Bk2).

## ACT THREE — BALANCE (Ch.16–22) · the working, the cost, the changed world, the void

### Ch.16 — The Approach · *chronological / action* · ~4,800
The approach to the source; obstacles as the human + Horror threats converge on the cavern; Marick/Fen thread
re-intersects (Marick's last working, or his end; Fen surviving). The found-family holds the line so she can
reach the pedestal. END HOOK: she sets her hand to the source for the last working.

### Ch.17 — The Weight, Taken Up · *fragmented→whole* · ~5,000 · the working begins
Establishing balance at the source-cavern — the rebirth's counterpart, but **WITNESSED** and **her hand the
proximate cause** (the Bk2 climax-agency lesson baked in): she contains the source's overflow and acknowledges
the void as counterpart rather than sealing it. Render as enacted struggle (Raizen the anchor, as Ch.18 of Bk2).
END HOOK: balance starts to hold — and the cost lands.

### Ch.18 — The Cost · *interior* · ~4,800
What balance takes (author to finalize; architect proposal: she becomes the permanent *living throttle* of
the source — tethered to it, the "weight of the source" made literal: she will feel every birth and death for
as long as she lives, and the True-Bond means that is a very long time). The void acknowledged, not defeated.
Grief inward. END HOOK: the Horrors begin to recede — pulled back as the differential settles, not killed.

### Ch.19 — What the World Keeps · *chronological, quieter* · ~4,800 · wonder + grief
Balance established; the world changed, not fixed. The Horrors gone quiet (the pressure eased). Wonder + grief
in the Bk2 Ch.19 register. The human/political world begins, slowly, to find a new shape no one designed —
people learning to live whole and ungoverned. END HOOK: the cost is permanent and hers; she carries it.

### Ch.20 — The New Shape · *mosaic* · ~4,800
Aftermath across the found-family and the world: Alice fully a healer again (the warmth back, the crooked
tooth); Amber; the leaderless world not fixed but *sustainable*; the pact/Dangris resolved; goodbyes and
stayings. Viridia holds enormous power and chooses, plainly, not to rule — the only thing that keeps her
herself. END HOOK: the long view opens.

### Ch.21 — The Long View · *interior / quiet* · ~4,800
Viridia + Raizen take the long view: the **True-Bond means they outlive almost everyone they love** — a strange,
heavy thing, carried together. What she has become and whether she is still herself (the series' final personal
answer: yes, *because* she chooses restraint daily). The balance held; the world borrowed, not owned. END HOOK:
quiet — almost an ending.

### Ch.22 — A Place Where a Core Should Be · *chronological→cut* · ~4,700 · FINALE
The close of the trilogy: the world, changed and sustainable; the family facing forward. THEN the **binding
final image, set off and UNEXPLAINED:** far away, in a low stone laboratory, a tentacle snaps back through a
portal, **severed**; **a young man named Fen runs**; and Viridia, who can feel every core in existence, **feels
a void for the first time** — a place where a core should be and is not. She does not know what it is or where.
She only knows something exists that she cannot see. **Last line. Do not explain it. Let it sit.** (The first
thread of Masters of the Void.)
