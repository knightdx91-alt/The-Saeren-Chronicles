## RISK-FREE SURGICAL TWEAKS (no plot/canon change; optional, chapter already PASSES)
1. ¶59 "The camera of her wants to look away" — the literal word *camera* is a craft-doc term leaking into prose (it reads as authorial scaffolding, not Viridia's register). Swap to an in-world phrasing, e.g. "The part of her that wanted to look away from this, she did not let." Pure prose hygiene.
2. ¶113 is one ~190-word sentence. It is deliberate and it WORKS as enacted seduction — keep it long — but consider one internal full-stop near its midpoint to give the reader a single breath, lowering rhythm-gate risk without losing the run.
