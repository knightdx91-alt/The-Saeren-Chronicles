## Anaphora check (the locked ≤2 deliberate-beats caution)
Drake's voice is natively anaphoric (a six-thousand-year orator), which raises the count. Auditing for STACKED REFLECTIVE TRIPLETS (the banned form), not in-dialogue rhetorical cadence:
1. **"There is a warmth and there is a cold... There is a presence and there is an absence"** (l.59) — the cosmology's defining couplet→expansion. INTENTIONAL, in-dialogue, load-bearing.
2. **"You did not make the cold. You did not call it. You made the warmth whole"** (l.87) — Drake's exoneration cadence. INTENTIONAL, in-dialogue.
The "and the more uneven the breath the harder the cold leans" escalation (l.87) is a single accelerating sentence (deliberate feedback-loop rendering), not a stacked triplet. **No banned stacked reflective triplet in NARRATION.** The anaphora that exists is voiced by a character whose register is anaphoric — within the sanctioned exception. Caution honored, but note Drake's cadence is the densest anaphora-source in the book so far; keep an eye on cumulative dragon-speech across Act Two so it does not become a tic across chapters.

## Character — Drake & Varissa (the assigned question): themselves, not generic wise-mentors? Cosmology through character, not lecture?
**YES on identity; QUALIFIED-YES on through-character delivery — the qualification IS the soft seam.**

**Varissa is the chapter's highest achievement and the strongest guarantor that this is not a sage-lecture.** She is unmistakably the Book Two antagonist-in-law: fierce-now, no centuries-patience, "I am not Drake and I do not weep my truths out over three days, I say them once and mean them." Her arc in-chapter is specific and ungeneric — the confession that she hated Viridia "for a year for being a thing that ends," undone by the rebirth into "a daughter-in-law I cannot kill in my heart anymore. It is very inconvenient." Her "you smell like a person... bread and cold and a woman who is not your kind" is a grief rendered through the nose, not the intellect. She delivers the SECOND cosmological beat (the dragons felt the breath shift a year ago) — so the cosmology is split across two distinct dragon-voices, not monologued by one sage, AND it arrives through felt experience ("the lung drawing too full"), not abstraction. This is the antidote to lecture and it works.

**Drake is wisdom WITH a wound, not a dispenser.** He is the Bk2 Ch.8 voice exactly: centuries-slow, refuses to soften, charges grief as the fee. The chapter keeps him a person by (a) anchoring every cosmological point to his DEAD DAUGHTER and his hundred failed door-closings, (b) his confessed impotence ("the evening of the breath is past me... the size of what I cannot do"), and (c) the homecoming tenderness toward Raizen ("Come here, rabbit. Let your mother see your ridiculous brown eyes"). The cosmology is delivered as a grieving father's hard-won bill, not a textbook.

**The qualification:** the chapter is near-wall-to-wall Drake-speech in its middle third (l.59, l.83, l.87, l.107, l.111), and those passages lean toward sustained exposition-through-monologue. It is RESCUED — by the daughter-anchor, the bounded authority, the Varissa split, and the frame texture — but the lecture risk is real and is precisely why Prose holds at 8.5 rather than rising. The delivery is character-FILTERED throughout; it is not always character-DRAMATIZED in the middle (Drake mostly talks; Viridia mostly receives). That is defensible for a consultation beat by an elder, and the single-line interjections ("The pressure," Viridia said. / "Forever," Viridia said.) keep her present — but one more beat of Viridia ACTING-against rather than receiving (see notes) would close the seam.

**Cover-the-name: PASS.** Four distinct voices: Drake (long, centuries-down, "child"), Varissa (flat, fierce, "girl," appalled-fact), Raizen (quiet, low, "only for her," the bond-carrier), Viridia (stripped, level, the cold working part, will-not-finish-the-sentence).
