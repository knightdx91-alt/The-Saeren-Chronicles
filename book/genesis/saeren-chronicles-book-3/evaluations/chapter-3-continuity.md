  of the ones who can't be trusted" / "hold it for them" — i.e. re-sever selectively. This is
  the council's original crime inverted and is thematically exactly the temptation the book is

- **Amber's question** is correctly attributed as Amber's ("how do you tell the difference,
  from the inside, between you and them," L95) — matches Book Two Ch.20 / Book Three Ch.1.

- **The "soured milk / cracked cup / deaths with no wound."** Consistent with Ch.1's lesson
  scene (the dark half souring milk, cracking cups) and Ch.1–2's drained-core deaths. Note the
  in-world distinction is preserved: human-on-human dark-half misuse ("deaths with no wound,"
  ordinary ungoverned power) is kept SEPARATE from the Horror/tear drainings of Ch.1–2. Ch.3
  does not conflate the political crisis with the cosmic-horror tear thread — correct.

## MINOR (polish-level, non-blocking; no fix required to proceed)

- **[MINOR-1] Capital-collapse cadence: "over a year" vs "a year ago."** L23 says the capital
  "over a year ... *stopped*," and the rebirth/waking-whole is "a year ago." Both round to
  ~one year, which is fine, but "over a year" for the stopping sits a hair after the
  "a year ago" waking. Reads naturally (the slow stopping postdates the instant waking), so no
  change needed; flagged only so a later pass doesn't sharpen one number without the other.
  *Suggested (optional) fix:* leave as-is, or make it "across the year" to match Ch.1's idiom.

- **[MINOR-2] "across a winter of stale papers" (L21) implies one winter has passed in the
  valley.** Ch.1 says the almond tree "flowered twice since they came to the valley" (two
  springs), implying the valley life spans more than a single winter. Not a contradiction —
  "a winter" can mean the most recent one in which she did this reading — but if a future
  chapter pins the valley tenure precisely, ensure "a winter of stale papers" reads as *the*
  recent winter, not the total tenure. *Suggested (optional) fix:* none needed; note for the
  timeline ledger.
