## OUTLINE COMPLIANCE (the assigned beat — "### Ch.10 — Absence Drawn to Presence, ~50% GUT-PUNCH")
Hits the beat and its stated craft constraint exactly. With her sight come back whole AT THE SOURCE (l.5–7, the physical Book One cavern matched: "the low rough chamber and the unworked walls and the pedestal with its pool of pale even light that was not water"), she does the complete accounting and the source confirms what was already named: the Horrors are pulled toward the source the way absence is drawn to presence; the rebirth ran the lung full and widened the differential; she made this. Rendered as the assigned "overwhelm resolving into one terrible clarity (the Bk2 Ch.18 counterpart)" — the sight gives her "every line one at a time until she could not hold them," then "last, the shape they all made together" (l.65), and the shape is "the exact shape and the exact size of the mercy she had done. Line for line" (l.71). END HOOK delivers verbatim to spec: "the cure had a cost no one could see... and it has her name on it. All the way down. Felt, now, and not merely known" (l.103, l.105).

## THE KEY JUDGMENT — does the witnessed-by-Raizen-alone break INCREASE impact without cheapening it; is it earned, restrained, singular, and a genuine MIRROR (not a repeat) to Bk2 Ch.16; does it still read as recognizably Viridia?
**YES on all five. This is the chapter's highest achievement and the strongest reason it clears the gut-punch bar.**

**Earned.** The break does not arrive cold. It is the third standing in this room (rebirth → blind cost → this), it follows a year of the small count "battened down to the doors she could shut" (l.15), it follows the said-aloud confession to Drake on the rock (Ch.9), and it lands only after the full enacted accounting (Sela → the eleven → the bottom under the count → the inside-out shape). The reader has watched the wall hold across two and a half books; the single inch given here is paid for by all of it.

**Restrained — and this is the craft.** The break is explicitly NOT weeping. "She did not weep" is stated plainly, twice, and the reason is canon-exact: "the not-weeping was not for the room, it had never been for the room... it was the shape of her, the wall she had made of herself a long time ago" (l.37). What she gives Raizen is "a small thing, the kind of thing that in another person would have been the catch of a breath, the giving of a single inch... no wail, no folding, no sound at all" (l.83). The break is a crack down the bond, not a collapse. **Restraint even in the breaking** — confirmed. This is the singular correct rendering for this character: a lesser draft would have had her sob into Raizen's chest and it would have broken her. This does not.

**Singular and a genuine MIRROR, not a repeat.** I verified against Bk2 Ch.16 directly. Ch.16: public ("let the whole grey world look at her grief"), torn out of her with no choosing ("the careful warning... did not surface... because the part of her that kept the warning was the part that had broken"). Ch.10 inverts every axis: **private** (Raizen alone, down the bond, "the one place no one else would ever stand," l.83), **chosen** ("At Jazen's death it had been torn out of her... with no choosing in it. This was the other thing... this she chose," l.83), and **controlled** (the front of her face holds for the room even though there is no room; only the bond-side is let go). The text names the mirror explicitly so the reader who carries Ch.16 feels the deliberate counterpart. It is the SAME woman doing the OPPOSITE thing for the opposite reason — the war tore it out of her in public; here, in safety, she chooses to let one person in. That is character growth rendered as inversion, not a second helping of the same beat. The guardrail (two sanctioned breaks only, the second private-to-Raizen, no third) is honored precisely.

**Does it increase impact vs a fully-inward version?** Yes, measurably, and without cheapening. A fully-inward Ch.10 would have repeated the Ch.6/Ch.9 move (grief held, bond thinned to a wire) and the reader would have felt the beat plateau at the exact moment it must peak. By letting the wall come apart on the bond-side only — "she had kept them the same all her life, flat to both, and now, this once, she let the two come apart" (l.83) — the chapter gives the midpoint a genuine first-time event (the thing she has never done) WITHOUT violating the no-public-weeping canon. The relief is real (Raizen "held it with her, so that for the first time the holding was not only hers," l.39) and load-bearing (the YA hope/connection guardrail is served at the darkest beat). It does not cheapen because nothing is resolved by it — the seeing "did not lessen by a grain" (l.65); the break buys her company in the weight, not escape from it.

**Recognizably Viridia: confirmed.** The cold working part, the bins, the wall, the wiping of the face "for no one" out of "the old habit" (l.99), the refusal to sink to the floor because "her body had a wall to be and the wall did not fold" (l.77), the bad coffee thought intruding (l.97) — every tic of her established interiority is intact. The break is the most Viridia thing in the chapter precisely because it is so small and so guarded.

## TECHNIQUE & GUARDRAIL CONFIRMATIONS (assigned checks)
- **Enacted overwhelm, NOT asserted.** Confirmed. The overwhelm is dramatized through the sight-mechanics: the bins reaching for a number and finding faces ("you cannot put a face in a bin, you can only see it," l.29); the eleven given "not as a number, *eleven*... but as eleven, as the eleven, each one a place in the weave" (l.31); the resolution into one shape rendered as a felt event, not stated. Crucially the chapter pre-empts the cheap version on the page: "the books all get it wrong, that the overwhelm breaks like a fever and you feel better. It did not break" (l.65). This is the Bk2 Ch.18 lesson (enact, don't assert) executed cleanly.
- **No axis re-reveal-as-information.** Confirmed. The fact is explicitly framed as already-known: "She had known it. What changed was that now she could see the line" (l.19–21). The chapter's new content is the EMOTIONAL/MORAL landing (sum→sight; known→felt) and the inside-out shape ("drawn inside out, the shape of the new wound," l.71) — not a fresh plot reveal. Correct.
- **YA camera-cut on deaths.** Confirmed. Sela and the eleven are rendered as absences in the weave ("a snuffed wick," "small dark absences," an old man / a child / a quick young woman read as emptied places), never as on-page dying. The horror lives in the tracing, not the viscera. Within YA register.
- **NO ONE but Raizen witnesses the break.** Confirmed. There is no other character in the cavern; the others are "three valleys and a portal away" (l.39). The break goes "down the bond instead of into the air" (l.83). The room "would never have her" (l.39). No third party, no third break.

## Anaphora check (the locked ≤2 deliberate-beats caution; no flat reflective triplets)
The flood-syntax of overwhelm is natively anaphoric, which raises the count. Auditing for STACKED FLAT REFLECTIVE TRIPLETS in narration (the banned form), not enactment-anaphora:
1. **"There was Sela. There was the line from Sela to her hand. There was the next absence... and the next, and the next"** (l.29) — this is the closest to a triplet, BUT it is ENACTMENT (the bins failing to count, the list refusing to close) and it accelerates rather than reflects — a deliberate overwhelm-render, not a reflective triplet. Borderline; judged within the enactment exception because it is doing dramatic work (the count that won't stop), not landing three parallel reflections.
2. **"no mark... no master... no end"** (echoing Ch.9's Drake-cadence) appears once in narration (l.103) — a single rhetorical run, not stacked.
The "the line, the line, the line. The sight would not stop giving her the line" (l.43) is a deliberate incantatory repetition (the obsessive return), not a reflective triplet. **No banned flat reflective triplet.** The repetition that exists is enactment of obsessive overwhelm, within the sanctioned exception. Note: l.29's "and the next, and the next" is the one place to watch on a style pass — it is defensible but it is the chapter's most triplet-adjacent narration beat.
