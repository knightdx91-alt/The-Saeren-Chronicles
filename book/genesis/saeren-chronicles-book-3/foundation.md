## 1. PREMISE (structural)

About a year after the rebirth, **Viridia Saeren — sixteen, the living vessel of the source,
her mana-sight returning a piece at a time** — must hold together a world she fundamentally
remade: the light-supremacist order has collapsed into chaos, everyone now carries both halves
of the gift and almost no one knows what to do with it, the capital is leaderless, and unstable
portals are tearing open across the world, leaking **Horrors** that drain the life from anything
they touch. The gut-punch she will have to live with: **the Horrors are not attacking. They are
being pulled toward the source the way absence is drawn to presence.** The rebirth created a
pressure differential between this world and the void. In saving the world, she made its next
threat — and the only person who can feel the whole shape of it is the one who caused it.

**Irony engine (the series' final turn):** Book One asked *who am I*; Book Two asked *do I have the
right to remake everyone*. Book Three asks the consequence of having done it: **the cure had a
cost no one could see, and now it is here, and it has her name on it.** She fought a regime that
controlled people through their magic. She can now feel every core alive — every birth, every
death, every act of violence. The power to *protect* the world is one keystroke from the power to
*control* it, and she knows it, and the book never lets her off that hook.

**Escalation ladder (maps to turning points):**
- ~10% (Ch.1–2): the unsettled new world + the FIRST Horror through a portal; her sight, returning,
  reads something wrong at the rim it could not read a year ago.
- ~25% (Ch.5–6): the Horrors are spreading, and the political vacuum is arguably the worse danger;
  she is pulled toward using her power to impose order — the temptation named.
- ~50% (Ch.10–11): **THE GUT-PUNCH** — she works out (and the source confirms) that the Horrors are
  *pulled toward her*; the rebirth is the cause; she made this. Everything reframes.
- ~70% (Ch.14–16): the cost of stopping it is monstrous (un-mending? closing the source? sealing the
  void with the Death symbol and what that would take); the temptation to "fix" the world by control
  peaks; she nearly becomes the thing she fought.
- Climax (Ch.17–21): not a defeat of the Horrors but the establishment of **BALANCE** — source
  contained, void acknowledged as the necessary counterpart; the world made sustainable, not fixed.
- Resolution + final image (Ch.22): the long view with Raizen (they outlive almost everyone); then,
  in the lab, **Fen runs**, and **Viridia feels a void for the first time** — a place where a core
  should be and is not. Do NOT explain it. (Thread to Masters of the Void.)

**The question (book/series spine):** *Can Viridia protect a world she fundamentally altered without
becoming the very thing she fought against — and is she still herself when it is done?*

## 2. THEME
Division is artificial and harmful — but so is forced unity. Book One: the severing was violence.
Book Two: undoing it without asking was its own kind of violence she chose to own. Book Three:
**balance, not victory.** The void is not evil; it is the counterpart to the source, the way dark is
the counterpart to light. The resolution is not to win but to let the world be *both*, sustainably —
and for Viridia to hold enormous power and choose, daily, NOT to use it to control, which is the only
thing that keeps her from becoming the council that cut the world. Found-family + the long-life weight
of the True-Bond are the load-bearing warmth between the cosmic-dread beats.

## 4. MAGIC / WORLD STATE (post-rebirth — per the reconciliation note)
- **Everyone now carries both halves of the gift** (the severing is mended). Almost no one is trained
  for the dark half; the world is full of people who suddenly can do things they don't understand —
  source of both wonder and disaster (and of Marick).
- **Viridia is the source-vessel.** Connected to every living core; her always-on mana-SIGHT was spent
  in the rebirth and **returns slowly across the year** (present but not yet whole at open — and the
  re-acquisition is how she first reads the widened rim).
- **The Death symbol closes any portal / ends any magic** (Drake taught it, Bk2). Since the Horrors
  come through portals, this is the tactical spine — but it is *easy and asks nothing*, which is its
  danger; using it to seal portals wholesale edges toward the "control" line.
- **The void:** abstract counterpart to the source (stillness/separation/absence). The rebirth ran the
  weave full and widened the source/void **pressure differential** (Bk2 Ch.20). **NO entity, NO void
  woman** anywhere in this trilogy. The Horrors are void-stuff drawn through unstable portals toward
  the fuller-than-ever source.
- **Portals** require both halves; done wrong (no proper Moravian) they risk tearing the veil — which
  is exactly what newly-whole untrained mages (Marick) now do.
- **The source = the physical Book One cavern** (pedestal + pool, reached by seam). Any return matches
  that prose.
- **Bella's-name thread** runs down into the source, unfollowable; held since Bk2 — a Book-Three
  personal thread (does the source-vessel finally get to follow it? — a temptation/cost, author's call).
