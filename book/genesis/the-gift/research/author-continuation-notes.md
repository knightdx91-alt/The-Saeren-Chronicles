## Status of existing prose
- Chapter 1 is written (~2,780 words): Troy, 17, 6'11", pale blond, X-Men–obsessed genetics
  prodigy, uses CRISPR to find and (it's implied) experiment on the "mutant gene" (X-17 m25)
  on himself. Chapter ends with him heading to a family dinner at the diner.

## Main characters (the three MCs)
- **Troy** — protagonist/POV (the narrator of Ch.1). Genetics prodigy; experiments on himself.
- **Raven** — Troy's girlfriend; gains the power to walk through walls (Kitty Pryde / phasing).
- **Knight** — Raven's brother; the serum turns his blood to electricity, eyes turn electric blue.

## Troy's family (the diner dinner — next chapter)
- They always have family dinners at the diner.
- **Mom** — a guardian ad litem.
- **Dad** — a mechanic who rides motorcycles; owns his own shop.
- **Sister** — very smart; has job offers (e.g. from a company like Intel; possibly government
  contracts in her future).
- After dinner Troy gets food to go and leaves to meet the other two MCs at the warehouse.

## The warehouse (origin of the team's powers)
- Run-down warehouse by the water; the three have been working on "this stuff" a long time.
- Troy meets Raven (girlfriend) and Knight (her brother) there.
- Raven is upset that Troy did the experiment on himself without calling them first.
- Knight laughs because Troy is in trouble.
- Troy gives them the serum one after another, so both are "down" (unconscious) during transformation.
- Raven wakes first; after a minute she reaches over to comfort Knight — he shocks her and she
  flies through the wall (first uncontrolled use of both their powers, played together).
- Some scenes follow; the sequence ends on a group photo: **"this is the start of the rest of
  our lives."** (This has been their plan ever since they realized it was possible.)

## TIME JUMP → the biotech company
- The three MCs now run a biotech company.
- Public face: gene editing — "designer babies," removing bad/genetic-disease genes, etc.
  (legitimate-sounding, lucrative work).
- The real purpose: all the work just FUNDS their true dream — **to seed new life on another
  planet.** They know people sent there couldn't survive as they currently are (hence powers).
- **Company slogan (public):** "A New World." **Full (hidden) slogan:** "A New World At Any Cost."

## The assistants (the choice)
- Opens with Troy interviewing a man for a personal-assistant position; Raven and Knight are
  interviewing too.
- Then a conference with all of them where the MCs reveal the company's REAL purpose to the new
  assistants, and give them a choice: take the serum (NOT knowing what it does) or leave.
- They choose to take it. Led down to the **3rd sub-level**, placed in separate rooms to await
  transformation (no one knows what powers/changes each will get).
- Meanwhile Troy heads home. Raven is moving in that night; she comes to dinner. Knight shows up
  later (he'd been running errands for their lawyer).

## The orphanages / the children (the dark engine)
- The MCs have been buying up orphanages — but they only want kids of certain ages (the most
  resilient).
- The kids are the BASE for the work: they'll be trained, and eventually given the serum.
- The kids are the ones meant to go to the new planet — the MCs found a planet in the Goldilocks
  (habitable) zone.
- The kids end up at a camp built far out in the wilderness — a training / "summer camp" that
  can be deadly.

## The government task force (antagonist clock)
- The government is investigating unethical genetic experiments.
- The man heading the task force was INSTRUCTED to leave the MCs' company alone...
- ...but his DAUGHTER died due to ANOTHER company's experiments, and since the MCs' company is
  one of the biggest in the field, he holds them guilty by association.
- He goes after Troy → it becomes a race against the clock.

## The escape tech (endgame)
- They have a **one-way teleporter — a portable wormhole.**
- Some MIT guys had been building one while Troy was there helping out; Troy later lured them
  (and others) to the company.
