# Outline — THE GIFT (Book One: A New World)

> 21 chapters. Targets sum to ~103,300 words (floor 95,000, target 100,000).
> POV per header. Turning points: TP1 ~Ch.5 (24%), TP2 ~Ch.10–11 (50%), TP3 ~Ch.16 (76%), TP4/climax ~Ch.20 (95%).
> Act breaks (epigraph device): before Ch.3, Ch.8, Ch.13, Ch.18.
> Ch.1 EXISTS (author's AO3 prose) — voice benchmark, do not rewrite from scratch; polish only.
> Powers described originally (phasing = "going thin"; bio-electric blood). NO Marvel IP in-world.

## ACT I — WONDER (Ch.1–7, ~origin to empire seed)

## Chapter 1: Today's the Day  — [TROY] — EXISTS
**Premise:** Troy wakes having synthesized X-17 m25, injects himself, and discovers his skin can't be pierced.
**Function:** Establishes voice, world, foster-wound, the science, the three-person plan; hooks toward the warehouse meeting.
**Emotional anchor:** the fountain pen that won't pierce his leg; "Today's the day."
**Emotional surprise:** he tests godhood by stabbing his own leg with a fountain pen — banal tool, cosmic result.
**Opening strategy:** Voice bomb + ordinary-made-strange (LOCKED as benchmark).
**Key beats:** wake/sticky note → CRISPR backstory → inject → pain/blackout → wake, scar gone, needle won't pierce → planner: Jacob 4pm, diner 6pm, warehouse 9pm → texts the group "I've got news."
**Progressive:** opens the whole book.
**Theme presence:** seeded (disposability → he'll never let his people be disposable).
**Structure:** Chronological, single-POV interiority.
**Word target:** ~2,780 (existing) — may pad to ~3,800 with the Jacob/MIT scene (currently a partial "Chapter 2" in the draft) folded in OR kept as Ch.1b; **Writer warning: preserve voice; do not over-correct grammar to sterility.**
**Beat subversion:** Genre expects the origin to be solemn/dangerous; here it's a sleepy teenager with a sticky note — the wrong, mundane register IS the device.

## Chapter 2: The Start of the Rest of Our Lives — [TROY] — ~5,200
**Premise:** Family diner dinner, then the warehouse origin: Troy serums Raven then Knight; their powers wake; they take the photo.
**Function:** Introduces the bio-family (warmth) and the chosen-family origin (the engine); births Raven's and Knight's powers; plants the dream.
**Emotional anchor:** Raven flying backward through the wall; the group photo line.
**Emotional surprise:** the most dangerous experiment of their lives is preceded by sibling bickering and Knight laughing that Troy's "in trouble."
**Key beats:** diner — Mom (GAL), Dad (failing shop), Cora (job offers); warm, funny, Cora saves him a seat. Troy gets food to go → warehouse by the water. Raven furious he self-experimented without calling. Knight laughs. Troy serums them one after another; both go down. Raven wakes, reaches to comfort Knight; he shocks her; she phases through the wall (first uncontrolled use of BOTH powers, together). Aftermath: wonder, fear, decision. Group photo: *"this is the start of the rest of our lives."*
**Progressive:** Builds on Ch.1's solo gift → now a trio. Adds: the dream is older than today. Opens toward: the company.
**Theme presence:** RECEDES at the diner (pure warmth) → returns hard at the warehouse.
**Chaos:** Troy's pen; Knight's first-shock memory is BORN here (will recur); Raven's hide-and-seek echo as she's lost in the wall.
**Subtext:** Raven's anger is fear of being left behind, not procedure. Surface: "you should've called." Real: "you nearly died without me."
**Shareable moment:** he gives the serum to the two people he loves before he knows it won't kill them.
**Reading speed:** diner decelerates (sensory, warm); warehouse transformation accelerates.
**Structure:** Chronological, two-scene.
**Writer warning:** romance heat OPEN-DOOR/MODERATE begins as tenderness here, not sex yet. Avoid "a wave of fear" — render Raven's terror physically (she can't feel the wall).
**Beat subversion:** super-origin expects triumphant power-discovery; instead the first use of powers HURTS the person he loves — the gift wounds on arrival.

## Chapter 5: The Choice — [TROY] — ~5,100  ★ TURNING POINT 1 (~24%)
**Premise:** Hiring the assistants; the conference where the MCs reveal the company's REAL purpose and offer the serum-or-leave choice; Priya leaves, Devon and Bull descend to sub-level 3.
**Function:** First irreversible moral threshold made on-page; establishes the "informed-but-blind consent" engine; the door motif.
**Emotional anchor:** the *click* of the door as Priya walks out while the others go down.
**Emotional surprise:** the one who refuses isn't punished or chased — she just leaves, and the silence is worse.
**Key beats:** Troy interviews Devon; Raven/Knight interview in parallel (briefly). The joint conference: the full pitch — "A New World," the planet, the powers, the price; the offer: take the serum (you don't get to know what it does) or walk and never speak of it. Priya walks (passes the test). Devon, Bull, others accept → sub-level 3, separate rooms, await transformation. Troy heads home; Raven moving in; Knight late (lawyer errands).
**Progressive:** pays Ch.4's hiring; opens the human-experimentation thread and Book-Two transformations.
**Theme presence:** central.
**Structure:** Parallel (interviews) → single big scene (conference) → quiet domestic coda.
**Shareable moment:** "take the shot, you don't get to know what it does, or leave forever" — and one person leaves.
**Subtext:** the conference is a recruitment cloaked as honesty; "we're telling you the truth" is itself the manipulation.
**Re-read seed:** Priya's exit = the only one who's right.
**Writer warning:** keep the MCs CHARMING here — the horror is that the pitch is genuinely inspiring.
**Beat subversion:** the cult-recruitment beat expects a holdout to be silenced/threatened; here the holdout simply, safely leaves — making the others' choice feel free and therefore worse.

**Emotional surprise:** Knight, the muscle, tears up at the cured kid — and then immediately checks his game leaderboard.
**Key beats:** public launch; the cure that's genuinely miraculous; investor/government attention (Cora's world brushes theirs); slogan "A New World" unveiled publicly; private, Knight and Troy share the hidden full version: *"At Any Cost."* Knight's true-believer interior.
**Theme presence:** present (the good as alibi).
**Structure:** Collage (press copy + scene + Knight interiority) — uses the epigraph device.
**Chaos:** Knight's game; mind-reading distortion ("everyone here would do what we're doing if they had the guts").
**Cultural-vocab:** public "A New World" planted; full slogan first spoken.
**Beat subversion:** the corporate-launch beat expects hollow PR; here the miracle is REAL, which implicates the reader who cheered.
