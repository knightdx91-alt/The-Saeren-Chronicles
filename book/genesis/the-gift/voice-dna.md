## 1. GLOBAL NARRATIVE VOICE

**Derived from Ch.1.** The book has TWO registers by POV form (see foundation §MULTI-POV):
- **Troy chapters = first-person, present-tense-leaning, warm.** This IS the benchmark. Preserve it.
- **Raven / Knight / Vance chapters = close-third, past tense.** Tighter, more controlled, judgmental distance.

**Vocabulary level:** 5 overall (Troy bright-colloquial; Vance 6, drier; Knight 4, profane; Raven 5, clipped).
**Sentence rhythm:** Troy = run-on enthusiasm, clauses chained with "and/so/which," occasional sentence fragments of wonder. Others = mixed/varied, shorter under tension.
**Formality:** Conversational (Troy/Knight), Measured (Raven/Vance).
**Humor:** Frequent/self-deprecating (Troy), profane-deflecting (Knight), dry (Raven), near-none-then-cracking (Vance).
**Emotional register:** Open (Troy), Restrained (Raven/Vance), Warm-then-flat (Knight).
**Distinctive feature (the book's signature):** Troy narrates atrocity in the same eager, delighted, slightly-rambling voice he narrates wonder. The reader's dread comes from the GAP between his tone and his acts. Never let Troy's narration sound like it knows it's doing wrong — it doesn't.
**Anti-patterns (never):** analytical/explanatory similes; "a wave of [emotion] washed over"; metacognitive narration ("she realized she was realizing"); adverb-laden dialogue tags; em-dash overuse; Marvel/trademark terms in-world (phasing = "going thin," "unstitching," never a brand name).

### VOICE UNDER PRESSURE
- **Troy:** enthusiasm hardens into JUSTIFICATION — sentences lengthen, reasons stack ("and besides, and anyway, and what's the alternative"), the comic-book references DROP OUT. The disappearance of joy is how the reader sees the lie.
- **Raven:** goes MORE clipped; asks questions she won't answer; involuntary phasing renders as a dropped beat / white space / a half-finished line. Touches a wall to make herself solid.
- **Knight:** the jokes STOP; sentences go short and military-precise; electricity arcs into the description (sparks, the smell of ozone) when control fails.
- **Vance:** bureaucratic control TIGHTENS then snaps — he addresses Ellie directly mid-paragraph ("you'd have liked the crossword today, El").

## 2. PER-CHARACTER VOICE CARDS

#### Troy Woods (first-person POV)
- **Vocabulary band:** self-taught genius + comics fandom + foster-kid plainness; science explained joyfully, never condescendingly; pop-culture references.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** spirals — long, comma-and-"so"-spliced sentences when excited; clean short ones when scared.
- **Rhythm:** statements that tip into wonder; rhetorical "I'll be damned" asides.
- **Verbal tics:** "Today's the day"; "I'll be damned"; calling things "my kind of place"; quoting comics.
- **Never says:** clinical detachment about people he loves; he never admits a moral cost in plain words (he reroutes to "the bigger picture").
- **Sample line:** "I gave the two people I love a needle full of maybe, and the worst part is I'd do it again, gladly, today."

#### Raven (close-third)
- **Vocabulary band:** operational, legal-adjacent, precise; sensory when stressed (citrus).
- **Syntax fingerprint:** clips; ends arguments with a question; counts things.
- **Rhythm:** fragments under pressure; short declaratives.
- **Verbal tics:** "Are we sure?"; counting ("that's four, Troy, four"); peeling an orange while she talks.
- **Never says:** sentimental reassurance; she won't lie to comfort.
- **Sample line:** "You keep saying we're saving them. Say the other word. Say what we're doing to them."

#### Knight (close-third)
- **Vocabulary band:** street-warm, profane, gamer slang, gentle with kids.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** loose and loud, then suddenly short and precise when he's about to do something terrible.
- **Rhythm:** jokes as punctuation; the joke-drop signals danger.
- **Verbal tics:** sarcastic "boss"; "everybody here knows it, they're just scared to say it"; leaderboard rage.
- **Never says:** doubt out loud (his lie is that doubt = betrayal).
- **Sample line:** "Don't look at me like that. Somebody's gotta make the call the lovebirds can't, and lucky me, I sleep fine."

#### Marcus Vance (close-third)
- **Vocabulary band:** federal/procedural, understated, cracking at grief.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** checklist cadence; understatement; one tender clause smuggled into a dry paragraph.
- **Rhythm:** controlled, then a direct address to Ellie that breaks the meter.
- **Verbal tics:** crossword references; "per the file"; calling his daughter "El."
- **Never says:** he never names his real motive (revenge by association); he calls it "the work."
- **Sample line:** "Per the file, the company did nothing wrong. Per the glovebox, my daughter is still nine."

## 4. ANTI-PATTERN BUDGET (Thriller, per 1,000 words; style gate is the ceiling)
- Explanatory/analytical similes: **≤4/1k** (style gate); aim ≤3.
- Em-dashes: **≤4 per chapter, ABSOLUTE** (style gate) — prefer periods/commas; this is the hardest cap, watch it.
- "It was as if / almost as though": ≤1 per chapter.
- Metacognitive narration ("she realized she was realizing"): ≤1 per chapter (0 preferred).
- Emotional temperature reports ("a wave of X washed over"): **0** — render emotion physically (Raven can't feel the wall; Knight's sparks; Vance's grocery aisle).
- Adverbs in dialogue tags: near-zero.
- In-world trademark terms: **0** (phasing = "going thin"; no Marvel names).
