## Ch.3-Deviation Judgment (the "exactly my age" beat pulled forward)
The writer moved the Ch.3 outline anchor (the photograph of Fenn "looking exactly her age") to Ch.2's final line as the page-turn pull. **This works, and is the higher-value placement.**
- **Why it works here:** Ch.2 needed a stronger closing pull than "she'll follow the coordinates," and the photograph is the chapter's best gasp. Crucially, the writer EARNED it inside Ch.2 by making Mara refuse the photograph's sale ("The photograph stays in... the man without his face is—") and then deliberately not-look at it until the bench — so the reveal is the chapter's emotional climax, not a borrowed beat. The "exactly my age" line also quietly seeds the cautionary-mirror theme (Fenn = who Mara becomes if she never chooses) at the perfect moment.
- **Does it leave Ch.3 enough?** Yes, but Ch.3 must be re-anchored. Ch.3's job (per outline: decode the notes, the isolation, the fieldwork ritual, travel, the threshold's cold air) is intact and the chapter is not gutted — but its DESIGNATED emotional anchor is now spent. **Action for Ch.3:** promote one of its existing beats to anchor. Strongest candidates already in the outline: (a) the fieldwork ritual / re-read seed #4 (loading the film camera, the wristwatch, the father's slides) rendered as the anchor — it is unique to Ch.3 and load-bearing for the whole book's climax; or (b) the threshold-place's cold air / boring-ruin subversion. Recommend (a): make the kit-ritual the Ch.3 anchor and let the photograph's "exactly my age" dread COLOR Ch.3 (she reads the mad notes now haunted by a face), which converts the borrowed beat into a Ch.3 asset rather than a loss. Log this as a standing note so the Ch.3 writer doesn't re-anchor on the now-spent photograph.
