## 1. PREMISE

**Structural premise (one sentence):**
A grieving modern woman who inherited her father's hunger for the ancient world walks
through a door she thought led to a dead library and into the living Roman past — and
must spend nine years building the family and the love she always longed for, only to
learn at the end that the door home will only take *her*, forcing a choice between her own
survival and the people she made, as Vesuvius buries the only world they can ever share.

**The irony engine (the structural contradiction that generates conflict by itself):**
She crosses time to *recover* a lost past (her father's, the ancient world's) — and in
doing so *builds* a present so real that the way home becomes the thing she most has to
fear. The map promised her the dead. It gave her the living. The door that saved her is
the door that will cost her everything. **The thing she searched for and the thing she
must lose are the same thing.**

**Concept Pitch Test:** PASS — "An antique map opens a door into ancient Rome, where she
marries a disgraced gladiator and raises a family — until she learns the way home only
works for her, and Vesuvius is about to erupt." Instantly shareable; the final clause does
the work. Risk flag: the premise is *long-fuse* (nine years), so each act must carry its
own engine, not coast on the climax.

**The central question (only the last page may answer):**
**Would you give up the world you came from to keep the world you made — knowing you can't
keep both?** (Stay vs. go. The eruption only forces the timing; the question is older than
the volcano.)

**Engagement Type:**
```
engagement_type:
  primary:   empathy (55%)        # we feel her love, her grief, her terror — and the children's
  secondary: fascination (30%)    # the time-door mystery, the vanished hunter, "the WHEN", real history
  tertiary:  self-insertion (15%) # wish-fulfillment of falling backward into history + the gladiator
```
**Tension to manage:** Empathy wants slow intimacy; fascination wants the mystery to keep
pulling. Resolution: the mystery (hunter, the WHEN, what the doors really are) is *braided
through* the love story, never paused for. Every clue lands inside an intimate scene, so the
two engines fire on the same beat instead of competing for chapters.

## 2. THEME AS QUESTION

**Primary:** *Is a chosen love worth more than the life you were born into — or does
choosing one always mean burying the other alive?*

**Secondary (braided):** *What do we owe the dead — and is honoring a dead father's love
of the past a way of living, or a way of refusing your own present?* (Her arc is learning
that the past she worshipped as a relic is, in fact, a place where she finally becomes a
person rather than a curator of someone else's grief.)

**Theme presence rule:** Present in ~38 of 48 chapters. RECEDES deliberately in the pure-
plot/survival chapters (the brigand road, the port-city con, the eruption-mechanics
chapters) where action and dread take the wheel.

**CHARACTER CHAOS (full — Romance/literary-leaning lead, 4/4):**
- **Irrelevant obsession:** Bread. She has *opinions* about bread — crumb, crust, hydration —

- **Failed emotional management:** She laughs — genuinely, horribly — at a funeral in Greece for

**CHARACTER CHAOS (full, 4/4):**

- **Surface:** Not the white-robed cliché — by 70 AD Delphi is in genteel decline. She is a

- **Need:** To risk one clear true thing.

- **Voice markers:** Speaks in true sentences disguised as riddles; mundane between visions

- **Contradiction:** The mouthpiece of a god who privately doesn't believe — until she does.

**CHARACTER CHAOS (lighter — appears ~6 chapters, 3/4):**

- **Ch.12 (the Oracle):** Anchor — "I can tell you the door. I will not tell you the hour." The

- **Ch.24 (marriage, midpoint):** Anchor — Valens re-tying her sandal at the wedding because his

- **Ch.36 (the WHEN / family can't pass):** Anchor — Fenn's flat old voice: "It only ever opens

- **Ch.43 (eruption onset):** Anchor — ash on the children's eyelashes; Aelia thinks it's snow and

- **Final (the choice):** Anchor — *the door, open, the light of the future on one side and her
  daughter's hand on the other.* Earned by every smaller "two things you can't both keep" beat seeded
  throughout.

### 4b. SHAREABLE MOMENTS (mapped ~every 6 chapters)
1. (Ch.6) "She followed a map to a dead library and walked into a living world."
2. (Ch.12) "The Oracle tells her where the door home is — but refuses to say *when* it opens."
3. (Ch.18–24) "She marries the gladiator the empire tried to erase."
4. (Ch.36) "The way home only works for the one it brought. Her husband and children can never pass."
5. (Ch.43) "Vesuvius starts to erupt the same week the door finally opens."
6. (Final) "She has one minute and two worlds and can only keep one."

### 4c. EMOTIONAL RESIDUE (designed backward from the ending)
The reader, ten minutes after the last page, should be **unable to decide whether she chose
right — and should catch themselves grieving people who never existed as if they had.** Not
"satisfied." *Implicated.* The final chapter must withhold authorial verdict.
**[PROPOSAL — the ending, with recommendation]:** She **STAYS**, and the book's last movement is
not the death but the *choice* — she lets the door close with her on the wrong/right side, and the
final pages are the family in the ash, the click of the glasses now her own. Recommended because
the irony engine demands it: a book about a woman who couldn't stop returning to the past must end
with her *choosing the present she built over the return she worshipped.* Going home would be the
genre-standard "survives, heartbroken" beat (Outlander-adjacent readers expect the impossible
reunion later); staying is the braver, more devastating, more *original* landing — and it makes the
whole nine-year build pay. **Alt (flagged):** she sends the children through somehow and stays alone
(requires bending the "only the one it brought" rule — NOT recommended, breaks the established law).
**Hard requirement either way:** the choice must be *hers and active*, not the volcano deciding for her.

### 4d. CULTURAL VOCABULARY
**"The one it brought."** The law of the door, stated by Fenn, then by Mara, then carved (re-read)
into the lintel where she first crossed. By the end the reader should use it for any door that only
opens one way. Introduced Ch.36; reinforced Ch.40, Ch.44, final.

### 4e. IDENTITY EFFECT
Designed beat ~every 6 chapters that lets the reader feel: *"I am someone who would have stayed"* —
or, more honestly, *"I am brave enough to admit I don't know if I would."* The book flatters the
reader's capacity for love at cost, not their cleverness.

### 4f. RE-READ ARCHITECTURE (seeds)
1. **The Oracle's Ch.12 line** ("you will lose nothing you keep, and keep nothing you can carry")
   reads as riddle-noise first time; second time it is the *exact* law of the door — she warned her.
2. **Fenn's marginalia in Ch.2** ("the map is honest, the door is not") is dismissed as a crank's
   note; post-Ch.36 it is the whole tragedy in six words.
3. **Valens counts the children** (a tic) every time they're all together — Ch.20 onward; on re-read
   it's a man rehearsing the arithmetic of who he can save.
4. **Mara's dead phone** kept charged-then-dead as a relic; the last time she checks it (Ch.42) the
   date stamp is the eruption date — she's been carrying the WHEN in her pocket the whole book.
5. **Aelia's "snow"** (Ch.43) — first read: a child's sweetness in horror. Re-read: the reader knows
   from page one (history) it was always going to be snow that wasn't snow.

## 5. SYMBOL & MOTIF SYSTEM
*(≥30% of detail must remain TEXTURE — not every object is a symbol.)*

**The Map / the door-as-threshold**
- First: Ch.2, antique shop, dismissed as a forgery that's "too accurate." Evolution: tool → trap →
  law → grave-marker. Final: the lintel she crosses bears the same coordinate-mark; it's a tombstone.
- Theme link: the thing that promised the dead delivered the living, then demanded a death.

**Reading glasses / the *click*** (auditory motif — varies the visual-heavy anchors)
- First: Ch.1 (father's). Evolution: grief-sound → marriage-sound (Valens has none; she misses the
  ritual) → her own, at the end. Theme link: who we keep by keeping their small sounds.

**Bread / yeast / the living culture**
- First: Ch.1 (a starter she let die when he died). Evolution: refuses to bake → learns Roman bread →
  teaches Aelia. Final: bread rising in a kitchen the ash will take. Theme link: the past is not a
  relic to preserve but a culture you have to *feed* to keep alive — the opposite of curating.

**Ash / preservation** (the cruel irony motif)
- First: dust on museum artifacts, Ch.1. Evolution: the conservator who preserves the dead becomes the
  *preserved* — Pompeii's ash is the ultimate conservation. Final: she ends as the thing she catalogued.
- Theme link: to be remembered and to be alive are not the same; she chose alive.

## 6. VOICE DEFINITION

**Vocabulary level:** 6/10 — literate, sensory, period-textured, but never antiquarian-showy.
**Sentence rhythm:** Mixed/varied, leaning long-and-flowing in wonder/intimacy, clipping hard in
danger. Outlander-adjacent: immersive period sentences with a modern intelligence underneath.
**Formality:** Conversational-to-measured. Mara's modern interiority vs. the formal antique world she
moves through is a *deliberate friction* — she translates, undercuts, dryly compares.
**Humor presence:** Dry/subtle, mostly Mara's, mostly defensive — disappears under real fear.
**Emotional register:** Open but earned; restraint is the default so that the breaks land.
**Distinctive features:** First-person retrospective narrator (older Mara telling it) — the
retrospection lets dread bleed backward ("I did not know yet that…") which is the book's signature
suspense engine, the same one Outlander uses. The narrator knows the volcano is coming; the reader
feels it before the character.
**Anti-patterns (NEVER):** No analytical/explanatory similes ("like a [abstract concept]"); no
metacognitive narration ("she realized she was realizing"); no emotional-temperature reports ("a wave
of grief washed over her" — show the glasses, the *click*); no modern slang in narration except as
deliberate, owned anachronism in Mara's private interiority; no info-dump Latin glossing.

**--- VOICE UNDER PRESSURE ---**
At emotional peak, Mara's voice **goes flat and procedural** — she catalogues. The conservator's
training is her panic response: when the worst happens she describes condition, material, damage, as
if the people were artifacts, because naming them as people would end her. The prose strips to
inventory. (Counter-default note for the Writer: do NOT escalate to lyrical at the climax — *deflate*
to the catalogue. The lyricism lives in the calm chapters; terror is a packing list.) Secondary break:
retrospective intrusions cluster ("I have had thirty years to ask myself—") when the memory is too hot
to narrate straight.

## 7. STYLISTIC DEVICE

**Device:** **Fenn's documents** (epistolary inserts) — map marginalia, journal fragments, one long
letter — set between certain chapters, ≤500 words each.
**Function:** Carries the mystery and the dread the first-person present can't see; lets the reader
know things Mara doesn't (the doors' law is foreshadowed in his notes long before Ch.36).
**Frequency:** ~6 inserts total, at act seams (after Ch.2, 6, 12, 24, 30, 36). Not every chapter.
**Quality target — REVEAL:** each insert must say something the narrative did NOT — by Ch.36 the
reader who's been reading Fenn's notes has half-assembled the law of the door before Mara hears it,
turning the reveal into confirmation-dread rather than surprise. (If an insert merely re-states the
chapter, cut it.)

## 8. OPENING STRATEGY

**Chapter 1: The wrong emotion + the failure** (not "competent professional meets anomaly").
Open on Mara clearing her dead father's study — a scene of grief played for the *small, embarrassing,
funny* register (she's arguing with a stuck drawer, she laughs at the wrong thing) rather than solemn
mourning. The reader is hooked by a grief that behaves like real grief: petty, physical, ambushing.
The map and the door wait; chapter 1 earns *why she'd walk through one.* WHY: a sweeping time-slip can
open with portent (cliché) or with a recognizably human woman whose specific grief makes the
impossible leap *inevitable*. We choose the human. The retrospective narrator drops ONE cold line of
foreknowledge near the end ("I was three weeks from a world that no longer exists, and I spent that
afternoon angry at a drawer") to plant dread without spectacle.
