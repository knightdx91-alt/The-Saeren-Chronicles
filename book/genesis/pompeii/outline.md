# ACT I — THE MODERN FRAME & THE CROSSING (Ch. 1–6, ~12%)

## Chapter 1: The Drawer
**Premise:** Mara clears her dead archaeologist father's study and fails, in small human ways, to mourn him properly.
**Function:** Establish wound, lie ("the dead don't leave"), voice, and the longing that makes the leap inevitable.
**Emotional anchor:** The reading glasses, folded, *click*, in the drawer she can't open.
**Emotional surprise:** She laughs at the wrong line in his draft obituary.
**Opening strategy:** The wrong emotion + the failure (see foundation §8).
**Key beats:** 1) Stuck drawer / petty grief. 2) The unfinished dig promise. 3) The dead bread-starter. 4) Retrospective cold-line: "three weeks from a world that no longer exists." 5) She decides to finish *something* of his.
**Progressive:** — (opening). Adds: who she is and why she's hollow. Opens toward Ch.2: she keeps his "to chase" list, one item the antique map.
**Theme presence:** What we owe the dead. **Chaos:** bread obsession; sunk-cost ("I'll just finish the room"); the *click* memory.
**Subtext:** Surface = sorting objects; real = deciding whether to live forward.
**Structure:** Chronological/single-location. **Reading speed:** decelerate (sensory grief).
**Word target:** 5,300. **Writer warnings:** Do NOT do solemn-mourning prose; keep grief petty/physical. Avoid emotional-temperature reports.
**Beat subversion:** Genre expects a numinous "called to adventure" opening; instead the call is a *chore list* and a tantrum at furniture.

## Chapter 2: Too Accurate to Be Fake
**Premise:** Mara tracks the map to an antique shop and meets the shopkeeper, who explains the vanished treasure hunter, August Fenn.
**Function:** Deliver the inciting object + the mystery's first thread; plant re-read seeds.
**Emotional anchor:** Fenn's marginal note in a stranger's hand: *"The map is honest. The door is not."*
**Emotional surprise:** The shopkeeper is bored by the thing that will end her life; haggles over it.
**Opening strategy:** Bridge — object-forward (the shop).
**Key beats:** 1) The shop, the map, the coordinates. 2) Estate-sale provenance; Fenn vanished. 3) The "accurate for its period" detail. 4) Fenn's notes bundled with it. 5) She buys it on sunk-cost impulse.
**Progressive:** Builds on her decision to "finish something." Adds: the map, Fenn, the warning note. Opens toward Ch.3: she'll follow the coordinates.
**Theme presence:** RECEDES (plot-acquisition). **Chaos:** completionism justifies the purchase she can't afford.
**Subtext:** Surface = a transaction; real = she's buying a way out of her own life.
**Structure:** Dialogue-heavy two-hander. **Reading speed:** brisk.
**Word target:** 5,200. **Writer warnings:** Don't over-explain the map's "magic"; keep it ambiguous, period-real. **[INSERT after Ch.2: Fenn marginalia #1]**
**Beat subversion:** Genre expects a wise/mystical seller; instead a pawn-shop pragmatist who only cares that it's authentic.

## Chapter 3: Coordinates
**Premise:** Mara reads Fenn's notes, becomes half-convinced, and travels to the site the coordinates name.
**Function:** Raise the mystery; show her competence (modern) and her isolation; commit her physically.
**Emotional anchor:** A photograph of Fenn at the same site, decades old, looking *exactly her age*.
**Emotional surprise:** She treats a possibly-supernatural quest as a fieldwork checklist.
**Key beats:** 1) Decoding the notes. 2) Telling no one (no one to tell — isolation). 3) Travel to the site [PROPOSAL: a Mediterranean dig-coast]. 4) The phone she charges out of habit. 5) The threshold-place: a ruined doorway, cold air.
**Progressive:** From buying to going. Adds: Fenn's face, her aloneness. Opens toward Ch.4: she'll survey the threshold.
**Theme presence:** Curating grief vs. living. **Chaos:** the phone ritual (re-read seed #4 begins).
**Structure:** Travel/montage + arrival. **Reading speed:** accelerate then decelerate at the door.
**Word target:** 5,300. **Writer warnings:** Resist info-dump on the Library legend; ration it.
**Beat subversion:** Genre expects a dramatic portal; she finds a *boring* ruin and almost leaves.

## Chapter 4: The Survey
**Premise:** At the threshold, instruments behave wrong; she ignores her training and steps closer.
**Function:** The uncanny enters; her fatal flaw (finishing what she shouldn't) is dramatized.
**Emotional anchor:** Her compass spinning, and the absolute *silence* on the far side of the arch.
**Emotional surprise:** She narrates it like an equipment fault, annoyed, not afraid.
**Key beats:** 1) Anomalies. 2) Rational explanations failing. 3) Fenn's note matches what she sees. 4) She crosses the line to "just check." 5) The world goes quiet — cliffhanger.
**Progressive:** Arrival → contact. Adds: the door is real. Opens toward Ch.5: she's on the threshold.
**Theme presence:** Sunk-cost as fatal flaw. **Chaos:** completionism = she steps in.
**Structure:** Single-scene, tightening. **Reading speed:** decelerate, then snap.
**Word target:** 5,200. **Writer warnings:** Keep the mechanism *felt*, not explained.
**Beat subversion:** Genre expects awe at the portal; she's irritated her gear is broken.

## Chapter 5: The Threshold
**Premise:** Half through, Mara experiences the crossing — disorientation, sensory overload — and resists, then is pulled.
**Function:** The literal transition; mark the point of no return.
**Emotional anchor:** The taste of the air changing — woodsmoke, pine, no diesel.
**Emotional surprise:** Her last modern thought is mundane (she left the rental car unlocked).
**Key beats:** 1) The pull. 2) Trying to step back; the way back is *gone*. 3) Crossing. 4) Collapse. 5) Waking to Greek voices.
**Progressive:** Contact → transit. Adds: no return. Opens toward Ch.6: she wakes in antiquity.
**Theme presence:** RECEDES (pure sensation). **Chaos:** the mundane last thought.
**Structure:** In medias res sensation. **Reading speed:** fast, fragmenting.
**Word target:** 5,300. **Writer warnings:** Don't make the crossing a light-show; make it bodily.
**Beat subversion:** Genre wants a heroic plunge; she's *yanked* mid-retreat, robbed of agency on purpose (her agency comes later, by choice).

## Chapter 6: Greece, 70 AD  **[TP — crossing complete, ~12%]**
**Premise:** Mara wakes near Delphi, realizes when/where she is, and survives her first hours as a woman who does not exist.
**Function:** Land the premise; flip from mystery to survival; first taste of the gladiator's world.
**Emotional anchor:** The silence cutting out the modern world; a goatherd staring at her shoes.
**Emotional surprise:** Her first urgent problem is her bladder and her clothes, not wonder.
**Key beats:** 1) Waking, denial, dating it by coins/dress. 2) The shoes/clothes problem. 3) Near-disaster with locals. 4) Glimpse of a big scarred man (Valens) who does NOT help — walks past. 5) Taken in / arrested-adjacent — handed to Kallia.
**Progressive:** Transit → arrival-in-world. Adds: it's 70 AD, Greece, Vespasian. Opens toward Act II survival.
**Theme presence:** The dead past is now living. **Chaos:** bread (she clocks the market loaves before the danger).
**Subtext:** Surface = lost tourist; real = a person erased.
**Structure:** Chronological survival. **Reading speed:** mixed. **Word target:** 5,500.
**Writer warnings:** Establish gender/status powerlessness HARD — this is the Act II engine. **[INSERT after Ch.6: Fenn journal #2 — "I, too, woke in the wrong year."]**
**Beat subversion:** Genre expects the love interest's rescue on arrival; he ignores her — competence/coldness where the genre wants gallantry.

# ACT II — GREECE: SURVIVAL, THE ORACLE, THE GLADIATOR (Ch. 7–18, ~12–37%)

## Chapter 7: Kallia's House
**Premise:** Kallia shelters Mara; Mara begins the brutal apprenticeship of learning to *pass*.
**Function:** Establish the foothold; the "lone foreign woman can't just travel" obstacle made concrete.
**Emotional anchor:** Mara's hands failing at a loom; Kallia's lamp left burning for an absent son.
**Emotional surprise:** A fit of helpless laughter at her own uselessness.
**Key beats:** 1) Suspicion → grudging shelter. 2) Language, dress, the rules of being a woman. 3) The lamp. 4) Mara hides the map/phone. 5) Why she can't just walk to Pompeii.
**Progressive:** Arrival → foothold. Adds: Kallia, the social rules. Opens toward Ch.8: she needs money/identity.
**Theme presence:** Living forward vs. curating. **Chaos (Kallia):** the lamp/the unsellable room (secondary-character life #1).
**Structure:** Domestic, two-hander. **Word target:** 5,300.
**Writer warnings:** Don't speed-run language acquisition; make competence costly.
**Beat subversion:** Genre montages assimilation; we sit in the humiliation of it.

## Chapter 8: A Woman Without a Name
**Premise:** Mara tries to establish an identity/livelihood and runs into the wall of having no male guarantor.
**Function:** Dramatize the status antagonist; force her toward needing protection.
**Emotional anchor:** Being turned away from a transaction any man could make.
**Emotional surprise:** She wins a small thing through a modern skill (numbers/forgery of letters) and is ashamed it worked.
**Key beats:** 1) Failed attempts. 2) Danger of being claimed/owned. 3) She forges a "widow's" backstory. 4) Hears of the Oracle. 5) Hears of "the Roman with the mules" who hates questions — Valens again.
**Progressive:** Foothold → strategy. Adds: the Oracle as goal; Valens as recurring presence. Opens toward Ch.9.
**Theme presence:** Identity/legacy. **Chaos:** completionism (over-builds the fake backstory).
**Structure:** Episodic. **Word target:** 5,200.
**Beat subversion:** Genre gives a plucky-girl-makes-good win; her win is a forgery she's ashamed of.

## Chapter 9: The Roman With the Mules
**Premise:** Mara seeks out Valens to buy passage/protection toward Delphi; he refuses, coldly.
**Function:** First real two-hander; establish his lie, his walls, the spark beneath hostility.
**Emotional anchor:** Him re-tying a perfectly good knot rather than look at her.
**Emotional surprise:** He's funny — dry, mean-funny — and she laughs before she means to.
**Key beats:** 1) The approach. 2) His refusal; his read of her as trouble (mind-reading distortion). 3) A flash of his competence/menace. 4) She clocks the scars, the Latin, the wrongness of a Roman hiding here. 5) He says no. She decides she'll change his mind.
**Progressive:** Goal set → obstacle (him). Adds: Valens up close. Opens toward Ch.10.
**Theme presence:** Being needed vs. loved (his). **Chaos (Valens):** knots; mind-reading threat.
**Subtext:** Surface = a transport negotiation; real = two ruined people recognizing ruin.
**Structure:** Single-scene two-hander. **Word target:** 5,400.
**Writer warnings:** Resist instant chemistry-as-stated; chemistry is *behavioral* (the knot).
**Beat subversion:** Genre meet-cute; instead a meet-refusal, the romance built on his NO.

## Chapter 10: Earning the Yes
**Premise:** Mara makes herself useful to Valens's trade (a thing only her oddness can do) and earns grudging passage.
**Function:** Forced proximity; competence as courtship; Drusus introduced.
**Emotional anchor:** Mara doctoring a sick mule with modern instinct; Valens watching, recalculating.
**Emotional surprise:** Drusus's loud venality lands as comedy in a tense scene.
**Key beats:** 1) She solves a problem he can't. 2) Drusus arrives — debt, schemes. 3) Valens relents: passage, conditions. 4) A near-threat from men asking about a foreign woman (the legate's reach, seeded). 5) They set out.
**Progressive:** No → conditional yes. Adds: Drusus; the pursuit thread. Opens toward Ch.11 (the road).
**Theme presence:** RECEDES (caper energy). **Chaos (Drusus):** buys luxury mid-crisis (secondary life #2).
**Structure:** Collage (negotiation + caper + road-prep). **Word target:** 5,300.
**Beat subversion:** Genre has the man choose to help out of attraction; he helps because she's *useful* — attraction is the thing he's fighting.

## Chapter 11: The Road to Delphi

**Emotional surprise:** After violence, she's calm and *he* is the one shaking.

**Subtext:** Surface = a prophecy; real = the Oracle deciding whether mercy is a lie.

**Beat subversion:** Genre's oracle is grand and cryptic-for-effect; hers is exhausted and cryptic from *guilt*.

# ACT III — CAMPANIA: ARRIVAL, DEEPENING, MARRIAGE BECOMES REAL (Ch. 19–28, ~37–58%)

**Premise:** They reach the Bay of Naples; Mara sees Vesuvius for the first time and the reader's dread detonates.
**Function:** Arrive at the deadline-place; dramatic irony engine fully online.
**Emotional anchor:** Mara admiring the "beautiful green mountain" — the reader screaming inside.
**Emotional surprise:** She finds it *peaceful*; the horror is entirely the reader's.
**Key beats:** 1) The bay, the towns, Pompeii. 2) The mountain. 3) Locating the door's site (Oracle's where confirmed). 4) Realizing she still doesn't know the WHEN. 5) Settling in to wait and find out.
**Progressive:** Journey → destination reached. Adds: Vesuvius (dramatic irony); the door's location; the missing when. Opens toward Ch.20.

**Subtext:** Surface = arriving home-adjacent; real = arriving at a grave.
**Structure:** Arrival panorama. **Word target:** 5,400.
**Writer warnings:** Do NOT have her sense doom — her ease is the whole engine. Retrospective narrator may drop ONE cold line.
**Beat subversion:** Genre's arrival at the goal is hopeful; the reader knows it's a countdown.
