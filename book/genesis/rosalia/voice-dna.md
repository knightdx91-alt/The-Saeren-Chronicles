# Voice DNA — Rosalia

> Executable, enforceable voice spec. The Writer writes FROM this; Dialogue Polish and
> Hook Craft check AGAINST it; the Disruptor enforces the anti-pattern budget; the
> Editor/Evaluator hold the line. Genre: epic/political fantasy with romance, dual close-third.

---

## 1. GLOBAL NARRATIVE VOICE

- **POV/tense:** Close third, past tense, single-POV per chapter (the chapter's named
  POV character). Ch.18 only braids A/K within the chapter, with hard section breaks.
- **Vocabulary level:** 6/10 — clear and grounded, occasional reach for the precise word;
  never Nabokovian, never bare Hemingway. Court chapters lean half a notch up; road
  chapters half a notch down.
- **Sentence rhythm:** Mixed/varied. Vary length deliberately around the POV (see cards).
- **Formality:** Measured in narration; the *character's* register colors the close-third.
- **Humor presence:** Dry/subtle in narration; located mainly in Korvan's deflection and
  in irony (Loric's courtesy, the chief's proverbs). Absent in court-crisis scenes.
- **Emotional register:** Restrained by default; earns rawness by rationing it. Big
  feeling is shown through behavior and the body, not named.
- **Distinctive features:** (1) Perception is a recurring texture — who sees what, who is
  seen, what carries and what is swallowed (the "eats voices" motif). (2) Inheritance
  imagery — things handed down, things refused. (3) The close-third *thinks in the POV's
  obsession* (Amelia measures; Korvan touches/builds).
- **Anti-patterns (NEVER):** explanatory similes that decode the moment for the reader;
  metacognitive narration ("she realized she was realizing"); emotional-temperature
  reports ("a wave of grief washed over her"); "magic" spelling (always **magyk**);
  info-dump of lore (dragon memory ONLY trickles, situational); competence-cascade where
  a character coolly solves everything; adverb-loaded dialogue tags.

### VOICE UNDER PRESSURE (how the prose breaks)
- **Amelia under overload → goes FLATTER and MORE controlled.** Syntax tightens, becomes
  clipped and clinical; she counts and measures harder; affect numbs. The reader feels
  the dissociation by the *absence* of feeling-words. (See Ch.9, Ch.15.)
- **Korvan under overload → syntax FRAGMENTS and the body takes over.** Sentences break;
  physical sensation floods; humor vanishes entirely; tense can lurch toward present for
  a beat. (See Ch.10, Ch.12.)
- **Loric under (rare) overload → the courtesy cracks for one line, then over-controls.**
  The grief surfaces as something other than rage and is buried mid-sentence.

## 2. PER-CHARACTER VOICE CARDS

#### Amelia (POV)
- **Vocabulary band:** Educated, formal, precise; architectural and acoustic words leak
  in ("carried," "swallowed," "registered," "interval").
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Balanced, subordinated, controlled; she completes her thoughts.
  Under stress she *shortens* (inverts to clipped).
- **Rhythm:** Measured statements; few questions aloud (she answers them internally).
- **Verbal tics:** Quantifies ("three steps," "a half-second too long"); rates rooms by
  how sound travels; rations warmth.
- **Never says:** Slang, crude oaths, anything sloppy or unfinished; never says "magic."
- **Sample line:** "The hall took her voice and gave back nothing — good. A room that
  keeps secrets is a room I can stand in."

#### Korvan (POV)
- **Vocabulary band:** Plain, concrete, physical; craft words (fire, rope, grip, weight);
  occasional clan-idiom.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Action-first; short clauses under stress, run-ons when excited
  or deflecting; talks to animals and objects.
- **Rhythm:** Lots of questions and asides; jokes to dodge.
- **Verbal tics:** Strong opinions on doing-things-right (esp. fire); catastrophizes
  sensation; trails into the body.
- **Never says:** Court formality, abstraction-for-its-own-sake, anything bloodless.
- **Sample line:** "You don't lay the kindling like that — it'll choke. Here. No, give
  it. Everyone in this clan builds a fire like they're angry at it."

#### Loric (antagonist, in-scene)
- **Vocabulary band:** Mild, reasonable, civic; the language of fairness and questions.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Soft interrogatives and permissions; never declarative threats.
- **Rhythm:** Patient, even; lets silence do the work.
- **Verbal tics:** "I only said what everyone saw"; "I'm only asking"; disclaims agency.
- **Never says:** A direct demand; never raises his voice; never names what he wants.
- **Sample line:** "I haven't accused her of anything. I asked a question. If the room
  answered it for me, that's the room's doing, not mine."

#### The Chief
- **Vocabulary band:** Weathered, rural-proverbial.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Long pauses (rendered as beats), then short verdicts; answers
  questions sideways, with weather and proverbs.
- **Verbal tics:** "Later"; deflects hard truths into the landscape.
- **Never says:** An apology; the word "afraid."
- **Sample line:** "A man who dies on the hill he's right about leaves the hill to the
  next fool. We'll talk about it later."

#### The Queen
- **Vocabulary band:** Imperial, exact, cold-elegant.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Imperative mood; instructs, never asks. One register of
  tenderness, used only on Amelia, and it sounds almost like an order.
- **Verbal tics:** Commands phrased as facts ("You will not be at the banquet.").
- **Never says:** "Please"; never explains herself.
- **Sample line:** "You will stay where I can find you. That is not cruelty. That is the
  only love I am permitted to give you."

#### Selwyn (support)
- **Syntax fingerprint:** States the obvious as if it's hard-won wisdom; hedges.
- **Verbal tics:** "Well, here's the thing" before saying nothing; hoards/lists objects.
- **Sample line:** "Here's the thing about a dragon, though — and I've thought about this —
  there's no hiding one. That's just... that's just dragons for you."

#### Maren (support, proposed)
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Speaks in negatives and double-negatives; says what she *won't* say.
- **Verbal tics:** "I won't say it's wrong, but—"; recounts the silver when afraid.
- **Sample line:** "I won't say the Queen's lying to you. I'll just say I've counted the
  silver twice and it still doesn't add up to safe."

## 3. VOICE DIFFERENTIATION MATRIX (cover-the-name test, pre-solved)

| Character | Sharpest distinguisher | Contrast vs nearest |
|-----------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Amelia | Quantifies + rates acoustics; controlled, completes thoughts | vs Queen: Amelia measures *space*; Queen commands *people* |
| Korvan | Fire/craft opinions; body-first; deflective jokes | vs Selwyn: Korvan has real competence; Selwyn fakes wisdom |
| Loric | Disclaims agency; questions & permissions, never demands | vs Chief: Loric is soft to manipulate; Chief is soft to endure |
| The Chief | Proverbs + "later"; sideways via weather | vs Loric: Chief deflects to *land*; Loric deflects to *the room* |
| The Queen | Imperative mood; instructs, never asks | vs Amelia: no questions, no measuring — pure command |
| Selwyn | Obvious-as-wisdom; "here's the thing"; hoards | vs Korvan: no real expertise behind the certainty |
| Maren | Speaks in negatives; counts silver when afraid | vs everyone: only character who defines by refusal |

No two characters share their sharpest marker. Amelia/Queen (the closest pair, both
formal) are separated by *measuring vs commanding* and *questions vs none*.

## 4. ANTI-PATTERN BUDGET (per 1,000 words — Disruptor enforces; ties to style_check.py)

- **Explanatory/analytical similes (the #1 AI fingerprint): ≤ 3 / 1,000** (literary-leaning
  fantasy). Combined simile+metaphor markers must hold **≤ 4.0 / 1,000** per the style gate.
- **Em-dashes: ≤ ~10 / 1,000** (style-gate ceiling). Prefer the period.
- **Adverbs in dialogue tags:** near-zero (target 0).
- **"As if / almost as though" constructions: ≤ 1 / 1,000.**
- **Metacognitive narration ("she realized she was realizing"): ≤ 0.5 / 1,000** (effectively avoid).
- **Emotional-temperature reports ("a wave of X washed over"): ≤ 0.5 / 1,000** (avoid; show via body/behavior).
- **No NEW repeated 4–6 word phrase across chapters** except allowlisted motifs (below).
- **Lore-dump guard:** dragon ancestral memory may surface at most once per chapter, ≤ ~80
  words, always situational. Never a paragraph of history.

**Deliberate motifs to ADD to `tools/style_check.py` ALLOWLIST** (intentional repetition):
- "only what everyone saw" / "only said what everyone saw" (Loric's tic / cultural vocab)
- "a country with one citizen" (the parallel-outsiders re-read seed)
- "eats voices" / "ate the voices" (the fountain/acoustics motif)
- "survive to fight later" / "later" as the Chief's creed
These four are intended and tracked; do NOT let the gate flag them as accidental repeats.
