## Core Premise

A fantasy novel with dual protagonists set in a world shared by vampires,
shifters, and humans, bound by an uneasy treaty and threatened by a personal
vendetta that escalates into war.

---

## World-Building

### Vampires
- Descended from Atlanteans who attempted to eradicate disease; the experiment
  turned them into vampires instead.
- Feed on humans in exchange for offering them "protection" — a feudal
  arrangement that legitimizes vampire rule over human populations.
- Ruled by a Queen who possesses blood magyk.

### Shifters
- Human until the first full moon after their sixteenth birthday, when they
  shift into their animal form for the first time.
- Upon shifting, they join the clan that matches their animal (wolf clan, bear
  clan, etc.).
- Ancestral memories are inherited from the clan, but they do NOT arrive all at
  once. They trickle in gradually and situationally over time.
- Organized into clans under a single chief who rules over all of them.

### The Treaty
- A peace treaty between vampires and shifters ended the last war.
- Its central term: no new vampires are to be made, because vampires had been
  encroaching on shifter lands.
- The treaty does not technically address naturally born vampires — because no
  one believed that was possible.

### Humans
- The "food source" for vampires, in a structured protection arrangement.
- Politically subordinate but essential — the entire vampire economy depends on
  them.
- Vulnerable to manipulation if someone questions the legitimacy of the
  protection pact.

## Main Characters

### Amelia
- The Queen's daughter. The first naturally born vampire in thousands of years.
- Her existence violates the treaty — though there is no proof she is
  natural-born rather than made.
- Has inherited her mother's blood magyk, which should not have been heritable.
- Has been hidden her entire life. Her core want is simply to exist openly — to
  be allowed to attend a banquet like a normal person.

### Korvan
- Son of the chief of all shifter clans.
- At the time of the banquet, he has NOT yet shifted for the first time. He does
  not know what animal he is.
- His first shift happens during the journey home from the banquet, on the first
  full moon after his sixteenth birthday — purely a calendar coincidence, not
  magically triggered.
- He shifts into a dragon. The dragon clan died out a very long time ago,
  leaving him a clan of one with no elders, no traditions, and no place in the
  existing shifter hierarchy.
- Ancestral memories from the dragon line will trickle in over time,
  situationally rather than chronologically.

### Loric (Antagonist)
- A shifter whose father was killed by the Queen during the last war.
- His true motivation is emotional, not strategic: he wants the Queen
  specifically to suffer for what he has been through.
- Cannot be negotiated with or bought off because no political outcome satisfies
  his real goal.
- Engineers a "heads I win, tails you lose" scenario at the banquet by publicly
  exposing Amelia.
- His scheme depends on the chief being a reasonable man — which is its own
  structural irony.

### The Chief (Korvan's father)
- Forced into an impossible position by Loric's public exposure of Amelia.
- Not cowardly or complicit — a pragmatist who chose survival to fight another
  day over a clean moral stand that would have killed him.
- Believes that dying in a leadership challenge to Loric would have helped no one.

### The Queen (Amelia's mother)
- Chose to have Amelia in defiance of what the treaty required of her.
- Now faces an impossible choice: defend her daughter publicly and confirm the
  treaty violation, or disavow her and let Loric win by destroying what she loves
  most.

## Inciting Incident

1. The shifters attend a banquet at the vampire castle.
2. Amelia is forbidden to attend but sneaks in, driven by a lifelong desire to
   simply exist openly.
3. Korvan, the chief's son, finds her in the garden. They talk, and they connect
   — both are people without a clear place in the existing order (Korvan
   pre-shift, Amelia hidden).
4. Loric spies them together and recognizes a catalyst he can use.
5. Loric publicly exposes Amelia at the banquet. Because the accusation is
   public, the chief must either declare war or face a leadership challenge from
   Loric — Loric wins either way.
6. The chief declares war.
7. Because the exposure was public, Korvan travels home not with a private secret
   but under public suspicion, having been publicly linked to the vampire girl.

---

## The Journey Home

- Takes days or weeks depending on village location.
- Korvan endures public shame, whispers, accusations.
- Loric uses the journey to consolidate his position, painting Korvan and his
  bloodline as vampire-sympathetic — groundwork for a future challenge to the
  chief.
- Korvan and his father have a significant argument: Korvan demands his father
  should have acted; his father explains that dying in a leadership challenge to
  Loric would have helped no one — he had to stay alive to potentially do
  something later.
- This argument introduces a recurring moral dilemma for Korvan: pragmatic
  survival versus moral clarity. It will echo through his later choices.
- On the first full moon after his sixteenth birthday, Korvan shifts — into a
  dragon.

## Key Thematic Principles

- **Accusation alone is sufficient.** Loric needs no proof of Amelia's origins.
  Truth becomes irrelevant; perception is the weapon.
- **Loric's emotional motive makes him uncompromising.** A villain who wants
  suffering rather than power cannot be negotiated with.
- **The chief's reasonableness is the lever.** Loric's entire scheme works
  because the chief is a sensible man who won't die over a point of honor. This
  is a structural irony worth developing.
- **Inheritance as theme.** What characters inherit (blood magyk, ancestral
  memory, grudges, treaties, guilt) versus what they choose. Amelia inherited
  magyk she shouldn't have. Korvan will inherit memories from a dead clan. Loric
  inherited his father's death and made it his identity. The Queen refused what
  the treaty demanded she inherit.
- **Korvan and Amelia as parallel outsiders.** Both are people the existing
  order has no place for — which is the real foundation of their bond, deeper
  than romance.
- **Ancestral memory as slow reveal.** Korvan's dragon knowledge unfolds
  situationally across the story, allowing lore to land when it matters most
  rather than as a single info dump.

## Open Questions To Resolve

- Is Korvan's mother in the picture? Does he have siblings?
- Does the Queen specifically remember killing Loric's father, or was it one
  wartime kill among many? (Both work, but they tell different stories.)
- Does the chief know how Korvan genuinely connected with Amelia in the garden —
  that she's not the propaganda figure she'll be made into?
- What is Amelia doing during the journey home? (Imprisoned? Hidden deeper? Being
  prepared to flee? Parallel timeline opportunity.)
- Does Korvan confide in anyone about the garden meeting, or carry it alone?
- How do dragon ancestral memories connect to Atlantis, if at all? (Potential
  bridge between Korvan's heritage and Amelia's.)
