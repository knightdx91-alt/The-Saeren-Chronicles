# Outline — Rosalia

> 29 chapters, ~5,100–5,400 words each (~150k target; clears the 140k floor with margin).
> TRUE DUAL PARALLEL POV. After TP1 the two threads run on separate clocks (Amelia at
> court / Korvan on the road), cut between chapters. Honors the bible's beat order. The
> added length is DEPTH, not padding: the journey home is broken into textured stages
> (more villages, whispers, incidents showing the propaganda spread); the court thread
> gains political maneuvering and more Maren scenes; the war escalates in steps; dragon
> ancestral memory trickles in situational reveals; supporting cast (Selwyn, the Chief,
> the Queen, Loric, Maren) get their own on-page beats. Blueprint only.
>
> POV legend: **[A]** = Amelia, **[K]** = Korvan. Structural-diversity tags vary
> chapter to chapter (no two consecutive alike). Beat subversion mandatory per ch.
>
> TURNING POINTS (relative to 29 ch): **TP1 Exposure → Ch.7** (~24%);
> **TP2 First Shift → Ch.15** (~50%); **TP3 The Choice → Ch.22 (Korvan) / Ch.23 (Amelia)**
> (~76–79%); **Climax → Ch.27–28** (~93–97%); **Residue → Ch.29**.

## ACT I — THE CAGES & THE EXPOSURE (Ch. 1–8, ~0–28%)

### Chapter 1 — "Contraband" [A]
- **Premise:** Amelia, hidden her whole life, decides she will be at the banquet.
- **Function:** Establish her cage, her want (to be *seen*), the world's rules by
  implication. Plant acoustics obsession + beeswax memory.
- **Anchor:** A gown laid out for a girl who has never been in a room with other people.
- **Surprise:** She laughs — wrong — at the Queen's cold tenderness.
- **Opening:** Ordinary made strange.
- **Structure:** Single-scene, slow.
- **Theme:** Present (inherit vs choose: she was *made* in defiance of the treaty).
- **Chaos:** Acoustics-rating; mind-reading the servants.
- **Subtext:** Mother-daughter scene = love vs control; surface "be careful," real "you
  must not exist."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the "chosen one yearns for adventure" open; instead
  her forbidden dream is the most ordinary thing imaginable — to attend a party.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 2 — "The Walls Have Names" [A]
- **Premise:** The day before the banquet inside the Queen's household — Amelia and Maren;
  the machinery of secrecy; how Amelia will slip the watch.
- **Function:** NEW deepening chapter. Establish **Maren** early (keeper/handmaid, the one
  human the Queen won't feed on, closest thing to a friend) and the court's interior world
  before it becomes a prison. Plant the silver-recounting tic and the Queen's untouched-
  servant quirk in plain sight. Show Amelia's competence at invisibility (her contradiction).
- **Anchor:** Maren teaching Amelia to walk the servants' passages — the cage's one seam.
- **Surprise:** Tenderness between two captives where the reader expects mistress/servant.
- **Opening:** Bridge — interior, from gown to the body that must wear it unseen.
- **Structure:** Two-hander dialogue + household movement.
- **Theme:** Present (what you owe what you chose to make — Maren as the Queen's first refusal).
- **Chaos:** Maren recounts the silver; Amelia rates a stairwell's acoustics.
- **Subtext:** "If you're caught, I never showed you" = "I'd burn for you and we both know it."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre uses the handmaid as exposition-delivery; here Maren has her
  own dread (the war rumor, her torn debt) visible before she serves any plot function.
- **Words:** ~5,100.

### Chapter 3 — "Unshifted" [K]
- **Premise:** Korvan, sixteen-plus and still no animal, travels to the vampire banquet
  in his father's retinue, an outsider among the about-to-belong.
- **Function:** Korvan's cage (the shame of not-yet); introduce the chief (his only
  family — mother dead, no siblings), Selwyn, Loric (reasonable, watchful). Plant
  fire-building pedantry + swim memory. Establish the father-son pair as a *pair of two*.
- **Anchor:** Korvan watching a younger clan-mate get congratulated for a recent first shift.
- **Surprise:** He jokes brightly at exactly the moment he's most humiliated.
- **Opening:** Bridge — contrast cut from court interior to Korvan's exterior world.
- **Structure:** Chronological travel scene.
- **Theme:** Present (belonging as inheritance).
- **Chaos:** Fire opinions; catastrophizes a leg cramp ("is this it?").
- **Subtext:** Father-son small talk = "are you ashamed of me?"
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the heir to be powerful/confident; he's the one
  thing his culture has no respect for — empty-handed.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 4 — "Men Who Only Ask Questions" [K]
- **Premise:** The retinue's last leg and arrival at the vampire castle; Loric works the
  camp; clan politics among the chief's people surface.
- **Function:** NEW deepening chapter. Build Loric on-page as *reasonable* before he is
  monstrous — his patience, his tidiness, his way of "only asking questions." Show the
  shifter clans as a political body (not a monolith) and the chief's standing within it,
  so the later "leadership challenge" lever has weight. Selwyn's betrothal dread seeded.
- **Anchor:** Loric polishing his boots in a war-rough camp, asking Korvan a kind question.
- **Surprise:** Loric is the only one who treats unshifted Korvan as fully a person — and it
  reads as warmth, not threat (re-read poison).
- **Opening:** In medias res into camp politics.
- **Structure:** Chronological with embedded faction dialogue.
- **Theme:** Present (the reasonable man as the exploitable lever — first quiet statement).
- **Chaos:** Loric's tidy boots; Selwyn states the obvious as wisdom; Korvan's fire opinion.
- **Subtext:** Loric's questions = a survey of where the chief's support is thin.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre flags the villain with menace; here he's the kindest man in
  the camp, and the kindness is the weapon being loaded.
- **Words:** ~5,100.

### Chapter 5 — "The Room That Eats Voices" [A]
- **Premise:** Amelia sneaks into the banquet, overwhelmed by simply *being among people*.
- **Function:** Pay off the want; world on full display (vampires, humans, the pact);
  she slips to the garden to breathe. Plant the fountain that "eats voices."
- **Anchor:** The first time a stranger looks at her and she is not corrected for existing.
- **Surprise:** Stillness/awe where the reader expects danger.
- **Opening:** Bridge — from her hidden vantage into the lit hall.
- **Structure:** Collage (banquet sensory + overheard politics + her cataloguing).
- **Theme:** RECEDES — pure experience of being seen.
- **Chaos:** She rates the hall's acoustics mid-wonder.
- **Subtext:** Overheard pact-talk = the war that hasn't started yet.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects infiltration tension; instead it's rapture — the
  danger is invisible to her precisely because she's happy.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 6 — "The Garden" [K]
- **Premise:** Korvan, escaping his own humiliation, finds Amelia in the garden; they talk.
- **Function:** The bond — parallel outsiders, deeper than romance. "A country with one
  citizen" line (re-read seed). The fountain eats their words (only seen, not heard).
- **Anchor:** Two people who've never had a place recognizing it in each other.
- **Surprise:** A burst of real laughter in a book that's been about loneliness.
- **Opening:** Single-scene, two-hander dialogue.
- **Structure:** Single-scene, two-hander dialogue.
- **Theme:** Present (what we are vs what we're allowed to be).
- **Chaos:** Korvan offers unsolicited fire opinion; Amelia rates the fountain's acoustics.
- **Subtext:** Surface = flirtation/banter; real = "is there anyone else like me?"
- **Shareable moment:** The garden meeting itself — the whole book's heart in one scene.
  **Yearning seed (romance B-plot):** the first ungovernable pull; both look away too late.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects love-at-first-sight; instead it's recognition of
  shared *exile* — they bond over having no place, not over attraction.
- **Words:** ~5,300.

### Chapter 7 — "Heads I Win" [A]
- **Premise:** Loric, having SEEN them, publicly exposes Amelia at the banquet.
- **Function:** **TP1 (~24%).** The accusation engine fires; no proof needed. The chief is
  trapped. Loric "only said what everyone saw" (vocab seed).
- **Anchor:** The hall going silent as every face turns to Amelia — her want granted as
  a curse (to be seen).
- **Surprise:** The Queen's face shows *nothing* — and that nothing is the worst thing.
- **Opening:** In medias res into the exposure; the wound is public.
- **Structure:** In medias res set-piece.
- **Theme:** Present (perception as weapon, full statement).
- **Chaos:** Amelia mind-reads the whole room and is mostly right this time.
- **Subtext:** Loric's "questions" = a noose; the chief's courtesy = surrender.
- **Shareable:** The trap mechanic — "he wins whether the chief fights or folds."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the reveal to empower the hidden heir; instead being
  seen *destroys* her — the wish granted is the catastrophe.
- **Words:** ~5,300.

### Chapter 8 — "What a Reasonable Man Does" [K]
- **Premise:** The chief declares war rather than face Loric's challenge; Korvan,
  publicly linked to the vampire girl, is folded into suspicion. They leave.
- **Function:** Close Act I; launch the two clocks. The chief's reasonableness is the
  lever (irony stated). Korvan loses his name before he ever had one.
- **Anchor:** The chief's hand on Korvan's shoulder steering him out — protection that
  feels like a leash.
- **Surprise:** Korvan feels *relief* it's over, then shame at the relief.
- **Opening:** Fragmented — the declaration, then aftershocks.
- **Structure:** Fragmented (short scenes: the declaration, the departure, the first whispers).
- **Theme:** Present (pragmatism vs clarity — first statement of the dilemma).
- **Chaos:** Korvan snaps at Selwyn for the first time (small), regrets it.
- **Subtext:** "We march at dawn" = "I could not save you."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects a defiant war-declaration as triumph; here it's a
  defeat dressed as resolve — everyone loses, exactly as Loric designed.
- **Words:** ~5,100.

## ACT II-A — TWO CLOCKS BEGIN (Ch. 9–14, ~28–48%)

### Chapter 9 — "The Long Quiet After" [A]
- **Premise:** Amelia, now exposed, is hidden *deeper* by the Queen — a tighter cage.
- **Function:** Open the court clock. The Queen's defend-or-disavow trap begins. Deepen
  the Amelia–Maren relationship under the new pressure; the protective choice reads as
  guilt to the world.
- **Anchor:** Amelia listening to the castle through walls — acoustics, now a prison sense.
- **Surprise:** The Queen does something tender and useless.
- **Opening:** Essayistic-interior + scene.
- **Structure:** Essayistic-interior + scene.
- **Theme:** Present (what you owe what you chose to make).
- **Chaos:** Beeswax memory returns, mutated; Maren recounts the silver when frightened.
- **Maren thread:** First crack of her torn loyalty — she overhears the court turning.
- **Subtext:** Queen/Amelia = "I'm protecting you" / "you're burying me."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the heroine to plot escape; instead she's smothered
  by love, and love is indistinguishable from a cell.
- **Words:** ~5,100.

### Chapter 10 — "On the Road" [K]
- **Premise:** The journey home begins; the first villages; whispers start; Loric consolidating.
- **Function:** Korvan's social descent, **stage one**. Loric paints the bloodline vampire-
  sympathetic. Fire-building pedantry pays comic beat before it turns.
- **Anchor:** A child in a village refusing to look at Korvan.
- **Surprise:** Loric is *kind* to Korvan in public — the worst kind of attack.
- **Opening:** Chronological road episode.
- **Structure:** Chronological road episodes.
- **Theme:** Present (accusation defines him before he can).
- **Chaos:** Korvan catastrophizes every body-twinge; Selwyn hoards a trinket.
- **Subtext:** Loric's kindness = laying groundwork for the future challenge.
- **Shareable:** "The villain consolidates power by being *nice* to his victim."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects open persecution; the cruelty is courtesy, undeniable
  and unfightable.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 11 — "The Servant She Will Not Feed On" [A]
- **Premise:** Amelia learns/confronts the Queen's own refused inheritance via Maren.
- **Function:** Re-read seed pays partway (the Queen always refused the pact). Amelia
  begins to separate "being seen" from "being known." A breather chapter; the bond to
  Korvan deepens by absence (she thinks of him — **yearning beat**).
- **Anchor:** The Queen sitting with the human servant, doing nothing, needing nothing.
- **Surprise:** Tenderness where the reader expects a power play.
- **Opening:** Parallel timeline (present + glimpsed past).
- **Structure:** Parallel (Amelia's present + a glimpsed past of the Queen's choice).
- **Theme:** RECEDES toward character — the Queen as a woman, not a monster.
- **Chaos:** Maren speaks only in negatives; Amelia rates a chapel's acoustics.
- **Subtext:** "Why won't you feed on her?" = "did you ever love anyone freely?"
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the cold queen to be a villain reveal; instead she's
  revealed as the *first* refuser — Amelia's mirror, not her warden.
- **Words:** ~5,100.

### Chapter 12 — "The Burned Village" [K]
- **Premise:** A deeper stage of the road: a village already touched by the war's first
  raids; the propaganda has outrun the retinue and is waiting for them.
- **Function:** NEW deepening chapter. Show the war as a *spreading thing*, not an
  abstraction — the first concrete cost. The whisper-campaign has hardened into belief
  here; Korvan meets open hostility for the first time. Loric reframes a tragedy he had
  no hand in as proof of his thesis. First faint, situational trickle of *something* in
  Korvan (a wrongness in his sleep — seeds the shift without naming it).
- **Anchor:** A burned doorway where children's chalk marks survive on the un-burned stone.
- **Surprise:** Korvan is the one who helps dig out the dead; it earns him *nothing* — they
  fear him more for the strength.
- **Opening:** In medias res into the aftermath.
- **Structure:** Single-scene, grim and slow.
- **Theme:** Present (perception over truth — they've decided who he is before he arrives).
- **Chaos:** Korvan's fire opinion turns black against actual ash; Selwyn hoards a charred toy.
- **Subtext:** Villagers' silence = a verdict already passed.
- **Shareable:** "He digs their dead out with his hands and they hate him more for it."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre rewards the hero's compassion with allies; here compassion
  deepens the suspicion — competence in a feared man is just more reason to fear.
- **Words:** ~5,300.

### Chapter 13 — "Blood Will" [A]
- **Premise:** Under threat/pressure, Amelia's inherited blood magyk surfaces for the
  first time — uncontrolled.
- **Function:** The "shouldn't be heritable" thing manifests = living proof of the
  accusation. The Queen's choice tightens. Inheritance theme at full voltage.
- **Anchor:** Her own blood answering her without permission — terror at her own power.
- **Surprise:** A flat, numb register where the reader expects awe (voice-under-pressure).
- **Opening:** Single-scene, escalating.
- **Structure:** Single-scene, escalating.
- **Theme:** Present (inherit vs choose — she didn't choose this).
- **Chaos:** Failed emotional management — she goes cold, dissociated, can't feel it.
- **Subtext:** Queen teaching her to hide it = "your power is a confession."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre treats power-awakening as triumph/agency; here it's
  incrimination — the more powerful she is, the more doomed.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 14 — "Let Go So You'd Learn to Swim" [K]
- **Premise:** The father-son argument: Korvan demands the chief should have acted; the
  chief explains survival-to-fight-later.
- **Function:** Core of Korvan's moral dilemma, stated and dramatized. Swim memory pays
  off as the chief's creed. The chief reveals he *knows* the bond with Amelia was genuine
  (resolved canon) — and allows the propaganda anyway; he infers the bond from the public
  exposure, NOT from any garden confession (Korvan never told him; the garden meeting
  stays sealed inside Korvan, unspoken even here). The chief's knowing-yet-allowing is
  what makes him tragic and the dilemma unbearable.
- **Anchor:** The chief letting go of a rope/hand — echo of the swim memory, now ruinous.
- **Surprise:** The chief weeps, briefly, and Korvan has never seen it — and it doesn't help.
- **Opening:** Two-hander, the book's quietest chapter.
- **Structure:** Single-scene, two-hander.
- **Theme:** Present (pragmatism vs clarity — the thesis scene).
- **Chaos:** Korvan snaps viciously at Selwyn afterward (the big one), can't take it back.
  **The snap is the secret leaking.** Korvan carries the garden meeting ENTIRELY ALONE —
  no confidant, the reader the only witness — and the pressure of holding what no one else
  may know, atop the father's tragic knowing-yet-allowing, has nowhere to go but sideways
  into a cruelty Selwyn didn't earn. Not a confession; a pressure-leak.
- **Subtext:** "Later" = a word the chief has never once cashed. Under it: the thing Korvan
  will not say to anyone, the weight he carries by himself.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the mentor-father to be either right or wrong; he is
  *both*, and the scene refuses to resolve which.
- **Words:** ~5,300.

## ACT II-B — THE HINGE (Ch. 15–18, ~48–62%)

### Chapter 15 — "The First Full Moon" [K]
- **Premise:** **TP2 (~50%).** On the first full moon after his birthday, Korvan shifts —
  into a dragon. Calendar coincidence, not magic.
- **Function:** The hinge. Clan of one, no elders, no traditions. First true trickle of
  dragon ancestral memory (situational — NOT linked to Atlantis). Propaganda gift to Loric.
- **Anchor:** Korvan's own hands becoming something with no name in his culture.
- **Surprise:** Bewilderment/grief, not power-fantasy — he wanted to *belong*, and became
  the loneliest thing alive.
- **Opening:** In medias res into the shift.
- **Structure:** In medias res, sensory and fragmented.
- **Theme:** RECEDES then slams back (he inherited a dead clan's memory he never chose).
- **Chaos:** Fire-building obsession recurs grotesquely against actual dragonfire.
- **Subtext:** N/A — pure experience, then dread as he grasps what it means.
- **Shareable:** "The boy with no animal turns out to be the last dragon — and it's a
  catastrophe, not a gift."
- **Beat subversion:** The single biggest expected payoff (epic dragon reveal as
  empowerment) is inverted into isolation and danger.
- **Words:** ~5,400.

### Chapter 16 — "Aftermath of a Body" [K]
- **Premise:** The morning after the shift: Korvan human again, the retinue's reaction,
  the chief's reckoning, the first eyewitness rumor racing ahead toward Loric.
- **Function:** NEW deepening chapter. Don't skip the consequence. Show the immediate
  fallout among the clans, the chief's tragic pragmatism turning the dragon into an asset
  in real time, and the second, sharper trickle of ancestral memory (a fragment of the
  dead clan's grief — situational, earned by his own new grief). Selwyn's fear and loyalty
  both tested. The legend leaves the camp before Korvan can decide what it means.
- **Anchor:** Men who'd traveled beside Korvan for weeks now keeping a doorway between them.
- **Surprise:** The chief's first instinct is not fear or pride but *calculation* — and
  Korvan sees, for the first time, exactly what his father is.
- **Opening:** Bridge from the shift into cold daylight.
- **Structure:** Chronological with embedded memory-fragments.
- **Theme:** Present (inherit vs choose — the memory arrives uninvited).
- **Chaos:** Korvan's catastrophizing now justified; Selwyn states the obvious, terrified.
- **Subtext:** "We can use this" (chief) = "I will spend you, because I always have."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre cuts from transformation straight to the hero's mastery; here
  the chapter sits in the wreckage of the morning after — no mastery, only consequence.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 17 — "Banner" [A]
- **Premise:** Word of the dragon reaches court; Amelia is being made into a war-banner,
  her magyk into a rallying symbol — by her own side.
- **Function:** Both outsiders weaponized; Amelia sees Korvan turned into a monster-story
  and knows it's a lie. She begins to want to *act*, not just survive.
- **Anchor:** A war-painting/proclamation of "the vampire witch and her dragon" — neither
  of them as they are.
- **Surprise:** Amelia is, briefly, flattered by the banner before she's horrified.
- **Opening:** Collage of proclamations.
- **Structure:** Collage (proclamations + court scene + interior).
- **Theme:** Present (perception as weapon, now turned on both).
- **Chaos:** Mind-reading the court; beeswax memory at the war council.
- **Maren thread:** Maren's torn loyalty sharpens — the war that makes Amelia a banner also
  forces Maren toward a choice between the Queen (who spared her) and Amelia (whom she
  loves). One quiet beat of Maren's own dread, visible through behavior, not Amelia's analysis.
- **Subtext:** "We honor you, princess" = "we own you."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects her own side to be refuge; instead her allies
  weaponize her exactly as her enemies do.
- **Words:** ~5,100.

### Chapter 18 — "The Court Tightens" [A]
- **Premise:** Court politics escalate: factions push the Queen to disavow Amelia or
  deploy her; an envoy/negotiation collapses because Loric (offstage) has made peace
  impossible by design.
- **Function:** NEW deepening chapter. Develop the war's escalation in *steps* on the court
  side and give Amelia agency-in-embryo — she starts reading the politics, not just enduring
  them. Maren is forced to carry a message that could damn or save Amelia (her loyalty-cost
  rises). Show the Queen maneuvering and failing — every reasonable move feeds Loric.
- **Anchor:** A treaty-table set for a peace no one at it is allowed to make.
- **Surprise:** The Queen *offers* a concession Amelia never expected — and it lands as
  another cage, not a release.
- **Opening:** In medias res into a council.
- **Structure:** Fragmented (council fragments + Maren's errand + Amelia's deductions).
- **Theme:** Present (the reasonable move feeds the unreasonable man).
- **Chaos:** Amelia rates the council chamber's acoustics during the war council (planted use);
  Maren recounts silver before the errand.
- **Maren thread:** The errand IS Maren's own-life beat — her risk, her fear, her choice,
  shown through behavior.
- **Subtext:** "We will protect you" = "we will decide what you're for."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects court intrigue to offer the heroine allies; instead
  every faction is a different way of owning her.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

## ACT II-C — NO NEUTRAL GROUND (Ch. 19–21, ~62–72%)

### Chapter 19 — "No Neutral Ground" [K]
- **Premise:** Korvan tries to opt out — to disappear, be no one's weapon — and learns a
  dragon cannot hide; both sides want or fear him.
- **Function:** Drive him toward TP3. Loric makes the first move to claim/control or
  destroy the dragon-asset. **No confession thread** — Korvan still tells no one about the
  garden; the secret stays his alone. The chapter's pressure is internal: the dragon makes
  hiding impossible, and the truth that might explain who he really is (the garden, the
  bond, that he is not the sympathizer-monster of the propaganda) is a truth he refuses to
  spend — so he is misread by everyone and cannot correct it, by his own choice.
- **Anchor:** Korvan trying to leave and finding there is nowhere a dragon is not seen.
- **Surprise:** Selwyn offers, plainly, to *come with him* — and Korvan realizes he can't
  bring anyone where he's going, and can't even tell Selwyn why; the friendship reaches its
  ceiling at the wall of the thing Korvan won't say.
- **Opening:** Chronological with an interior-pressure scene.
- **Structure:** Chronological + interior-pressure (no confession beat).
- **Theme:** Present (you cannot inherit a war and decline it).
- **Chaos:** Selwyn states the obvious as wisdom; Korvan's swim memory at the choice point.
- **Subtext:** "Just leave, then" (Selwyn) = "take me with you"; Korvan's silence =
  "I can't, and I can't tell you why."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the "confide-in-the-loyal-friend / run away together"
  beat; instead Korvan declines both — the marked carry it alone.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 20 — "The Shape of the War" [A]
- **Premise:** The war turns from rumor to front line; the first real battle/raid the court
  must answer; the human cost (via Maren's people) becomes concrete.
- **Function:** NEW deepening chapter. Escalate the war a measurable notch and bind it to
  the human stake — it's *Maren's* people who are between the armies. Amelia, watching her
  banner being carried into killing, fully crosses from enduring to needing to act. Plant
  the logistics that make the Ch.27–28 climax possible (where the armies will meet).
- **Anchor:** A casualty list read at court with Maren's home village named on it.
- **Surprise:** Amelia does something small and forbidden to help — and it works, and it
  terrifies her that it worked.
- **Opening:** Bridge from court interior to the war's edge via reports.
- **Structure:** Collage (dispatches + Maren's grief + Amelia's first act of will).
- **Theme:** Present (perception drives the dying; the lie has a body count now).
- **Chaos:** Maren recounts silver, then stops mid-count — the first time the tic fails her;
  Amelia rates the silence of an emptied wing.
- **Maren thread:** Her village on the list IS her own-life devastation, on-page.
- **Subtext:** "It's only a village" (a courtier) = the whole logic of the pact, exposed.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre keeps the heroine's first action heroic/clean; hers is small,
  illicit, and the success scares her more than failure would.
- **Words:** ~5,300.

### Chapter 21 — "Heirloom" [K]
- **Premise:** A quieter road-stage: an old shifter (a keeper of clan-lore, or a relic site)
  gives Korvan a fragment of what the dragon clan *was* — and why it died.
- **Function:** NEW deepening chapter + the richest situational dragon-memory reveal yet
  (still NOT linked to Atlantis — per canon, the bond with Amelia is parallel-outsiders, not
  bloodline). Korvan learns the dragons died out *contained, outlasted* — seeding the climax's
  logic and his eventual self-authoring. A breather that deepens lore without an info dump
  (delivered as one person's half-remembered telling, contradicted by Korvan's own trickling
  memory — the truth is uncertain, which is the point). **Yearning beat:** alone, he thinks
  of Amelia — the only other person who'd understand being a clan of one.
- **Anchor:** A weathered carving of a dragon among extinct clans' marks — his face in stone
  no one living has worn.
- **Surprise:** The old keeper is not afraid of him — and Korvan almost can't bear the relief.
- **Opening:** Single-scene, contemplative.
- **Structure:** Essayistic-interior + two-hander (the telling).
- **Theme:** RECEDES toward lore/character (inherit vs choose, examined gently).
- **Chaos:** Korvan's fire opinion softened to grief; swim memory mutates again.
- **Subtext:** "Your kind were not killed; they were outwaited" = the book's whole strategy
  against Loric, foreshadowed in lore.
- **Re-read seed:** "outwaited" / "outlasted" — the method that ends the book, planted as
  the dragons' end.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre uses the lore-keeper to grant the hero a power/destiny; here the
  inheritance is grief and a warning, not a weapon.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

## ACT III — WHAT YOU CHOOSE (Ch. 22–29, ~72–100%)

### Chapter 22 — "Survive to Fight Later" [K]
- **Premise:** The chief asks Korvan to be *useful* — to let the dragon be leveraged.
  Moral clarity asks him to refuse. **TP3 for Korvan (~76%).**
- **Function:** The inherited dilemma demands payment. Korvan chooses to *act where his
