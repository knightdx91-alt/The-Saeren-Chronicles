# Outline — Rosalia

> 20 chapters, ~5,000 words each (~100k). TRUE DUAL PARALLEL POV. After TP1 the two
> threads run on separate clocks (Amelia at court / Korvan on the road), cut between
> chapters. Honors the bible's beat order. Blueprint only.
>
> POV legend: **[A]** = Amelia, **[K]** = Korvan. Structural-diversity tags vary
> chapter to chapter (no two consecutive alike). Beat subversion mandatory per ch.

---

## ACT I — THE CAGES & THE EXPOSURE (Ch. 1–6, ~0–30%)

### Chapter 1 — "Contraband" [A]
- **Premise:** Amelia, hidden her whole life, decides she will be at the banquet.
- **Function:** Establish her cage, her want (to be *seen*), the world's rules by
  implication. Plant acoustics obsession + beeswax memory.
- **Anchor:** A gown laid out for a girl who has never been in a room with other people.
- **Surprise:** She laughs — wrong — at the Queen's cold tenderness.
- **Opening:** Ordinary made strange.
- **Structure:** Single-scene, slow.
- **Theme:** Present (inherit vs choose: she was *made* in defiance of the treaty).
- **Chaos:** Acoustics-rating; mind-reading the servants.
- **Subtext:** Mother-daughter scene = love vs control; surface "be careful," real "you
  must not exist."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the "chosen one yearns for adventure" open; instead
  her forbidden dream is the most ordinary thing imaginable — to attend a party.
- **Words:** ~5,000.

### Chapter 2 — "Unshifted" [K]
- **Premise:** Korvan, sixteen-plus and still no animal, travels to the vampire banquet
  in his father's retinue, an outsider among the about-to-belong.
- **Function:** Korvan's cage (the shame of not-yet); introduce the chief (his only
  family — mother dead, no siblings), Selwyn, Loric (reasonable, watchful). Plant
  fire-building pedantry + swim memory. Establish the father-son pair as a *pair of two*:
  the chief is all Korvan has, and vice versa.
- **Anchor:** Korvan watching a younger clan-mate get congratulated for a recent first shift.
- **Surprise:** He jokes brightly at exactly the moment he's most humiliated.
- **Opening:** Bridge — contrast cut from Amelia's interior to Korvan's exterior world.
- **Structure:** Chronological travel scene.
- **Theme:** Present (belonging as inheritance).
- **Chaos:** Fire opinions; catastrophizes a leg cramp ("is this it?").
- **Subtext:** Father-son small talk = "are you ashamed of me?"
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the heir to be powerful/confident; he's the one
  thing his culture has no respect for — empty-handed.
- **Words:** ~5,000.

### Chapter 3 — "The Room That Eats Voices" [A]
- **Premise:** Amelia sneaks into the banquet, overwhelmed by simply *being among people*.
- **Function:** Pay off the want; world on full display (vampires, humans, the pact);
  she slips to the garden to breathe. Plant the fountain that "eats voices."
- **Anchor:** The first time a stranger looks at her and she is not corrected for existing.
- **Surprise:** Stillness/awe where the reader expects danger.
- **Structure:** Collage (banquet sensory + overheard politics + her cataloguing).
- **Theme:** RECEDES — pure experience of being seen.
- **Chaos:** She rates the hall's acoustics mid-wonder.
- **Subtext:** Overheard pact-talk = the war that hasn't started yet.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects infiltration tension; instead it's rapture — the
  danger is invisible to her precisely because she's happy.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 4 — "The Garden" [K]
- **Premise:** Korvan, escaping his own humiliation, finds Amelia in the garden; they talk.
- **Function:** The bond — parallel outsiders, deeper than romance. "A country with one
  citizen" line (re-read seed). The fountain eats their words (only seen, not heard).
- **Anchor:** Two people who've never had a place recognizing it in each other.
- **Surprise:** A burst of real laughter in a book that's been about loneliness.
- **Structure:** Single-scene, two-hander dialogue.
- **Theme:** Present (what we are vs what we're allowed to be).
- **Chaos:** Korvan offers unsolicited fire opinion; Amelia rates the fountain's acoustics.
- **Subtext:** Surface = flirtation/banter; real = "is there anyone else like me?"
- **Shareable moment:** The garden meeting itself — the whole book's heart in one scene.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects love-at-first-sight; instead it's recognition of
  shared *exile* — they bond over having no place, not over attraction.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 5 — "Heads I Win" [A]
- **Premise:** Loric, having SEEN them, publicly exposes Amelia at the banquet.
- **Function:** TP1 (~25%). The accusation engine fires; no proof needed. The chief is
  trapped. Loric "only said what everyone saw" (vocab seed).
- **Anchor:** The hall going silent as every face turns to Amelia — her want granted as
  a curse (to be seen).
- **Surprise:** The Queen's face shows *nothing* — and that nothing is the worst thing.
- **Structure:** In medias res into the exposure; the wound is public.
- **Theme:** Present (perception as weapon, full statement).
- **Chaos:** Amelia mind-reads the whole room and is mostly right this time.
- **Subtext:** Loric's "questions" = a noose; the chief's courtesy = surrender.
- **Shareable:** The trap mechanic — "he wins whether the chief fights or folds."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the reveal to empower the hidden heir; instead being
  seen *destroys* her — the wish granted is the catastrophe.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 6 — "What a Reasonable Man Does" [K]
- **Premise:** The chief declares war rather than face Loric's challenge; Korvan,
  publicly linked to the vampire girl, is folded into suspicion. They leave.
- **Function:** Close Act I; launch the two clocks. The chief's reasonableness is the
  lever (irony stated). Korvan loses his name before he ever had one.
- **Anchor:** The chief's hand on Korvan's shoulder steering him out — protection that
  feels like a leash.
- **Surprise:** Korvan feels *relief* it's over, then shame at the relief.
- **Structure:** Fragmented (short scenes: the declaration, the departure, the first whispers).
- **Theme:** Present (pragmatism vs clarity — first statement of the dilemma).
- **Chaos:** Korvan snaps at Selwyn for the first time (small), regrets it.
- **Subtext:** "We march at dawn" = "I could not save you."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects a defiant war-declaration as triumph; here it's a
  defeat dressed as resolve — everyone loses, exactly as Loric designed.
- **Words:** ~5,000.

## ACT II — TWO CLOCKS (Ch. 7–14, ~30–62%)

### Chapter 7 — "The Long Quiet After" [A]
- **Premise:** Amelia, now exposed, is hidden *deeper* by the Queen — a tighter cage.
- **Function:** Open the court clock. The Queen's defend-or-disavow trap begins. Introduce
  **Maren** — Amelia's keeper/handmaid, the one human the Queen won't feed on, the closest
  thing Amelia has to a friend. Establish their relationship as Amelia's human stake. The
  protective choice reads as guilt to the world.
- **Anchor:** Amelia listening to the castle through walls — acoustics, now a prison sense.
- **Surprise:** The Queen does something tender and useless.
- **Structure:** Essayistic-interior + scene.
- **Theme:** Present (what you owe what you chose to make).
- **Chaos:** Beeswax memory returns, mutated; Maren recounts the silver when frightened.
- **Subtext:** Queen/Amelia = "I'm protecting you" / "you're burying me."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the heroine to plot escape; instead she's smothered
  by love, and love is indistinguishable from a cell.
- **Words:** ~5,000.

### Chapter 8 — "On the Road" [K]
- **Premise:** The journey home; whispers, accusations, Loric consolidating.
- **Function:** Korvan's social descent; Loric paints the bloodline vampire-sympathetic.
  Fire-building pedantry pays comic beat before it turns.
- **Anchor:** A child in a village refusing to look at Korvan.
- **Surprise:** Loric is *kind* to Korvan in public — the worst kind of attack.
- **Structure:** Chronological road episodes.
- **Theme:** Present (accusation defines him before he can).
- **Chaos:** Korvan catastrophizes every body-twinge; Selwyn hoards a trinket.
- **Subtext:** Loric's kindness = laying groundwork for the future challenge.
- **Shareable:** "The villain consolidates power by being *nice* to his victim."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects open persecution; the cruelty is courtesy, undeniable
  and unfightable.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 9 — "Blood Will" [A]
- **Premise:** Under threat/pressure, Amelia's inherited blood magyk surfaces for the
  first time — uncontrolled.
- **Function:** The "shouldn't be heritable" thing manifests = living proof of the
  accusation. The Queen's choice tightens. Inheritance theme at full voltage.
- **Anchor:** Her own blood answering her without permission — terror at her own power.
- **Surprise:** A flat, numb register where the reader expects awe (voice-under-pressure).
- **Structure:** Single-scene, escalating.
- **Theme:** Present (inherit vs choose — she didn't choose this).
- **Chaos:** Failed emotional management — she goes cold, dissociated, can't feel it.
- **Subtext:** Queen teaching her to hide it = "your power is a confession."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre treats power-awakening as triumph/agency; here it's
  incrimination — the more powerful she is, the more doomed.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 10 — "Let Go So You'd Learn to Swim" [K]
- **Premise:** The father-son argument: Korvan demands the chief should have acted; the
  chief explains survival-to-fight-later.
- **Function:** Core of Korvan's moral dilemma, stated and dramatized. Swim memory pays
  off as the chief's creed. The chief reveals he *knows* the bond with Amelia was genuine
  (resolved canon) — and allows the propaganda anyway; he infers the bond from the public
  exposure, NOT from any garden confession (Korvan never told him; the garden meeting
  stays sealed inside Korvan, unspoken even here). The chief's knowing-yet-allowing is
  what makes him tragic and the dilemma unbearable.
- **Anchor:** The chief letting go of a rope/hand — echo of the swim memory, now ruinous.
- **Surprise:** The chief weeps, briefly, and Korvan has never seen it — and it doesn't help.
- **Structure:** Single-scene, two-hander; the book's quietest, hardest chapter.
- **Theme:** Present (pragmatism vs clarity — the thesis scene).
- **Chaos:** Korvan snaps viciously at Selwyn afterward (the big one), can't take it back.
  **The snap is the secret leaking.** Korvan is carrying the garden meeting ENTIRELY ALONE
  — no confidant, the reader the only witness — and the pressure of holding what no one
  else may know, on top of the father's tragic knowing-yet-allowing, has nowhere to go but
  sideways into a cruelty Selwyn didn't earn. Not a confession; a pressure-leak.
- **Subtext:** "Later" = a word the chief has never once cashed. Under it: the thing
  Korvan will not say to anyone, the weight he carries by himself.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the mentor-father to be either right or wrong; he is
  *both*, and the scene refuses to resolve which.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 11 — "The Servant She Will Not Feed On" [A]
- **Premise:** Amelia learns/confronts the Queen's own refused inheritance via Maren.
- **Function:** Re-read seed pays partway (the Queen always refused the pact). Amelia
  begins to separate "being seen" from "being known." A breather chapter for the bond
  to deepen by absence (she thinks of Korvan).
- **Anchor:** The Queen sitting with the human servant, doing nothing, needing nothing.
- **Surprise:** Tenderness where the reader expects a power play.
- **Structure:** Parallel (Amelia's present + a glimpsed past of the Queen's choice).
- **Theme:** RECEDES toward character — the Queen as a woman, not a monster.
- **Chaos:** Maren speaks only in negatives; Amelia rates a chapel's acoustics.
- **Subtext:** "Why won't you feed on her?" = "did you ever love anyone freely?"
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the cold queen to be a villain reveal; instead she's
  revealed as the *first* refuser — Amelia's mirror, not her warden.
- **Words:** ~5,000.

### Chapter 12 — "The First Full Moon" [K]
- **Premise:** TP2 (~50%). On the first full moon after his birthday, Korvan shifts —
  into a dragon. Calendar coincidence, not magic.
- **Function:** The hinge. Clan of one, no elders, no traditions. First trickle of dragon
  ancestral memory (situational — NOT linked to Atlantis). Propaganda gift to Loric.
- **Anchor:** Korvan's own hands becoming something with no name in his culture.
- **Surprise:** Bewilderment/grief, not power-fantasy — he wanted to *belong*, and became
  the loneliest thing alive.
- **Structure:** In medias res into the shift, sensory and fragmented.
- **Theme:** RECEDES then slams back (he inherited a dead clan's memory he never chose).
- **Chaos:** Fire-building obsession recurs grotesquely against actual dragonfire.
- **Subtext:** N/A — pure experience, then dread as he grasps what it means.
- **Shareable:** "The boy with no animal turns out to be the last dragon — and it's a
  catastrophe, not a gift."
- **Beat subversion:** The single biggest expected payoff (epic dragon reveal as
  empowerment) is inverted into isolation and danger.
- **Words:** ~5,400.

### Chapter 13 — "Banner" [A]
- **Premise:** Word of the dragon reaches court; Amelia is being made into a war-banner,
  her magyk into a rallying symbol — by her own side.
- **Function:** Both outsiders weaponized; Amelia sees Korvan turned into a monster-story
  and knows it's a lie. She begins to want to *act*, not just survive.
- **Anchor:** A war-painting/proclamation of "the vampire witch and her dragon" — neither
  of them as they are.
- **Surprise:** Amelia is, briefly, flattered by the banner before she's horrified.
- **Structure:** Collage (proclamations + court scene + interior).
- **Theme:** Present (perception as weapon, now turned on both).
- **Chaos:** Mind-reading the court; beeswax memory at the war council.
- **Maren thread:** Maren's torn loyalty sharpens — the war that makes Amelia a banner
  also forces Maren toward a choice between the Queen (who spared her) and Amelia (whom she
  loves). One quiet beat of Maren's own dread, visible through behavior, not Amelia's analysis.
- **Subtext:** "We honor you, princess" = "we own you."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects her own side to be refuge; instead her allies
  weaponize her exactly as her enemies do.
- **Words:** ~5,000.

### Chapter 14 — "No Neutral Ground" [K]
- **Premise:** Korvan tries to opt out — to disappear, be no one's weapon — and learns a
  dragon cannot hide; both sides want or fear him.
- **Function:** Drive him toward TP3. Loric makes the first move to claim/control or
  destroy the dragon-asset. **No confession thread** — Korvan still tells no one about the
  garden; the secret stays his alone. The chapter's pressure is internal: the dragon makes
  hiding impossible, and the one thing that might explain *who he really is* (the garden,
  the bond, that he is not the sympathizer-monster of the propaganda) is a truth he refuses
  to spend — so he is misread by everyone and cannot correct it, by his own choice.
- **Anchor:** Korvan trying to leave and finding there is nowhere a dragon is not seen.
- **Surprise:** Selwyn offers, plainly, to *come with him* — and Korvan realizes he can't
  bring anyone where he's going, and can't even tell Selwyn why; the friendship reaches its
  ceiling at the wall of the thing Korvan won't say. (The isolation is self-chosen and total.)
- **Structure:** Chronological with an interior-pressure scene (no confession beat).
- **Theme:** Present (you cannot inherit a war and decline it).
- **Chaos:** Selwyn states the obvious as wisdom; Korvan's swim memory at the choice point.
- **Subtext:** "Just leave, then" (Selwyn) = "take me with you"; Korvan's silence =
  "I can't, and I can't tell you why."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects the "confide-in-the-loyal-friend / run away together"
  beat; instead Korvan declines both the confession and the company — the marked carry it
  alone, and the book denies even the comfort of being known by a friend.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

## ACT III — WHAT YOU CHOOSE (Ch. 15–20, ~62–100%)

### Chapter 15 — "The Cause of Every Death" [A]
- **Premise:** Loric engineers a public moment framing Amelia and Korvan as the *cause*
  of every death in the war so far.
- **Function:** Accusation engine at full power. The Queen's trap comes due in public.
  Push Amelia to her choice.
- **Anchor:** A list of the dead read aloud and laid at Amelia's feet.
- **Surprise:** The Queen finally acts — and it's the *wrong* save, making it worse.
- **Structure:** Single-scene set-piece.
- **Theme:** Present (perception over truth, the body count of a lie).
- **Chaos:** Loric, winning, hears his father's *laugh* (re-read seed curdles his victory).
- **Maren thread:** Maren's choice comes due — she acts (or refuses to act) in a way that
  costs her, giving Amelia's public ordeal a private human price (the friend who paid for her).
- **Subtext:** "Look what you've done" = "look what I made them think you did."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects a courtroom-style vindication; truth is offered and
  it changes *nothing* — the thesis made literal.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 16 — "Survive to Fight Later" [K]
- **Premise:** The chief asks Korvan to be *useful* — to let the dragon be leveraged.
  Moral clarity asks him to refuse. TP3 for Korvan (~75%).
- **Function:** The inherited dilemma demands payment. Korvan chooses to *act where his
  father only waited* — naming a third path (neither the chief's instrument nor a
  runaway). Dragon memory trickles the lore that makes the choice possible.
- **Anchor:** Korvan setting down the role his father held out — and his father's face.
- **Surprise:** The chief is *relieved*, not betrayed — he wanted this and couldn't say so.
- **Structure:** Two-hander into action.
- **Theme:** Present (choose over inherit — the turn).
- **Chaos:** Fire opinion, last time, now as competence not pedantry.
- **Subtext:** "Stay alive" = "do the thing I never could."
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects father-son rupture or reconciliation; instead a
  quiet handoff — the pragmatist *blesses* the clarity he couldn't afford.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 17 — "Seen on My Own Terms" [A]
- **Premise:** Amelia stops hiding — chooses to be visible deliberately, accepting she'll
  be misread, denying Loric the secret he weaponizes. TP3 for Amelia.
- **Function:** Her arc turn — being seen vs being known, resolved by choosing *how*.
  Her blood magyk used by her will for the first time.
- **Anchor:** Amelia walking into a room she was forbidden, on purpose, in daylight.
- **Surprise:** It is not triumphant — it's terrifying and lonely and she does it anyway.
- **Structure:** Single-scene, mirrored against Chapter 1.
- **Theme:** Present (the wish reclaimed as a choice, not a curse).
- **Chaos:** Acoustics-rating, now as control; beeswax memory resolves.
- **Subtext:** "Here I am" = "you don't get to define me anymore."
- **Shareable:** The mirror of Ch.1 — the contraband girl becomes uncontainable by choice.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre saves the public reveal for triumph; here it's an act of
  bleak-control, sacrifice not victory.
- **Words:** ~5,200.

### Chapter 18 — "The Convergence" [K+A alternating]
- **Premise:** The two threads meet at the war's edge; Amelia and Korvan reunite under
  fire, no longer the people from the garden.
- **Function:** Bring the parallel clocks together for the climax run-up. Bond tested by
  who they've each become.
- **Anchor:** The fountain-line ("eats voices") inverted — now they speak and are heard.
- **Surprise:** They almost don't recognize each other; the garden feels like another life.
- **Structure:** Parallel/alternating POV within the chapter (only chapter that braids).
- **Theme:** Present (parallel outsiders, chosen now not assigned).
- **Chaos:** Both signature tics collide once (fire opinion meets acoustics).
- **Subtext:** "You're different" = "are we still the same to each other?"
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects romantic reunion catharsis; instead wary
  re-acquaintance — love as a decision, not a given.
- **Words:** ~5,400.

### Chapter 19 — "Outlasted" [A]
- **Premise:** Climax. Loric is stopped/contained — not defeated by argument (impossible
  by canon) but outlasted at unrecoverable cost; the war is redirected, not undone.
- **Function:** Resolve the engine. The reveal (to reader only): the Queen never knew
  Loric's name or his father. Loric's grief breaks through once, then is buried forever.
- **Anchor:** Loric, at the end, hearing his father's laugh one last time — and no one
  ever knowing why he did any of it.
- **Surprise:** No catharsis of justice; a hollow, costly stop where the reader wanted
  triumph.
- **Structure:** Set-piece climax, fragmented under pressure.
- **Theme:** Present (grief-as-identity cannot be set down — the horror answered).
- **Chaos:** Loric's tidy boots in the ruin; failed emotional management breaks through.
- **Subtext:** "You don't even remember" (Loric) — the Queen, genuinely, does not.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects villain defeat as moral payoff; the book denies it —
  he is stopped, never answered, never understood by his target.
- **Words:** ~5,400.

### Chapter 20 — "What We Choose" [K, with A coda]
- **Premise:** Aftermath. Partial, bittersweet peace; the cost is named; both outsiders
  claim authored places the order had no slot for.
- **Function:** Deposit the emotional residue. Korvan as a dragon by choice, not curse;
  Amelia seen and finally *known* (by him, by herself). Theme question handed to reader.
- **Anchor:** The two of them somewhere with no name on the map — a place for people the
  order has no place for.
- **Surprise:** A small, banal domestic detail (a fire built right) carrying the whole weight.
- **Structure:** Quiet single-scene + short alternating coda.
- **Theme:** Present (inherit vs choose — left open, handed to the reader).
- **Chaos:** Korvan builds a fire correctly; Amelia rates the silence's acoustics, content.
- **Subtext:** "Now what?" = "we get to decide — for the first time."
- **Re-read payoff:** "A country with one citizen" returns, now two.
- **Emotional residue:** Unsettled by how little truth mattered; quietly re-examining
  one's own certainty about people already judged.
- **Beat subversion:** Genre expects restored-order epilogue; instead a deliberately
  *small*, off-map ending — no throne reclaimed, no clan rebuilt, just two authored lives.
- **Words:** ~5,000.

## OUTLINE QUALITY CHECKS
- **Progressive, not parallel:** each premise line advances on the last; cannot reorder.
- **POV interleave:** A/K/A/K/A/K · A/K/A/K/A/K/A/K · A/K/A/A/K(+A)/A/K — court and road
  clocks alternate cleanly; Ch.18 braids both at convergence.
- **Anchor variety:** visual (gown, painting), auditory (acoustics, laugh), gestural
  (hand on shoulder, letting go), verbal (fountain line) — spread, no 3 same in a row.
- **Chaos distribution:** each major character ≥2–3 chaos beats, spread not clustered.
- **Secondary-character own-life scenes:** Maren's own dread/loyalty-choice is visible
  through behavior in Ch.7, Ch.11, Ch.13, Ch.15; Selwyn's own life (betrothal dread,
  hoarding, the "come with you" offer) surfaces in Ch.6, Ch.8, Ch.14. Both clear the
  "≥2 secondary-character own-life moments" bar.
- **Opening diversity:** Ch.1 ordinary-made-strange; later bridges vary (in medias res,
  collage, two-hander) — never repeated back-to-back.
- **Breathing room:** Ch.3 and Ch.11 RECEDE; Ch.12 receding then slamming.
- **Structural diversity:** single-scene / chronological / collage / fragmented /
  essayistic / parallel / in-medias-res rotated; no two consecutive identical.
- **Word floor:** 20 ch × ~5,000–5,400 ≈ 102,000 words — clears the 95k floor with margin.
