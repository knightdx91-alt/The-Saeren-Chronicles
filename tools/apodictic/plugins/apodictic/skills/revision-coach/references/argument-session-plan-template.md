## Testimony Containment Variant

Use when the project is testimony or witness-heavy advocacy.

```markdown
## Testimony Containment Overlay

**Observational layer:** [What the witness can directly establish]

**Interpretive layer:** [What the witness is inferring from observation]

**Representative layer:** [What the manuscript is asking the testimony to stand for]

**Cross-exam risk:** [Highest-risk question corridor]

**Containment goal:** [How today's work narrows or clarifies the testimony's argumentative reach]
```

---

## Deadline Variant

Use when the coach is sequencing multiple argument sessions under time pressure.

```markdown
## Deadline Compression Note

**Above the line:** [What must be repaired before deadline]

**Below the line:** [What remains important but deferred]

**Reason for the line:** [Dependency and leverage explanation]
```

---

## Output Discipline

The coach should:

1. cite codes and sections from `Argument_State.md`
2. keep scope narrow enough to be achievable
3. define completion in structural, testable terms
4. avoid sentence-level drafting instructions

---

## Bottom Line

An argument session plan should tell the writer what structural corridor to work on, why it matters first, and how they will know they actually improved it.
