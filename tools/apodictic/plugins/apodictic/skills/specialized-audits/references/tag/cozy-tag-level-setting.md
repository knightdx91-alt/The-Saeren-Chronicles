## What Is a Level-Setting Brief?

Every tag audit includes an inline **Level-Setting Notes** section that summarizes the external evidence grounding its diagnostic axes. When external evidence is unusually deep — as it is for cozy, which has dedicated BISAC/Thema codes, peer-reviewed recovery-psychology research, and cross-media scholarship — a standalone companion brief like this one supplements the inline section with full citations and source evaluation.

A standalone brief is the exception, not the standard. Most tags will be adequately served by the inline section alone. This brief exists because the cozy evidence base is genuinely rich enough to warrant it.

**Evidence labels used in this document:**
- `SOURCE-VERIFIED` — Taxonomy/sales data or peer-reviewed findings.
- `MARKET INFERENCE` — Editorial projection from mixed trade signals.

## 1. Taxonomy Reality (What the Standards Say)

### BISAC (US-heavy workflows) `SOURCE-VERIFIED`

As of the BISAC 2025 fiction list:
- `FIC155000` exists as `FICTION / Cozy` — a broad fiction-level code.
- Cozy is explicitly represented under mystery via `FIC022070` (`FICTION / Mystery & Detective / Cozy / General`) plus granular subcodes (Animals, Culinary, Crafts, Books/Bookstores/Libraries, etc.).
- Cozy also appears as `FIC009160` (`FICTION / Fantasy / Cozy`).

**Operational implication:** In BISAC workflows, cozy is now both a broad fiction-level signal and a subgenre pattern (strongest in mystery and fantasy, emerging in other categories).

### Thema (international workflows) `SOURCE-VERIFIED`

Thema 1.6 supports cozy variants across multiple parent genres:
- `FFJ` — Crime and mystery: cosy mystery
- `FMJ` — Fantasy: cosy / cozy
- `FLJ` — Science Fiction: cosy / cozy
- `FRDJ` — Romance: cosy / cozy

**Operational implication:** Thema operationalizes cozy as a true cross-genre emotional profile, not only as "cozy mystery." This validates the tag audit's cross-genre architecture.

### Merchandising Gap `SOURCE-VERIFIED`

BISAC Merchandising Themes (2025) does not currently include an explicit "cozy/cosy" theme code.

**Operational implication:** Cozy discoverability still depends heavily on subject coding + jacket/blurb copy + comp framing, not just merchandising tags. The Shelf Positioning audit should flag this gap when evaluating cozy-positioned manuscripts.

## 2. Market Signal Reality

### Stronger signal (trade + sales infrastructure) `SOURCE-VERIFIED`

Circana BookScan coverage in PW (Jan 9, 2026) shows a mostly flat but durable print market (762.4M units in 2025, +0.3% YoY), with adult fiction still resilient. Category divergence (romance up, fantasy down, SF up) is consistent with subgenre fragmentation and crossover behavior.

### Moderate signal (trade commentary) `MARKET INFERENCE`

Library Journal reporting (Nov 2024) describes mystery/thriller as strong in a mostly flat market, with cozy remaining a stable and visible substream. Additional LJ preview reporting notes format and subsegment shifts inside cozy (format mix, culinary growth, geographic diversification).

### Weaker signal (media trend coverage) `MARKET INFERENCE`

Popular-press reporting (Goodreads trend coverage via syndication) indicates "comfort read" demand and cozy visibility.

**Operational rule:** Treat consumer-trend articles as directional, not dispositive. Use for hypothesis generation, not hard market claims.

## 3. Reader-Psychology Baseline

### Recovery and Stress Regulation `SOURCE-VERIFIED`

Communication and media psychology findings support a recovery model: entertaining media can support psychological detachment, relaxation, and vitality (Rieger et al., 2014). Experimental work with physiological measurement (cortisol-linked design) suggests entertainment features can amplify stress recovery effects (Rieger & Bente, 2018).

**Editorial inference:** Cozy value is not just "low conflict" — it is reliable recovery architecture (pressure + decompression + restored orientation). This directly supports the Recovery Rhythm axis and the distinction between high-stakes cozy (which can work) and recovery-deficit cozy (which can't).

### Narrative Resistance and Trust `SOURCE-VERIFIED`

Narrative persuasion research indicates that story forms can reduce reactance/counterarguing in some conditions via character involvement and lowered overt persuasive pressure (Moyer-Gusé et al., 2010).

**Editorial inference:** Cozy delivery is undermined by didactic pressure, not by conflict itself. Trust is preserved when guidance is embedded in scene consequence and relationship dynamics rather than explicit lecturing. This supports the false-positive diagnosis: conflict doesn't break cozy; lecturing does.

### Social Connectedness Mechanism `SOURCE-VERIFIED`

Nostalgia/social connectedness research repeatedly links comfort-oriented memory states with strengthened perceived social connection and support-seeking tendencies (Wildschut et al., 2010; Juhl et al., 2021).

**Editorial inference:** Belonging is not decorative in cozy work; it is load-bearing craft infrastructure. This is the empirical foundation for the Belonging Engine axis and the "return address" concept.

---

## 4. Cross-Media Transfer (With Guardrails)

Game-studies work on "cozy" (Project Horseshoe, 2017) converges on: safety, softness, abundance/sufficiency, bounded challenge with recoverable stakes. Related scholarship on "dark cozy" (Waszkiewicz, 2024) suggests darker themes can coexist with cozy framing if safety cues and recovery structure remain intact.

**Editorial transfer (book-facing):** Cozy should be modeled as an affective contract with bounded threat and predictable restoration, not as a ban on all intensity. Cross-media evidence should be treated as analogical, not directly prescriptive — game and book cozy share experience-layer logic but differ in reader agency and pacing.

---

## 5. Operational Conclusions

1. Keep cozy as a cross-genre tag, not a parent genre. Both BISAC and Thema now support this architecturally.
2. Calibrate by market locale: BISAC workflows show cozy present but uneven across parent genres; Thema codifies cross-genre cozy more explicitly.
3. In diagnostics, prioritize Safety Envelope, Recovery Rhythm, Belonging Engine, and Restoration Arc — the four axes with the strongest empirical support.
4. Explicitly separate `high stakes` from `high threat load`, and `sadness/grief` from `unbuffered despair`.
5. Use evidence labels in reports when citing external findings: `SOURCE-VERIFIED` vs. `MARKET INFERENCE`.
