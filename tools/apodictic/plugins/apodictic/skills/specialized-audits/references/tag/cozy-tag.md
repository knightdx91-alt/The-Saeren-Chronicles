## What Is a Tag Audit?

Tag audits evaluate **experience-layer promises** — reader expectations that sit on top of any parent genre's structural contract. Unlike genre audits (which diagnose whether structural machinery works), tag audits diagnose whether the manuscript sustains a promised *quality of experience* across its runtime.

Tags are cross-genre modifiers. Mystery, fantasy, and romance each have structural obligations (information economy, worldbuilding coherence, relationship arc). A tag like "cozy" promises something about how those structures *feel to read*. A cozy mystery still needs a fair solution. A cozy fantasy still needs functional worldbuilding. The tag doesn't replace the genre contract — it layers an experience promise on top of it.

**The tag audit family includes:** Cozy, Philosophical, Erotic, Dark, Grimdark, Literary, Hopepunk, and others as they emerge. Each follows this same structural pattern: experience model with named axes, named diagnostic flags, intensity spectrum, cross-genre calibration matrix, anchor + interacting lenses analysis method, output template, and an inline **Level-Setting Notes** section grounding the diagnostic axes in external evidence (taxonomy standards, market data, reader-psychology research). When external evidence is unusually deep, a standalone companion brief may supplement the inline section — but the inline section is the standard, not the brief.

---

## Purpose

Diagnose whether a manuscript credibly delivers a "cozy" reader experience. This audit evaluates comfort, emotional safety, belonging, and restorative payoff without requiring a specific parent genre.

**Core claim:** Cozy is not a genre. It is an experience layer that can sit on mystery, fantasy, romance, horror, SF, literary, or hybrid structures. The question is not "is this a cozy genre?" but "does this manuscript sustain cozy delivery for the target reader?"

**What this audit is:**
- A reader-experience and promise-delivery diagnostic
- A cross-genre calibration layer
- A mismatch detector between "cozy" marketing and actual page experience

**What this audit is not:**
- A replacement for parent-genre structural standards
- A mandate for low stakes or no conflict
- A requirement to remove all darkness

## Level-Setting Notes

This audit's diagnostic axes are grounded in external evidence documented in the companion brief `references/tag/cozy-tag-level-setting.md`. Key findings:

- **Taxonomy:** Both BISAC (`FIC155000`) and Thema (`FFJ`, `FMJ`, `FLJ`, `FRDJ`) now codify cozy as a cross-genre classification, validating the tag audit architecture.
- **Recovery psychology:** Experimental research supports recovery architecture (pressure + decompression + restored orientation) as the mechanism behind cozy's reader value — not low conflict per se (Rieger et al., 2014; Rieger & Bente, 2018).
- **Belonging as infrastructure:** Social connectedness research shows comfort-oriented states strengthen perceived social connection, validating the Belonging Engine axis as load-bearing craft (Wildschut et al., 2010; Juhl et al., 2021).
- **Narrative resistance:** Story forms reduce reader reactance through character involvement, not through absence of pressure. Cozy delivery is undermined by didactic pressure, not by conflict (Moyer-Gusé et al., 2010).

Use `SOURCE-VERIFIED` and `MARKET INFERENCE` labels when citing external evidence in audit reports.

## Trigger Conditions

Activate when any of the following are true:

1. Author, comps, pitch, blurb, or metadata uses "cozy" or adjacent language ("gentle," "comfort read," "soft," "warm," "feel-good").
2. Shelf and positioning work suggests cozy discoverability.
3. Beta readers report tone mismatch ("marketed as cozy, felt harsh/anxious/cold").
4. Pass 11 identifies promise-delivery risk tied to warmth/safety expectations.

Do not auto-run if cozy is never implied by author intent or market positioning.

## Cozy Intensity Spectrum

Not all cozy promises are equal. A manuscript's declared or inferred cozy intensity determines how strictly these axes apply.

**High Intensity** — The cozy experience *is* the primary reader promise. Conflict exists but is bounded, buffered, and always resolved within the comfort envelope. Violence and cruelty are off-page or absent. The social world is warm and functional. Endings are restorative without significant residual unease. *Think: cat-solves-the-crime mystery, village bakery romance, cottage-core fantasy.*

**Medium Intensity** — Cozy is a major experience promise alongside genre obligations. Real stakes are present and may produce genuine distress, but recovery beats are regular and meaningful, the belonging system is credible, and the ending restores enough order to satisfy cozy expectations. Some sharp moments are acceptable if held by the surrounding warmth. *Think: cozy fantasy with real peril but a hearth to return to, cozy mystery where the murder matters but the community holds.*

**Low Intensity** — Cozy is a tonal modifier rather than a dominant promise. The manuscript has warmth, humane framing, and restorative elements, but it doesn't promise safety from significant distress. Parent-genre obligations may produce extended discomfort. The cozy layer provides *relief* within a harder structure rather than defining the experience. *Think: a thriller with domestic warmth between set-pieces, literary fiction with gentle humor and community despite heavy themes.*

**Why this matters:** A CZ-2 (Safety Envelope Breach) that would be a Must-Fix at High intensity might be a Could-Fix at Low intensity. The spectrum prevents over-diagnosis and under-diagnosis alike.

## Cozy Delivery Model (Six Axes)

Evaluate each axis as `Strong / Mixed / Weak`, then assign named flags where needed.

### Axis A: Safety Envelope

Can the target cozy reader trust that emotional and sensory risk stays within declared bounds?

Track:
- On-page explicitness (violence, cruelty, gore, degradation)
- Threat persistence (constant dread vs. bounded pressure)
- Irreversibility load (how often outcomes feel permanently bleak)
- Consent and interpersonal safety clarity (when relevant)

**Named failure patterns:**

- **Trapdoor:** The manuscript establishes cozy trust, then drops the reader into content that violates the implied safety contract without warning or buffering. Distinct from legitimate high-stakes cozy, where stakes are declared and buffered. The trapdoor is a *surprise* breach.
- **Ratchet Without Release:** Each stress event tightens tension slightly but no individual event feels like an obvious breach. Cumulatively, the manuscript drifts out of the comfort envelope without ever crossing a single bright line. Diagnosed by tracking the stress ledger over time, not scene by scene.
- **Cruelty Leak:** Characters — especially the narrator — treat side characters with contempt, dismissiveness, or casual meanness. The social world is warm *for the protagonist* but hostile for others. This breaks cozy for attentive readers because the safety envelope applies to the *world*, not just the main character.

### Axis B: Softness Signals

Does the prose continuously supply warmth, humane attention, or gentle texture even during conflict?

Track:
- Warmth vs. abrasion in diction and imagery
- Presence of comfort anchors (food, home, ritual, humor, craft, nature, friendship)
- Tonal whiplash frequency

**Named failure patterns:**

- **Cozy Skin:** Packaging, cover, blurb, and chapter titles signal warmth, but sentence-level prose is sharp, bleak, ironic, or contemptuous. The cozy promise is marketing-deep, not prose-deep.
- **Comfort Prop:** Comfort anchors are present (the tea shop, the cat, the cozy kitchen) but they're set dressing, not load-bearing. They don't participate in emotional recovery, relationship building, or value expression. Remove them and nothing changes.
- **Warmth Collapse:** Prose sustains warmth through early and middle sections but abandons it under late-book narrative pressure. The final quarter reads like a different, harder book. Common in manuscripts that prioritize plot resolution over tonal contract.

### Axis C: Belonging Engine

Does the manuscript create social fabric the reader wants to inhabit?

The core mechanism here is what might be called a **return address** — a social-emotional place the reader can always come back to. When the return address is credible, the book can take real risks because the reader trusts that the social world will catch the characters. When it's not, even mild conflict feels unsafe because there's nowhere to land.

Track:
- Prosocial interactions (care, repair, generosity, small loyalties)
- Community legibility (recurring social nodes, not just isolated strangers)
- Relational trust trajectory
- Return address credibility

**Named failure patterns:**

- **Relational Vacuum:** Characters share scenes but not meaningful social bonds. The manuscript has a cast but not a community. No one checks on anyone. No one remembers what someone said two chapters ago.
- **Protagonist Island:** The main character has warm relationships, but the social world doesn't extend beyond them. Side characters don't have relationships with each other. The community is a spoke-and-hub around the protagonist rather than a web.
- **Unrepaired Breach:** An interpersonal rupture (betrayal, harsh words, misunderstanding) occurs and is never adequately repaired on-page. In non-cozy fiction this can be realistic. In cozy fiction it damages the return address — the reader can no longer trust the social world to hold.

### Axis D: Recovery Rhythm

After stress, does the book provide timely emotional decompression?

Track:
- Buffer lag: how long after peak stress before a recovery beat
- Recovery quality: true re-centering vs. thin exposition
- Peak clustering: repeated stress spikes without relief

**Named failure patterns:**

- **Summary Recovery:** After a stressful scene, the narrative provides a brief expository transition ("The next morning, things felt better") rather than a dramatized recovery beat with emotional texture. The manuscript *tells* the reader that recovery happened without *delivering* the recovery experience. This cheats the cozy reader of the decompression they need.
- **Adrenaline Stack:** Multiple high-intensity scenes pile up without intervening recovery. Any individual scene might be in-bounds, but the cumulative effect is exhausting rather than exciting. Diagnosed by mapping pressure/recovery rhythm across the manuscript's midsection and late corridors.
- **Fridge Recovery:** Recovery beats are present but delayed so far from the stress event that the reader has already carried the discomfort through multiple scenes. By the time recovery arrives, the damage to cozy trust is done.

### Axis E: Everyday Stakes Presence

Does ordinary life matter on-page, or does cozy language sit on top of nonstop escalation?

Track:
- Functional domestic/routine scenes (not filler)
- Small-scale concerns that carry emotional meaning
- Integration of ordinary and extraordinary pressures

**Named failure patterns:**

- **Crisis Saturation:** Every scene is a crisis scene. There is no lived-in normality. The cozy promise requires that ordinary life — cooking, walking, tending a garden, a quiet conversation — has weight and value on-page, not just as downtime between plot events.
- **Inert Domesticity:** Everyday scenes exist but they're dead weight. They don't advance relationships, express values, build attachment, or carry emotional stakes. They're "cozy" by content (tea, cats, rain on the window) but not by function. The reader skims them. This is the domestic equivalent of Comfort Prop (Axis B) but at the scene level rather than the detail level.
- **Escalation Addiction:** The manuscript starts with everyday stakes but progressively abandons them as the plot accelerates. By the final third, the ordinary world has been entirely consumed by the crisis. The return address goes dark.

### Axis F: Restoration Arc

Does the ending restore enough order, trust, or meaning to satisfy cozy expectations for this book's promise?

Track:
- Closure quality (practical and emotional)
- Repair signals (relationships, community, self-trust, moral order)
- Residual dread load in final movement

**Named failure patterns:**

- **Pyrrhic Landing:** The plot resolves but the emotional cost is too high for the declared cozy intensity. The reader gets "the mystery is solved" but also "three relationships are destroyed and the protagonist is emotionally devastated." That's a valid ending for a thriller. It's a contract breach for high-intensity cozy.
- **Dangling Thread:** Key relational or community lines are left unresolved. The murder is solved but the protagonist's rift with her sister is never addressed. For non-cozy fiction, not every thread needs tying. For cozy, unresolved relational threads undermine the restoration promise.
- **Tonal Whiplash Landing:** The ending shifts register — suddenly dark, cynical, or ambiguous in a way the rest of the manuscript hasn't prepared the reader for. The final emotional residue doesn't match the 90% of the book that preceded it.

## Cross-Genre Calibration Matrix

Use parent genre as calibration, not override.

| Parent Genre | Cozy-Compatible Profile | Typical Breach |
|--------------|-------------------------|----------------|
| Mystery | Puzzle-forward, low explicit harm, humane community texture | Graphic violence or procedural brutality dominates |
| Fantasy | Wonder + hearth + relational warmth despite conflict | Lore-heavy peril overwhelms comfort scaffolding |
| Romance | Emotional safety, relational repair, warm interpersonal field | Trust breach without sufficient repair on-page |
| Horror (cozy-adjacent) | Spooky atmosphere with bounded terror and strong buffers | Unbuffered dread, body-horror intensity, hopeless end state |
| Thriller (cozy-adjacent) | Tension with humane framing and frequent recovery beats | Continuous adrenaline with no emotional refuge |
| SF | Competence + community + humane stakes | Cold systems pressure with minimal relational warmth |
| Literary | Intimate, humane observation with restorative meaning line | Bleak detachment framed as comfort without support |

Rule: parent-genre obligations still apply. Cozy modifies reader-experience calibration; it does not erase genre contract.

## Analysis Method (Anchor + Interacting Lenses)

This audit is not a sequential checklist. After one required anchor, evaluate the manuscript through interacting lenses and synthesize patterns across them.

### Required Anchor: Cozy Claim Lock

Before analysis, state the implied cozy promise:

```
Cozy Claim (inferred): [one sentence]
Parent Genre: [primary + secondary]
Intended Cozy Intensity: [High / Medium / Low]
Key Comfort Boundaries: [what readers can trust will remain in-bounds]
```

If cozy claim cannot be stated, flag positioning ambiguity (`CZ-8` likely).

### Core Lenses (Run Iteratively, Not Sequentially)

Use all lenses on the same scene set and update judgments as cross-evidence appears.

#### Lens 1: Promise Signal and Opening Trust

Focus: First 10% contract legibility.

Checks:
- Is the warmth/safety promise legible early?
- Are conflict signals present without overwhelming comfort cues?
- Does opening tone align with cozy-adjacent packaging language?
- Is the return address established (or at least previewed) before the first significant stress event?

Failure tendency: early pages project a harsher contract than the marketed/stated one (`CZ-1`, `CZ-8`).

#### Lens 2: Safety-Recovery Coupling

Focus: Whether stress intensity is matched by proportional decompression.

Checks:
- Log stress events as `In-Bounds / Edge / Out-of-Bounds` relative to the declared cozy intensity
- For `Edge`/`Out-of-Bounds`, track whether recovery appears within 1-2 scenes
- Distinguish true recovery (dramatized, emotionally textured) from Summary Recovery
- Watch for Ratchet Without Release (cumulative drift out of the envelope)

Failure tendency: technically in-bounds content but systemic recovery lag (`CZ-2`, `CZ-3`).

#### Lens 3: Belonging-Everyday Coupling

Focus: Whether social and ordinary-life scaffolding actually carries emotional load.

Checks:
- Are relationships sites of care and repair, not only conflict?
- Do everyday scenes carry stakes, attachment, or value conflict?
- Is the return address credible and functional?
- Are domestic/routine scenes load-bearing or Inert Domesticity?

Failure tendency: decorative coziness (tea/shops/vibes) without relational security (`CZ-4`, `CZ-6`).

#### Lens 4: Softness Stability Under Pressure

Focus: Sentence-level and scene-level tonal behavior during conflict peaks.

Checks:
- Warmth vs. abrasion in diction during the hardest scenes
- Contempt/scorn leakage in narration (Cruelty Leak)
- Tonal whiplash frequency across act transitions
- Whether the prose voice itself stays in the cozy register even when the plot doesn't

Failure tendency: Cozy Skin over abrasive tonal core (`CZ-5`).

#### Lens 5: Restoration and Aftertaste

Focus: Ending residue relative to declared cozy intensity.

Checks:
- Is disorder sufficiently transformed by the end?
- Are relational and emotional lines restored to the promised comfort band?
- Is lingering unease proportionate or contract-breaking?
- Are there Dangling Threads that undermine the restoration promise?

Failure tendency: last-act acceleration strips away cozy scaffolding (`CZ-7`).

#### Lens 6: Parent-Genre Pressure Test

Focus: Differentiate legitimate parent-genre pressure from true cozy breach.

Checks:
- Does the book meet parent-genre obligations while preserving cozy delivery?
- Are intense beats buffered rather than removed?
- Is this a "cozy modifier on a genre" or a "genre label on a non-cozy book"?
- Where a parent-genre beat breaches cozy, is the breach necessary for the genre contract or just habit?

Failure tendency: category confusion where cozy is used as label but not delivered (`CZ-8`).

### Interaction Patterns (Where Diagnosis Becomes Accurate)

Prioritize cross-lens patterns over any single metric:

- **High stakes + strong buffering + credible return address + restorative end** = cozy at Medium or Low intensity. Not a failure.
- **Low explicitness + weak belonging + no recovery** = cozy failure despite surface safety.
- **Warm opening + harsh middle/late collapse** = Promise Drift even if early pages pass.
- **Single sharp tonal spike + immediate humane recovery** = usually not a breach.
- **Cozy signifiers present + contempt tone persistent** = Cozy Skin. Substantive mismatch.
- **Strong return address + occasional Edge stress + reliable recovery** = the signature of well-executed medium-intensity cozy. Protect this pattern.
- **Everyday scenes present but inert + recovery present but summary** = the manuscript has cozy *furniture* without cozy *function*. Both Inert Domesticity and Summary Recovery point to the same underlying problem: the author knows cozy needs certain scene types but hasn't made them do emotional work.

### Minimum Evidence Packet

For a full run, sample at least:
- Opening corridor (first 10%)
- One midpoint pressure corridor
- One late-book corridor
- Ending movement

Minimum reference density: 2-4 scene/page references per severe flag.

## Cozy Flag Family (CZ Codes)

| Code | Flag | Named Patterns | Severity Guidance |
|------|------|----------------|-------------------|
| `CZ-1` | Promise Drift | Warmth Collapse | Should-Fix or Must-Fix (if packaging explicitly says cozy) |
| `CZ-2` | Safety Envelope Breach | Trapdoor, Ratchet Without Release, Cruelty Leak | Must-Fix when repeated or severe |
| `CZ-3` | Recovery Deficit | Summary Recovery, Adrenaline Stack, Fridge Recovery | Should-Fix; Must-Fix if systemic |
| `CZ-4` | Belonging Vacuum | Relational Vacuum, Protagonist Island, Unrepaired Breach | Should-Fix |
| `CZ-5` | Tonal Abrasion Cluster | Cozy Skin | Should-Fix |
| `CZ-6` | Ordinary-Life Absence | Crisis Saturation, Inert Domesticity, Escalation Addiction | Could-Fix or Should-Fix depending on promise |
| `CZ-7` | Non-Restorative Ending | Pyrrhic Landing, Dangling Thread, Tonal Whiplash Landing | Must-Fix for high/medium cozy claims |
| `CZ-8` | Cozy Label Mismatch | (market-level flag, no named pattern) | Must-Fix for market positioning use |

For each CZ flag include:
- Named pattern (if applicable)
- Evidence (2-4 scene/page references)
- Confidence (High/Medium/Low)
- Reader impact if unfixed

Severity calibrates to declared cozy intensity: a CZ-2 that's Must-Fix at High may be Should-Fix at Low.

## Common False Positives and Misreads

### Not a Cozy Failure

- Presence of conflict by itself
- High stakes when emotional and sensory risk is clearly bounded
- Presence of sadness or grief when buffered, witnessed, and meaningfully held
- One sharp tonal beat in an otherwise warm manuscript
- Moderate or even high peril with consistent recovery and humane framing
- A strong antagonist if the return address remains intact
- A fast climax if followed by sufficient repair/restoration beats
- Mystery/quest urgency if ordinary-life and belonging beats still recur

### High-Stakes Cozy (Common Misread)

High stakes do not automatically negate cozy delivery. This is the most frequent misdiagnosis in beta reader feedback and the most common reason authors are told to "lower the stakes" when the real problem is elsewhere.

Cozy fails when **buffering and restoration** fail, not when stakes are high. A cozy fantasy where the kingdom is genuinely threatened can absolutely deliver cozy — if:
- Stakes are real but explicitness is bounded
- Recovery beats are regular and emotionally textured (not Summary Recovery)
- The return address remains credible throughout
- The ending restores order/trust to the declared comfort band

The diagnostic question isn't "are the stakes too high?" but "does the manuscript's support system hold under the stakes it's chosen?"

When beta readers report "this didn't feel cozy enough" about a high-stakes manuscript, check Recovery Rhythm and Belonging Engine before recommending lower stakes. The problem is almost always in the buffering, not the stakes themselves.

### True Cozy Risk Often Missed

- **Comfort Prop without function:** Cozy signifiers (tea, bookshops, cats, rain) present as decoration rather than as sites of recovery, belonging, or value expression.
- **Cruelty Leak:** Narrator or protagonist treats side characters with casual contempt. Warm for the inner circle, cold for everyone else.
- **Summary Recovery:** Recovery beats exist but they're expository transitions, not dramatized emotional experiences.
- **Boundary Uncertainty:** Reader cannot predict what "safe enough" means in this manuscript because the safety envelope isn't legible.
- **Inert Domesticity:** Everyday scenes are present but carry no emotional stakes, no relationship work, no value expression. They're cozy by content but dead by function.
- **Unrepaired Breach:** Emotional or relational damage is acknowledged but never repaired on-page. The return address has a crack in it.
- **Late-book warmth abandonment:** Cozy scaffolding disappears when the plot accelerates in the final act. The ending may be satisfactory on plot terms but the cozy contract has already been broken by the time it arrives.

## Lightweight Scoring (Optional)

Use only if needed for triage clarity.

Score each axis A-F:
- `2 = Strong`
- `1 = Mixed`
- `0 = Weak`

Total (0-12):
- `10-12`: Strong Cozy Delivery
- `7-9`: Partial Cozy Delivery
- `0-6`: Cozy Mismatch

Hard gates override total score:
- Repeated `CZ-2` or `CZ-7` caps outcome at `Partial` or lower.
- `CZ-8` with explicit cozy packaging prevents "Strong" result.

Severity of all flags calibrates to declared cozy intensity.

## Firewall Compliance

Allowed:
- Diagnose cozy delivery and mismatch
- Flag promise breaches with named patterns
- Prioritize revision targets by reader impact

Forbidden:
- Inventing scenes to "make it cozier"
- Writing replacement prose
- Prescribing new content events

This audit identifies where cozy promise holds or breaks. The author chooses the creative solution.
