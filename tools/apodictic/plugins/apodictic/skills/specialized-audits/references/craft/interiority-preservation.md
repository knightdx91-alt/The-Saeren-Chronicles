# Specialized Audit: Interiority Preservation in High-Intensity Scenes

**The issue: when does POV interiority disappear during scenes of peak physical or psychological intensity?**

**Deficit-First Diagnostic Rule:** Do not validate interiority preservation by confirming cognitive presence in quiet moments. You must hunt for the *absence* of interiority under high structural load. If a character becomes an empty vessel or a mechanical agent precisely when the plot demands action, it fails interiority preservation. You are auditing for the omission of consciousness.

This applies across genres and scene types — combat, intimate encounter, interrogation, crisis, confrontation. The POV character may stop having internal experience and become a camera or action-delivery mechanism.

This applies symmetrically in intimate scenes:
- Male POV observing female body → appropriate if he maintains his interiority
- Female POV observing male body → she should have internal experience, not disappear into describing him
- Any POV during sex → should maintain sensation, thought, psychological presence

## Track

- Does POV character maintain internal experience during high-intensity scenes?
- When observing another person closely, is that observation *in character* (filtered through this person's psychology)?
- If interiority disappears, is it intentional (dissociation, tunnel vision, trance, performance-mode) or accidental?

## Detect

- POV interiority loss during climactic scenes (character becomes agent without sensation or thought)
- Observation that feels authorial rather than character-driven
- Interiority that disappears during the intensity and returns only after it ends

## Distinguish

- **Intentional interiority loss:** Dissociation, tunnel vision, shock, trance, performance-mode — psychologically valid and can be powerful if the text signals the absence (flag for reader if thematic)
- **Accidental interiority loss:** POV character just stops having internal experience because the author is focused on describing external action (problem)
- **Character-filtered observation:** POV character observing another person closely in ways that reveal the observer's psychology (a soldier assessing threat, a lover noticing vulnerability, a detective reading micro-expressions) — this IS interiority, expressed through attention

## Genre-Specific Applications

- **Action/Thriller:** Combat scenes where the protagonist becomes a choreography report → see **Force Architecture** audit (Disembodied Combatant flag)
- **Romance/Erotic:** Intimate scenes where the POV character disappears into describing the other person's body → see **Erotic Content tag** (EC-8: Vanishing Interiority) and **Romance genre module**
- **Horror:** Terror scenes where fear is described rather than experienced → see **Horror Craft** audit
- **Literary:** Any scene where the narrator's consciousness goes quiet during the moment that matters most
