# Specialized Audit: Literary Craft Deep Dive
## Version 0.4.9
*Last Updated: February 2026*
*Synthesized from three-model independent drafting (Opus, Codex, Gemini)*

---

## Purpose

This audit evaluates whether a manuscript's literary-mode ambitions do structural work — generating meaning, shaping experience, constraining narrative possibility — or whether the literary apparatus is cosmetic sophistication applied to a story that would function the same without it.

**The core problem is not complexity. It is ornamentation.** A manuscript can have beautiful prose, sophisticated themes, unusual structure, and dense image systems that all sit on top of the story rather than constituting it. The literary mode becomes a veneer — expensive wallpaper over genre drywall. The style impresses but doesn't do work. The themes are present but not embodied. The structure is unusual but not meaningful.

The audit's fundamental question: **Does the literary apparatus do narrative work, or could you strip it back to competent genre prose without changing what the reader understands, feels, or discovers?**

### Anchor insight

Literary mode that doesn't do work is structurally identical to worldbuilding that doesn't do work. A prose style that could be swapped for a plainer style without changing the reader's experience is the literary-mode equivalent of a magic system that could be swapped for a different one without changing the story. In both cases, the craft labor is real but disconnected from meaning. The mechanism is beautiful. It just doesn't matter.

This is a craft audit, not a taste test. Minimalist prose operating in literary mode (Carver, Johnson, Offill, Berlin) is within scope alongside maximalist prose (Pynchon, Wallace, Rushdie). The question is never whether the prose is complex enough. It is whether the literary choices — whatever they are — earn their place by doing work that competent non-literary prose couldn't.

**This is a cross-genre audit.** It applies wherever literary mode operates: literary fiction, literary thrillers, literary SFF, literary horror, literary crime, upmarket fiction, and any manuscript where the *how* of the telling is part of the *what*. It is not limited to capital-L Literary Fiction as a shelf category.

## Activation and Boundaries

### Literary-mode activation rubric

Activate this audit when at least **2 of 5** indicators are present, or when user explicitly requests it.

| Indicator | Trigger evidence |
|---|---|
| Style claim | Manuscript foregrounds voice, syntax, diction, or sentence-level craft as central value |
| Formal claim | Nonlinear, fragmentary, polyphonic, or otherwise self-conscious structure |
| Thematic density claim | Persistent thematic motifs/questions beyond plot function |
| Interpretive ambition claim | Ambiguity, indirection, open-ended recognition as intentional target |
| Market/positioning claim | Labeled literary/upmarket/literary-hybrid in metadata, query, or comps |

If 0–1 indicators present, do not run this audit by default.

### Relationship to adjacent systems

**Literary Fiction genre calibration** (core SKILL.md): Genre calibration asks shelf-contract questions. This audit provides deeper mechanism-level diagnostics applicable across genres. The genre calibration's Thematic Embodiment Audit, Interiority Function Audit, and "Nothing Happens" Assessment are precursors; this audit provides the full diagnostic apparatus.

**AI-Prose Calibration**: AI-Prose flags generic fluency and voice singularity. This audit flags literary-ambition disconnection, regardless of provenance. A manuscript can pass AI-Prose (voice is distinctive, prose has friction) and fail this audit (the distinctive voice isn't doing work).

**Emotional Craft**: Emotional Craft diagnoses transmission pipeline failures. This audit diagnoses literary apparatus disconnection, including cases where emotion transmits but apparatus is ornamental.

**SFF Worldbuilding Integration**: Structural sibling audit. Both diagnose real craft labor disconnected from narrative load-bearing function.

### When to activate

- Any manuscript that claims literary-mode ambitions through prose style, structural sophistication, or thematic density
- Work where beta readers say "beautiful writing but I don't care" or "I can tell it's good but it's not landing"
- Literary fiction where craft quality is high but emotional impact is low
- Genre fiction (thriller, SFF, horror, crime, romance) operating in literary mode
- Work where the prose draws attention to itself and the question is whether that attention is earned
- Any time an author asks "I know the writing is strong — why isn't the story working?"

### Exclusions

Do not run when: (1) manuscript is pre-draft ideation only, (2) structural coherence is not yet established, (3) author does not claim literary-mode burden and does not request this audit.

## The Literary Mode Framework

Literary mode is not a genre. It is a set of craft commitments — to prose as architecture, to thematic integration through form, to image and tonal systems that accumulate meaning, to a trust relationship with the reader that operates through implication rather than statement. These commitments can operate in any genre. They can also fail in any genre, in specific and diagnosable ways.

### Five Integration Dimensions + One Cross-Cutting

Assess five primary dimensions, plus one cross-cutting dimension.

**1. PA: Prose Architecture**
Style as load-bearing structure. Sentence-level decisions that constrain what can be said, shape how meaning is received, and produce effects that competent non-literary prose couldn't achieve. Includes rhythm, diction, syntax, figurative language, and the relationship between sentence form and sentence content.

*Load-Bearing when:* Sentence decisions encode perception and value pressure. Syntactic rhythm and diction alter interpretation. Simplification would reduce thematic or emotional precision. The prose style could not be simplified without losing meaning.

*Ornamental when:* Style mainly beautifies familiar content. Prose can be paraphrased plain with little loss. Stylistic peaks are disconnected from scene pressure.

**2. TF: Thematic Integration Through Form**
When structure IS meaning. The narrative's arrangement — chronology, POV architecture, white space, chapter logic — embodies its thematic argument rather than merely containing it.

*Load-Bearing when:* Form creates meanings unavailable to linear conventional telling. Arrangement of information produces thematic discovery. Endings complete or complicate form-generated meaning.

*Ornamental when:* Theme is discussed, not enacted. Formal novelty is descriptive metadata, not experiential necessity. Structure can be normalized without interpretive loss.

**3. IA: Image Architecture**
Recurring images, motifs, and sensory patterns that do thematic work across the manuscript. How sensory patterns accumulate meaning and shift as the manuscript's thematic argument develops.

*Load-Bearing when:* Images recur with altered value under new context. Motif network tracks character/thematic transformation. Image shifts generate inference beyond explicit statement.

*Ornamental when:* Symbols recur mechanically. Images remain static in meaning. Striking imagery never returns or compounds.

**4. ST: Subtext and Tonal Control**
The ratio of said to unsaid. The precision of the emotional register. The degree to which the manuscript trusts the reader to infer meaning from juxtaposition, omission, and implication rather than from statement.

*Load-Bearing when:* Inference gaps are interpretable. Tonal modulation aligns with narrative pressure. Exposition does not collapse emergent subtext.

*Ornamental when:* Ambiguity produces vacuum. Tone drifts without structural cause. Narrative repeatedly explains what scenes already imply.

**5. RA: Recognition Architecture**
The distinctive payoff of literary mode — moments where the reader suddenly understands something about human experience that they couldn't have articulated before reading. Recognition is not the same as information or emotional impact. It is the sensation of *seeing* something clearly for the first time, achieved through accumulated craft pressure rather than direct statement.

*Load-Bearing when:* Insight arrives through accumulation. Recognition is non-generic and surprising in form. Setup-pressure-release sequence supports insight credibility.

*Ornamental when:* Insight is delivered as lecture. Recognition remains truism-level. Revelation appears without architecture.

**6. VA: Voice Architecture (cross-cutting)**
Voice as worldview engine, not just a sound profile. This dimension cuts across all others — voice decisions shape what can be known, what can be said, and what ethical stance the narrative takes. When voice is load-bearing, the narrator's way of seeing *is* the work's meaning.

*Load-Bearing when:* Stance and attention choices shape ethics and meaning. Register shifts are motivated and strategic. Narrator distance is controlled and consistent with burden.

*Ornamental when:* Voice mimics prestige sound without stance coherence. Tonal posture substitutes for argument. Irony/lyricism blocks consequence access.

### Integration Levels

Rate each primary dimension as:

| Level | Operational definition |
|---|---|
| **Load-Bearing** | Removing the element alters meaning, pressure, or recognition architecture. Dimension repeatedly generates meaning in high-load scenes. |
| **Partially Integrated** | Element contributes but can be reduced without core collapse. Functions in some scenes but not others. |
| **Ornamental** | Element decorates surface while core interpretation remains unchanged. Present but removable without loss. |

### The Defamiliarization Test

The single most useful diagnostic question, adapted from Shklovsky: **Does this literary element make the reader see something they couldn't see without it, or does it make the reader admire the writing?**

If the prose, the structure, the image system, or the tonal register is producing *perception* — making the reader experience something familiar as if encountering it for the first time — the literary mode is doing its job. If it's producing *appreciation* — the reader notices the craft and admires it without being changed by it — the mode is ornamental.

This is the literary-mode equivalent of the SFF worldbuilding audit's inhabitation test. A world that the reader admires but doesn't inhabit is an integration failure. A literary style that the reader admires but isn't changed by is the same failure in a different register.

## Named Diagnostic Flags

Each flag identifies a specific pattern of literary-mode failure. Flags can co-occur. Severity depends on frequency, pattern, and the manuscript's implied contract.

### PA Flags

**PA-1: Verbal Wallpaper**
Beautiful prose that could be replaced by competent prose without changing meaning, tension, or character access. The sentences are lovely but interchangeable — swap any passage with a plainly written equivalent and the reader's understanding is identical. Style as decoration rather than architecture.
- *Detect:* Select five prose passages the manuscript seems proud of. Rewrite each in plain English. What is lost? If only aesthetic pleasure is lost — no meaning, no tension shift, no character access — the prose is wallpaper.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Must-Fix if the literary contract promises prose as a primary experience.

**PA-2: Register Cosplay**
The prose imitates a recognizable literary register without inhabiting it. The author is wearing the voice, not speaking in it. The imitation is competent but the register doesn't emerge from the material; it's applied to the material.
- *Detect:* Can you name the literary influence within a page? Does the borrowed register generate effects specific to *this* material, or could it be applied to any subject? If the register is immediately identifiable as belonging to someone else and doesn't generate material-specific effects, it's cosplay.
- *Default severity:* Could-Fix isolated, Should-Fix repeated.

**PA-3: Purple Archipelago**
Literary-mode prose appears in set-piece passages — opening paragraphs, landscape descriptions, emotional peaks — but the connective tissue between them is workmanlike. Literary ambition is intermittent rather than architectural. The manuscript has two voices: the show voice and the getting-things-done voice.
- *Detect:* Map prose intensity across the manuscript. Is literary-mode prose clustered in predictable locations? Do dialogue scenes, transitional passages, and plot-mechanics scenes drop to a different register? If the two registers don't feel like intentional modulation, the literary mode is archipelagic.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix.

**PA-4: Difficulty Without Reward**
Demanding prose — long sentences, unusual syntax, dense allusion, neologism — where the difficulty doesn't pay off in meaning, experience, or surprise. The reader works hard for nothing.
- *Detect:* Identify the manuscript's most demanding passages. What does the difficulty *produce* that easier prose couldn't? If the answer is "a sense of literary seriousness" rather than a specific effect, the difficulty is unrewarding.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Must-Fix if sustained and contract-damaging.

**PA-5: Workshop Finish**
Prose that is technically accomplished — varied sentence lengths, active verbs, concrete sensory detail, no clichés — but sounds like everyone and no one. The voice is competent literary default: the product of knowing what not to do without arriving at what *to* do. The prose is correct but has no signature.
- *Detect:* Could passages from this manuscript be swapped into another literary novel without detection? If the voice is distinguished only by what it avoids rather than what it does, it's a workshop finish.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Could-Fix if voice specificity is strong in character-driven passages but weak in narration.
- *Cross-reference:* AI-Prose Calibration AIC-1 (Voice Singularity). The workshop finish is the human-authored version of the same problem.

### TF Flags

**TF-1: Thesis Statement Novel**
Theme is present, articulable, and consistently referenced — but never embodied in form or structure. The novel is *about* something; it never *is* something. Characters discuss the theme. The narrator reflects on it. But the narrative's formal choices don't enact it. Theme as content rather than architecture.
- *Detect:* State the manuscript's central theme in one sentence. Does the narrative *form* embody this theme, or does the narrative *content* contain it? If the structure, POV, and prose rhythm would work equally well for a different theme, the theme is content-level only.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Must-Fix if the manuscript's contract promises thematic sophistication as a primary experience.

**TF-2: Structural Stunt**
Unusual narrative structure that doesn't generate meaning. The structure is interesting to describe but doesn't change what the reader experiences. The conceit is the pitch, not the point.
- *Detect:* Mentally reassemble the narrative in conventional form. Does anything important change? If the reader's understanding and emotional experience would be substantially equivalent, the structure is a stunt.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix.

**TF-3: Thematic Furniture**
Symbols, motifs, and image systems that are present but not load-bearing. They recur without accumulating. Remove them and the story works the same.
- *Detect:* Catalog recurring images and motifs. Does each *develop* — acquiring new meanings, shifting in valence, complicating the initial association? Or does it simply repeat, restating the same thematic note? If the images are static, they're furniture.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Could-Fix if underdeveloped rather than structurally inert.

**TF-4: Interpretive Vacuum**
The manuscript refuses resolution in ways that don't create productive ambiguity but instead leave the reader with nothing to interpret. Ambiguity without stakes.
- *Detect:* At the point of greatest ambiguity, what competing interpretations does the text support? If you can't articulate at least two substantive, text-supported readings in tension with each other, the ambiguity is a vacuum. Productive ambiguity gives the reader *too much* to think about; the vacuum gives them nothing.
- *Default severity:* Must-Fix when ending-level. Should-Fix otherwise.

### IA Flags

**IA-1: Dead Metaphor Garden**
Image system relies on default, culturally pre-decoded symbolic mappings without friction. The imagery fails to trigger defamiliarization.
- *Detect:* For each major image or metaphor, is the image-meaning relationship conventional or surprising? If you could predict the symbolic meaning from the image alone, the garden is dead.
- *Default severity:* Could-Fix local, Should-Fix patterned.

**IA-2: Orphan Image**
Striking images that appear once and never return. Literary energy spent without structural payoff.
- *Detect:* High-salience images receive no recurrence or variation. Removal does not alter later interpretation.
- *Default severity:* Could-Fix isolated, Should-Fix repeated.

**IA-3: Symbol Tyranny**
Symbolic system overdetermines meaning and suppresses inference. Motifs are repeatedly decoded by narrator. The reader decodes rather than experiences.
- *Detect:* Imagery leaves low interpretive openness. Symbols announce rather than accrue.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix.

**IA-4: Sensory Wallpaper**
Rich sensory description disconnected from character psychology, thematic development, or narrative pressure. Beautiful for its own sake.
- *Detect:* Sensory detail does not alter decision or emotion interpretation. Descriptive passages can move without consequence. Sensory mode is ornamental rather than diagnostic of POV state.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix.

### ST Flags

**ST-1: Transparent Character**
Character interiority states everything the reader should understand. No gap between what's shown and what's meant. No space for inference.
- *Detect:* In key psychological moments, what does the reader understand that the text hasn't stated? If the answer is "nothing," the character is transparent.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Could-Fix if limited to secondary characters.
- *Cross-reference:* Emotional Craft audit — the meaning pipeline may be intact but operating without compression.

**ST-2: Explanatory Impulse**
After a scene that works through implication, the narrative explains what just happened. Subtext is created and then immediately excavated. The manuscript doesn't trust the reader.
- *Detect:* Identify strongest moments of implication. Is there a reflective passage within two pages that explains what the scene meant? If the pattern is consistent (imply → explain), the impulse is structural.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Must-Fix if systematic and the explanations flatten consistently effective subtext.

**ST-3: Tonal Drift**
The manuscript's emotional register shifts without narrative justification. Not intentional modulation but loss of control. The author isn't sure what the scene should feel like.
- *Detect:* Track the dominant emotional register scene by scene. Are shifts motivated by narrative events, or do they reflect uncertainty? Unmotivated tonal shifts often cluster in scenes with competing genre obligations.
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Must-Fix if tonal control is part of the manuscript's literary contract.

**ST-4: Ironic Collapse**
Sustained ironic distance that prevents the narrative from ever arriving at genuine emotion. The voice is always slightly above the material. Smart but never vulnerable. Irony as defense mechanism rather than craft tool.
- *Detect:* Identify the manuscript's most emotionally demanding moments. Does the voice maintain ironic distance through these moments? If the prose can't drop its guard when the story demands it, irony has collapsed into self-protection.
- *Default severity:* Could-Fix if intentional and bounded. Must-Fix if systemic and contract-damaging.

### RA Flags

**RA-1: Near Miss**
The manuscript approaches recognition moments — the reader senses something forming — but deflects, abstracts, or rushes past them.
- *Detect:* Identify moments where narrative builds toward insight. Does the scene stay in the pressure long enough for recognition to arrive? Or does it release prematurely — through scene break, plot interruption, or pivot to action?
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Must-Fix if systematic.

**RA-2: Generic Recognition**
The moment of understanding arrives but isn't specific. "People are contradictory." "Love is complicated." Recognition without surprise.
- *Detect:* State the insight in one sentence. Is it specific to *this* story, *these* characters, *this* situation? Or is it a general truth that the narrative merely illustrates?
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix.

**RA-3: Insight Lecture**
Recognition is delivered through character reflection or narrator commentary rather than earned through accumulated dramatic pressure. The character has the insight *for* the reader rather than the reader having it *through* the character.
- *Detect:* At moments of thematic arrival, did the reader *discover* this through accumulated detail, or were they *told* it through reflection?
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Must-Fix if the insight lecture replaces the manuscript's climactic recognition.

**RA-4: Recognition Without Preparation**
A powerful insight arrives without the structural preparation that would make it feel discovered rather than asserted. The insight is real but weightless.
- *Detect:* Trace the climactic recognition backward. What specific scenes, images, and dramatic encounters prepare the reader? If fewer than three specific elements demonstrably build toward the recognition, it's underprepared.
- *Default severity:* Must-Fix when climax-linked.

### VA Flags (cross-cutting)

**VA-1: Voice as Veneer**
Voice has audible style but weak worldview logic. Stance shifts with convenience rather than ethics. Stylistic persona floats above scene consequence.
- *Detect:* Does voice constrain interpretation? Do stance and attention choices shape meaning, or is the voice a sound layer applied over events?
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix.

**VA-2: Workshop Neutral**
Prose is highly competent and balanced but low-distinctiveness and low-risk. Sentence variety is controlled but predictable. Diction avoids commitment to sharp perspective.
- *Detect:* Comparable passages from different scenes/characters are near-interchangeable. Voice is distinguished by avoidance rather than commitment.
- *Default severity:* Could-Fix if local, Should-Fix manuscript-wide.

**VA-3: Posture Mismatch**
Narrative stance conflicts with manuscript's thematic burden. Playful/detached voice narrates material requiring grave precision, or inverse. Tonal ethics and thematic ethics diverge persistently.
- *Detect:* Does the voice's ethical stance match what the material demands? Does stance undermine intended recognition?
- *Default severity:* Should-Fix. Must-Fix if end-state meaning is destabilized.

### Pattern interpretation protocol

After artifacts are complete, run:

1. **Load-bearing ratio check:** what % of elements are ornamental?
2. **Act continuity check:** does literary integration hold under rising pressure?
3. **Channel concentration check:** which dimensions carry most failures?
4. **Ending integrity check:** are RA and TF load-bearing in final movement?
5. **Contract-fit check:** literary claim level vs delivered load-bearing level.

### Reading the Map

- **LB across dimensions and acts** → Fully integrated literary mode. Rare and excellent.
- **LB in Prose, O in Thematic Form and Recognition** → Beautiful Vessel. Gorgeous prose carrying thin ideas.
- **LB in Thematic Form, O in Prose** → Smart Chassis. Strong structural ideas in undistinguished prose.
- **LB in Act I, declining through Acts II–III** → The Opening Fade. Literary ambition front-loaded, then abandoned when plot mechanics take over.
- **LB in emotional peaks only, O in connective tissue** → Purple Archipelago pattern. Literary mode is event-driven rather than architectural.
- **O across all dimensions** → Cosmetic Literary Mode. The apparatus is entirely decorative.
- **Consistent PI across everything** → The Promising Draft. Literary ambitions are real and partially realized but need deepening.

## Severity Hard Gates

These gates improve reproducibility. If triggered, minimum severity applies regardless of local strengths.

**Gate 1: Ornamental Majority**
Trigger: More than 60% of mapped literary elements rated Ornamental.
Minimum severity: Must-Fix (Systemic).

**Gate 2: Ending Recognition Failure**
Trigger: RA-3 or RA-4 controls final recognition movement.
Minimum severity: Must-Fix.

**Gate 3: Form Non-Necessity**
Trigger: Form Necessity Test fails for principal formal device and TF-2 fires in 2+ acts.
Minimum severity: Should-Fix. Upgrade to Must-Fix if ending interpretation depends on failed device.

**Gate 4: Pressure Collapse**
Trigger: PA or ST channels drop from Load-Bearing/Partial to Ornamental across Act III high-load scenes.
Minimum severity: Should-Fix.

**Gate 5: Hybrid Contract Breach**
Trigger: Literary apparatus repeatedly undermines parent-genre core function in crossover manuscript.
Minimum severity: Must-Fix for commercial-readiness claims, Should-Fix otherwise.

**Gate 6: Thesis Over Embodiment**
Trigger: TF-1 + TF-3 persist across all acts in theme-forward manuscript.
Minimum severity: Should-Fix. Must-Fix if theme claim is primary market positioning.

**Gate 7: Voice-Meaning Decoupling**
Trigger: VA-1 + VA-3 repeated in major turning scenes.
Minimum severity: Should-Fix.

**Gate 8: Recognition Genericity Cluster**
Trigger: RA-1 + RA-2 appear in 3+ recognition candidates with no successful high-specificity landing.
Minimum severity: Should-Fix.

## The Distinguish Framework

Literary restraint, ambiguity, and experiment are valid modes. Not all simple prose is a failure. Not all conventional structure is stunt-avoidance. The audit must distinguish between craft that's ornamental and craft that's purposefully minimal.

### Distinguish classes

1. **Intentional Restraint** (Pass)
2. **Intentional Difficulty** (Pass with note)
3. **Developmental Ambiguity** (Conditional)
4. **Ornamental Detachment** (Fail)

### Required distinguish tests

Apply all tests before final classification:

1. **Style Swap Test:** Can prose be simplified without major interpretive loss?
2. **Form Necessity Test:** Would conventional structure produce equivalent meaning?
3. **Inference Productivity Test:** Do omissions generate constrained inference, not vacuum?
4. **Recognition Specificity Test:** Are recognition moments specific, surprising, and context-bound?
5. **Pressure Durability Test:** Do devices remain functional at midpoint, crisis, and ending?
6. **Hybrid Contract Compatibility Test:** In cross-genre manuscripts, does literary apparatus support, not sabotage, parent-genre function?

### Decision matrix

| Test profile | Classification | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Swap fails, necessity high, inference productive, recognition specific, pressure durable | Intentional Restraint/Difficulty | Pass with calibration notes |
| Mixed results, partial necessity, localized failures | Developmental Ambiguity | Targeted Could/Should revisions |
| Swap passes, necessity low, inference vacuum, pressure collapse | Ornamental Detachment | Should/Must by hard gates |

### False-positive guardrails

Before calling systemic ornamentation, confirm:
1. You did not apply maximalist standards to minimalist craft. Restraint can be load-bearing.
2. You assessed patterns across the manuscript, not single weak passages.
3. You measured what the literary element *produces* (meaning, perception, experience), not what it *looks like* (beautiful, complex, unusual).
4. You checked genre-contract calibration before calling literary elements ornamental.
5. Ambiguity is evaluated for interpretive productivity, not penalized by default.

### Special Caution Zones

Require stronger evidence before calling systemic failure:
- **Literary genre hybrids** — The literary mode may be doing different work than in pure literary fiction. Calibrate to the hybrid's promises, not to literary fiction standards.
- **Debut manuscripts** — Literary ambition that's partially realized is a revision opportunity, not a craft sentence. The impulse is right; the execution needs development.
- **Translated work** — Prose architecture flags must account for translation. A translator's style may be partially ornamental even when the original's style is load-bearing. Flag with caution.
- **Lyric novels and prose poetry** — These forms sometimes prioritize accumulation and atmosphere over conventional narrative recognition. Evaluate on their own terms before applying recognition-architecture flags.

## Genre-Hybrid Calibration

Literary mode operates differently in different genre contexts. Each hybrid has its own characteristic failure pattern and its own standard for what counts as ornamental.

| Genre Hybrid | Named Risk | Core Failure | Tighten Severity On | Loosen Severity On |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Literary Realist | **Elegant Plateau** | Scene competence without escalating pressure | RA, ST, PA durability | Plot-complexity demands |
| Literary Thriller | **Velvet Brake** | Literary apparatus stops the thriller's clock without generating insight | PA under pressure, hybrid compatibility | High device density in low-load scenes |
| Literary SFF | **Metaphor Shelter** | Beautiful prose masking worldbuilding that doesn't integrate with character or theme | TF, RA, cross-audit with world integration | Hard-rule exposition demands |
| Literary Horror | **Beautiful Rot** | Literary distance prevents fully experiencing the horror | ST, RA, ethical stance | Explanatory closure demands |
| Literary Crime | **Case and Chorus Split** | Investigation and reflection run in parallel, not integration | TF, RA, voice stance coherence | Procedural detail count |
| Upmarket/Domestic | **Competent Fog** | Prose competence and thematic presence without depth in either | RA specificity, ST inference | Formal experimental burden |
| Experimental/Lyric | **Structural Stunt** (elevated risk) | Form as container rather than experience engine | Form necessity, recognition yield | Linear clarity obligations |
| Literary Historical | **Period Piece** | Gorgeous historical setting and prose without thematic integration through form | Thematic relevance of historical material, recognition that connects past to present | Period-appropriate style, research-rich texture |
| Literary Memoir/CNF | **Beautiful Recollection** | Lyric prose rendering experience without transforming it through structure or insight | Recognition architecture, structural meaning, subtext between recollected and recollecting self | Lyric prose style, non-linear chronology as memory logic |

### Calibration principles

1. Tighten criteria at high-load scenes first.
2. Loosen only on burden types not promised by contract.
3. Never loosen on ending-level recognition integrity.
4. Do not ask every hybrid for the same literary burden. Ask: What literary claim is being made? Where in the manuscript is that claim most burdened? Does the apparatus hold under those loads?

## What This Audit Is Not

1. **Not a demand for complexity.** Simple prose, conventional structure, and straightforward narration can be fully load-bearing literary craft. The question is whether the choices do work, not whether they're elaborate.

2. **Not a taste test.** The audit does not prefer one literary register over another. Maximalism, minimalism, lyric prose, spare prose, experimental structure, conventional structure — all can be load-bearing or ornamental. The audit measures integration, not style.

3. **Not a prohibition on beauty.** Beautiful prose is fine. The question is whether beauty is doing structural work (producing perception, enabling meaning) or existing for its own sake (producing appreciation without changing understanding).

4. **Not a genre-fiction devaluation.** A thriller that works as a thriller doesn't need to pass this audit. This audit activates only when the manuscript claims literary-mode ambitions and evaluates whether those ambitions are fulfilled.

5. **Not a requirement for thematic clarity.** Productive ambiguity, deliberate withholding, and interpretive multiplicity are strengths. The audit penalizes ambiguity that's empty, not ambiguity that's rich.

6. **Not a penalty for influence.** Register Cosplay (PA-2) flags *unmetabolized* influence — borrowed registers that don't generate material-specific effects. Influence that has been absorbed and transformed is craft development, not cosplay.

7. **Not an originality requirement.** Familiar literary modes can be fully load-bearing. The question is never "is this new?" but "is this doing work?"

8. **Not a replacement for the core passes.** Structure, character, pacing, and scene function must work before literary-mode evaluation is meaningful. A manuscript with a broken spine doesn't need a literary audit — it needs a structural one.
